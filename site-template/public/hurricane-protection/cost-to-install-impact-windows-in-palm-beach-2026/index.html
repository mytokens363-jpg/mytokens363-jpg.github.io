Cost To Install Impact Windows In Palm Beach 2026

Updated April 5, 2026 9 min read

How Much Does It Cost to Install Impact Windows in Palm Beach? (2026 Guide)

Quick Answer: Installing impact windows in Palm Beach costs between $15,000 and $45,000 for a standard single-family home, with the average homeowner paying around $28,500. The biggest factors driving your price are window size, frame material, and Palm Beach County’s strict wind load building requirements.

Palm Beach sits on the Atlantic coast, exposing homes to intense salt air and high wind speeds. Your windows must meet Florida Building Code wind pressure ratings that go beyond standard glass. This adds cost — but it also means your home is protected from the storm surge and flying debris that Florida hurricanes bring.

What Affects the Cost in Palm Beach

1. Window Size and Quantity

Impact windows are priced per unit rather than per square foot. A standard 3x5 window costs less than a 6x6 picture window. A typical Palm Beach home has between 20 and 40 window openings. At $700–$1,500 per window installed depending on material, a home with 20 windows runs roughly $14,000–$25,000 while 40 windows pushes $28,000–$45,000.

2. Frame Material Choice

This is the single biggest price lever you control. Vinyl frames are the most common option at $600–$900 per window installed. Aluminum frames — popular in coastal areas — jump to $900–$1,400. Custom wood or thermally broken aluminum is the premium choice at $1,500–$2,500 per window but lasts 40–60 years.

3. Labor Rates in Palm Beach

Window installation labor in Palm Beach County runs $60–$85 per hour, roughly 20% above the national average. Demand stays high year-round from storm damage repairs and insurance-mandated replacements. After a named storm, rates can spike another 15–25%.

4. Permits and Inspections

Palm Beach County requires a building permit for any window replacement involving structural changes or new openings. Permit fees run $300–$800 depending on project value. The process includes a plan review and final inspection. Your contractor should pull the permit — if they suggest skipping it, walk away.

5. Installation Complexity

A standard rectangular window is straightforward to install. Add arch tops, sliders, or bay windows and labor climbs. Complex installations can add $2,000–$6,000 to the project. Replacement windows that fit existing frames cost less than new construction openings that require framing.

6. Wind Load and FBC Requirements

Palm Beach is in a high wind zone. Every window product must hold a Florida Product Approval or Miami-Dade NOA. This limits material options and adds $1,000–$3,000 compared to non-coastal areas. Glass lamination requirements are stricter too — double or triple layers of glass than standard code.

Fixed glass panels offer maximum view and durability without moving parts. Rated to 160 mph and very low maintenance. No ventilation but best for picture windows.

Florida-Specific Considerations

Building Codes: Florida Building Code 8th Edition (2023) governs all window replacements in Palm Beach. Key requirements include specific wind pressure ratings (150+ mph), impact testing standards, and drainage capabilities. These go beyond what most states require.

Wind Zone: Palm Beach is in a High Velocity Hurricane Zone extension area. All windowing materials must hold a Miami-Dade NOA or Florida Product Approval. Products are tested with missile impact tests and cyclic pressure tests that simulate hurricane conditions. Your contractor should provide NOA numbers for every product they plan to use.

Permits: Apply through Palm Beach County’s ePlan online portal or at the Palm Beach Building Department. Turnaround is 10–15 business days for a standard residential permit. Budget $300–$800 for the fee. One or two inspections required depending on scope.

Insurance Impact: This is the big one. A new impact window system resets the clock with your insurance company. Many Florida insurers offer wind mitigation discounts of 20–35% on your premium for impact-rated windows. Upgrading can lower your annual premium by hundreds of dollars.

HOA: Many Palm Beach communities restrict window color, frame thickness, and style. Wood or aluminum is often required in deed-restricted communities. Get written HOA approval before signing a contractor agreement — changing materials mid-job is extremely expensive.

Season: Best time to install windows here is December through April — dry season, lower humidity, fewer storm damage backlogs. Avoid peak hurricane season (August–October). Rainy season (May–October) brings daily afternoon thunderstorms that delay projects.

My Safe Florida Home: If your home was built before 2008 and is homesteaded, you may qualify for a free wind inspection and matching grants up to $10,000 toward hurricane hardening improvements including window upgrades.

How to Save Money on Impact Window Installation in Palm Beach

  1. Get at least 4 written quotes. Contractor pricing varies by 20–35% for identical scope in Palm Beach. On a $28,000 job, that’s $6,000–$9,000 in potential savings. Make sure each quote itemizes materials, labor, disposal, permits, and warranty separately.

  2. Schedule in dry season. December through April is slower for window installers. You’ll have more leverage to negotiate and fewer weather delays. Some contractors offer 5–10% off-season discounts.

  3. Choose aluminum frames over wood if your HOA allows it. The savings between aluminum ($900–$1,400/window) and custom wood ($1,500–$2,500/window) can be $5,000–$10,000 on a typical home.

  4. Bundle hurricane upgrades with the roof. Adding storm shutters or door upgrades during a window replacement is 40–60% cheaper than standalone projects. The combined wind mitigation improvements can also cut your insurance premium by hundreds per year.

  5. Check for My Safe Florida Home eligibility. If your home is eligible, you may get matching funds up to $10,000 for the upgrades. Apply before signing a contractor agreement to maximize the rebate.

When to Replace Your Windows — Warning Signs

Your windows are 15–20 years old. Vinyl and aluminum degrade faster than in cooler climates due to intense UV, heat cycling, and salt air. A 20-year-old window in Palm Beach has taken more abuse than a 30-year-old window in the Midwest.

Your insurance company is pressuring you. A non-renewal notice or letter requesting a window inspection means the clock is ticking. Many Florida insurers are actively dropping homes with aging glazing.

Visible condensation between panes. Check your glass for fogging. This means the seal has failed and insulation is gone. Wet spots on sills mean water intrusion. Multiple failures usually mean the whole system has failed.

Drafts or noise infiltration. If you feel cold air or hear street noise, your weatherstripping is gone. In Palm Beach’s humidity, even small leaks lead to mold fast. Single pane windows are also a liability during hurricanes.

How to Hire a Window Contractor in Palm Beach

  1. Verify their Florida license at MyFloridaLicense.com. You need a Certified General Contractor (CGC) or a Specialty Contractor license for glazing.

  2. Confirm active insurance. Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify.

  3. Check for a Palm Beach County local business tax receipt. This confirms they’re registered locally, not a storm chaser from out of state.

  4. Get 3–4 written estimates with line-item breakdowns — material brand, glass type, frame material, number of units, removal, permits, labor, timeline, and warranty.

  5. Check reviews on Google and BBB. Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings.

  6. Never pay more than 10% upfront. Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, delivery, mid-project, final inspection passed.

  7. Get everything in writing. Contract must include: scope, material specs with NOA numbers, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, permit responsibility, and cleanup plan.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does installing impact windows cost in Palm Beach in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between $15,000 and $45,000. The average is around $28,500. Your actual cost depends on window count, material choice, complexity, and whether structural repairs are needed.

Do I need a permit to install impact windows in Palm Beach County?

Yes, always. Apply through Palm Beach County’s ePlan portal or at Building Services. Budget $300–$800. One or two inspections required. Never let a contractor skip the permit — it can void your insurance and create title issues when you sell.

How long does an impact window installation take in Palm Beach?

Vinyl windows take 2–4 days for an average home. Aluminum frames take 3–5 days. Rainy season can add 2–5 days of weather delays.

Does new impact windows increase home value in Palm Beach?

Impact windows recoup 70–80% of their cost at resale. But the bigger value in Palm Beach’s market is insurability — a home with impact windows is dramatically easier to sell because buyers can actually get insurance on it.

Can I install my own impact windows in Florida?

Florida’s homeowner exemption allows it if you own and occupy the home. But you still need permits, inspections, and must meet all FBC wind load requirements. Insurance companies may refuse to cover a homeowner-installed window. For most people, it’s not worth the risk.

Does homeowners insurance cover window replacement?

Storm damage (wind, hail, flying debris) is typically covered minus your deductible — often 2% of insured value for hurricane claims in Florida. Normal wear and aging is never covered.

Bottom Line

Installing impact windows in Palm Beach runs $15,000 to $45,000 for most homes, with aluminum frames at the sweet spot of cost and performance. The most important thing to know: Palm Beach’s high wind requirements add cost but your new windows meet some of the toughest wind standards in the country — and it keeps you insurable in a market where carriers are dropping homes with aging glazing every day.
