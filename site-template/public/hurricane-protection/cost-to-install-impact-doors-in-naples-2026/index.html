Cost To Install Impact Doors In Naples 2026

Updated April 5, 2026 9 min read

How Much Does It Cost to Install Impact Doors in Naples? (2026 Guide)

Quick Answer: Installing impact doors in Naples costs between $5,000 and $15,000 for a typical home project involving two to four doors. The average homeowner spends around $9,500 on this upgrade. Your final price depends heavily on door type, frame material, and Collier County’s strict wind-borne debris requirements.

Naples sits in a high-wind coastal zone where standard doors are not enough. You need products tested to resist hurricane-force winds and flying debris. This testing drives up material costs but protects your home and your insurance rates.

Budget covers standard aluminum or steel entry doors on a small footprint. These units meet basic Florida Product Approval but offer minimal energy efficiency or aesthetic customization.

Mid-range is the sweet spot for Naples homeowners. This includes vinyl or composite frames with dual-pane Low-E glass. It often includes one sliding patio door and two entry doors for a standard ranch or split floor plan.

High-end covers custom wood or fiberglass frames with decorative glass inserts. These units often include integrated screens, multi-point locking systems, and enhanced weatherstripping for maximum hurricane protection and energy savings.

These prices reflect Naples market rates as of early 2026. They include materials, labor, disposal of old doors, standard permits, and cleanup. They do not include structural framing repairs if rot or damage is found during removal.

What Affects the Cost in Naples

1. Door Size and Configuration

Standard sizes are cheaper than custom cuts. A typical 36-inch entry door costs $1,500–$2,500 installed. A sliding patio door that is 8 feet wide can range from $2,500–$4,000 installed. If your opening is non-standard, expect to pay an extra $500–$1,000 per unit for custom framing and glass ordering.

2. Frame Material Choice

Aluminum frames are the most affordable option for impact doors. They are durable but conduct heat. Vinyl frames offer better insulation and cost about 10–15% more. Wood and fiberglass frames are premium options that cost 20–30% more than aluminum but offer better curb appeal for Naples estates.

3. Glass Type and Glazing

Impact glass is not a single pane; it is laminated with a PVB interlayer. Basic dual-pane impact glass starts at $1,200 per door. Upgrading to Low-E coating or argon gas fill adds $200–$400 per unit. This coating reflects heat and improves energy efficiency in Naples’ intense summer sun.

4. Labor Rates in Collier County

Specialized labor for impact doors costs more than standard carpentry. Experienced installers in Southwest Florida charge $80–$120 per hour. A full door replacement takes 4–8 hours per unit. Total labor for a four-door project typically runs $1,200–$2,500 depending on complexity.

5. Permits and Inspections

Collier County requires a building permit for most door replacements. Permit fees generally range from $100 to $400 based on the valuation of the work. The process includes a plan review and a final inspection to ensure wind-load compliance. Never skip this step to save money.

6. Wind Zone Requirements

Naples is in a high-wind zone that requires specific testing standards. While not Miami-Dade HVHZ, Collier County requires Florida Product Approval (FPA). Products must pass cyclic pressure testing. This certification adds cost but ensures the doors won’t blow out during a Category 3 or 4 hurricane.

Aluminum is the workhorse of impact doors. It resists corrosion from salt air and handles wind loads well. It is the most common choice for budget-conscious Naples homeowners who prioritize function over style.

Vinyl offers superior thermal performance compared to aluminum. It does not conduct heat as much, keeping your AC bills lower. It is a popular choice for patio sliders where energy efficiency matters most.

Fiberglass combines the strength of aluminum with the look of wood. It is extremely durable and holds paint well. This material is ideal for Naples homeowners who want a premium look without the maintenance of real wood.

Wood provides a high-end, traditional aesthetic. It requires regular maintenance to prevent rot in the humid Florida climate. While expensive upfront, it can add significant resale value to luxury Naples properties.

Florida-Specific Considerations

Building Codes: The Florida Building Code 8th Edition (2023) applies to all Naples installations. Key requirements include specific anchoring schedules and wind load calculations. Your contractor must submit a wind load certification with the permit application.

Hurricane Zone: Collier County is not technically HVHZ, but it has strict Wind Zone 2 and 3 requirements. Coastal properties often require the highest rating (ASTM E1886/1996). This means the glass must stop a 9-pound 2x4 missile at high speeds.

Permits: Apply through the Collier County Building Department online portal. Processing times vary from 5 to 15 business days. Fees are based on the replacement value of the doors. Budget $150–$400 for permit costs.

Insurance Impact: Installing impact doors can lower your wind insurance premium. Many carriers offer discounts of 10–25% for impact-rated glazing. This can save $200–$500 annually on your policy. Always provide your insurance company with the product certification.

HOA: Naples has many deed-restricted communities with strict architectural guidelines. Some HOAs prohibit certain frame colors or styles. Get written approval before purchasing materials. Changing doors after installation is a costly mistake to fix.

Season: The best time to install doors is the dry season, November through April. Rain delays are common in summer. Avoid August and September when hurricanes are active and contractors are busiest with storm repairs.

My Safe Florida Home: Homeowners built before 2008 may qualify for grants. The program offers matching funds up to $10,000 for wind mitigation improvements. Impact doors often qualify as a major upgrade under this program.

How to Save Money on Impact Door Installation in Naples

  1. Get at least 4 written quotes. Pricing varies wildly among Naples contractors. On a $10,000 project, a 20% difference is $2,000. Ensure each quote lists glass type, frame material, and labor hours separately.

  2. Schedule in dry season. December through April is slower for storm contractors. You have more leverage to negotiate pricing. Some companies offer 5–10% discounts for scheduling during off-peak months.

  3. Keep existing frames if possible. If your current frames are structurally sound, a “retrofit” installation is cheaper than a full frame replacement. This can save $500–$1,000 per door on labor and disposal.

  4. Bundle with other upgrades. If you are replacing windows or the roof, bundle the door work. Contractors often give volume discounts on labor. Combined wind mitigation upgrades also maximize your insurance savings.

  5. Check for rebate eligibility. Look into utility rebates or the My Safe Florida Home program. These programs can offset up to $10,000 of the project cost. Apply early as funding is limited and runs out quickly.

When to Install Impact Doors — Warning Signs

Your current doors are over 10 years old. Standard glass degrades and loses impact resistance over time. Florida UV rays weaken the framing seals. If your doors are a decade old, they may not meet current code standards for hurricane protection.

You see cracks or chips in the glass. Even small fractures compromise the laminated layer. A small crack can spread rapidly during high wind events. If you see damage, replace the door immediately rather than trying to patch it.

Your insurance premiums are rising. Insurers are tightening underwriting standards for coastal homes. If your carrier is threatening non-renewal, impact doors are the fastest way to prove your home is wind-hardened. This secures your ability to get coverage.

You feel drafts or hear wind noise. Gaps around the frame indicate failed weatherstripping. In a hurricane, these gaps allow water intrusion that damages walls and floors. If air is leaking now, water will leak during a storm.

How to Hire an Impact Door Contractor in Naples

  1. Verify their Florida license at MyFloridaLicense.com. You need a Certified Building Contractor (CBC) or a specialized window/door contractor. General contractors (CGC) can also perform this work.

  2. Confirm active insurance. Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability. Minimum coverage should be $1 million. Call the insurer to verify the policy is active and valid for Naples.

  3. Check for Collier County local business tax receipt. This confirms they are a registered local business. Avoid storm chasers from out of state who might disappear after the job is done.

  4. Get 3–4 written estimates with line-item breakdowns. The quote should specify: glass thickness, interlayer type, frame material, and warranty terms. Do not accept vague “lump sum” bids.

  5. Check reviews on Google and BBB. Look for patterns in complaints about weather delays or cleanup. Naples has a large contractor community; reputation matters significantly in this market.

  6. Never pay more than 10% upfront. Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, delivery, installation, and final inspection passed.

  7. Get everything in writing. Contract must include: product specs with FPA numbers, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, and permit responsibility. Ensure the contractor handles the permit pulling.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does impact door installation cost in Naples in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between $5,000 and $15,000 for a full project. The average cost is around $9,500. Your actual price depends on the number of doors, frame material, and whether structural repairs are needed.

Do I need a permit to install impact doors in Collier County?

Yes, always. Apply through the Collier County Building Department. Budget $150–$400 for the fee. Two inspections required. Never let a contractor skip the permit — it can void your insurance and create title issues when you sell.

How long does the installation take in Naples?

A single door replacement takes 4–8 hours. A full project with four doors usually takes 2–3 days. Rainy season can add 2–5 days of weather delays. Plan for at least one week of work.

Does a new door increase home value in Naples?

Impact doors recoup 70–80% of their cost at resale. But the bigger value in Naples’ market is insurability. A home with impact glass is dramatically easier to sell because buyers can actually get insurance on it.

Can I replace my own doors in Florida?

Florida’s homeowner exemption allows it if you own and occupy the home. But you still need permits, inspections, and must meet all wind code requirements. Insurance companies may refuse to cover a homeowner-installed door. For most people, it is not worth the risk.

Does homeowners insurance cover impact door replacement?

Storm damage is typically covered minus your deductible — often 2% of insured value for hurricane claims. Normal wear and aging is never covered. However, wind mitigation discounts apply after installation.

Bottom Line

Installing impact doors in Naples runs $5,000 to $15,000 for most homes, with aluminum frames at the budget end and fiberglass at the premium end. The most important thing to know: Collier County’s wind requirements add cost but your new doors meet strict Florida Product Approval standards — and they keep you insurable in a market where carriers are dropping homes with aging windows every day.
