Cost To Install Hurricane Garage Door In Miami 2026

Updated April 6, 2026 10 min read

How Much Does It Cost to Install Hurricane Garage Door in Miami? (2026 Guide)

Quick Answer: Installing a hurricane-rated garage door in Miami costs between $4,500 and $12,000 for most residential projects, with the average homeowner paying around $7,500. The biggest cost drivers are the door size, insulation level, and Miami-Dade County’s strict impact testing requirements.

Miami sits inside the High Velocity Hurricane Zone, which means standard garage doors are not enough. Your new door must pass specific missile impact tests to be legal. This requirement adds upfront cost but protects your home from wind damage and flying debris during a storm.

Budget gets you a single-car steel door that meets Miami-Dade NOA impact standards. It usually includes a standard torsion spring system and basic weather sealing. It is functional and code-compliant but lacks high-end insulation or custom styling.

Mid-range is the standard choice for most Miami homeowners — a double-car door with R-12 or higher insulation. This includes impact-rated glass inserts and a heavy-duty track system. It handles wind pressure better and keeps the garage cooler.

High-end covers custom wood finishes, carriage house styling, and integrated smart home openers. These doors often feature decorative hardware and upgraded seals. You pay extra for aesthetics and convenience features like Wi-Fi connectivity.

These prices reflect Miami market rates as of early 2026. They include materials, labor, removal of the old door, and disposal. They do not include structural framing repairs if rot is found behind the old door.

What Affects the Cost in Miami

1. Door Size and Openings

Garage doors are priced by the opening size, not just the door itself. A standard single-car opening is 8 to 9 feet wide. A double-car opening is 14 to 16 feet wide. Doubling the width nearly doubles the material cost. Custom wide openings over 16 feet require reinforced headers and cost significantly more.

2. Material and Insulation

Steel is the most common material at $3,000–$6,000 for a standard unit. Aluminum is lighter but less impact resistant unless reinforced. Wood offers a premium look but costs $6,000–$10,000 and requires more maintenance. Insulated doors cost about 20–30% more than uninsulated but save on energy bills.

3. Labor Rates in Miami-Dade

Garage door installation labor in Miami runs $75–$125 per hour, slightly higher than the national average. Skilled labor is in high demand due to frequent storm repairs. Complex installs involving custom headers or electrical work for openers will push labor costs higher.

4. Permits and Inspections

Miami-Dade County requires a building permit for any garage door replacement. Permit fees typically run $200–$500 depending on the valuation of the work. You must submit product data showing Miami-Dade NOA approval. The process takes 3–7 business days for approval.

5. Opener and Automation

A standard garage door opener adds $300–$600 to the project. Smart openers with Wi-Fi and cameras cost $400–$800. If you are replacing both the door and the opener, contractors often bundle the labor at a discount. Battery backup systems add another $200–$400.

6. HVHZ Code Requirements

Miami-Dade is in the High Velocity Hurricane Zone. Every door must carry a Miami-Dade Notice of Acceptance (NOA). This proves the door passed cyclic pressure and missile impact tests. Non-HVHZ doors are illegal to install in Miami. Compliance adds $1,000–$2,000 compared to standard doors.

Single steel is the entry point for hurricane protection. It meets code but offers less insulation. Best for detached garages or homes where energy efficiency is less critical.

Double steel is the workhorse for Miami families. It covers two vehicles and provides better structural integrity against wind uplift. The wider span requires heavier springs and tracks.

Insulated steel adds foam core between steel skins. This raises the R-value to 12 or higher. It reduces noise and heat transfer. It is highly recommended for attached garages in Miami’s heat.

Wood provides a high-end aesthetic that steel cannot match. It requires regular staining and sealing to prevent rot in Miami’s humidity. Impact-rated wood doors are expensive to manufacture.

Aluminum is corrosion resistant for coastal areas. It is lighter than steel but can dent easier. It is a good choice for homes right on the water where salt air is a major factor.

Custom doors feature unique designs, glass inserts, and decorative hardware. They are built to order and require longer lead times. Prices vary wildly based on design complexity.

Florida-Specific Considerations

Building Codes: Florida Building Code 8th Edition (2023) mandates impact-resistant openings in HVHZ areas. Every garage door must have a Miami-Dade NOA number stamped on the frame. Installers must verify this number before starting work.

Hurricane Zone: Miami-Dade County is the strictest wind zone in Florida. Testing involves a large missile (2x4 lumber) fired at the door at specific speeds. Cyclic pressure testing simulates 3,000 wind cycles. This ensures the door won’t blow out during a Category 3 or 4 hurricane.

Permits: Apply through Miami-Dade County’s ePlan online portal. You must upload the product NOA and engineering drawings. The permit fee is roughly $250 for a standard replacement. Final inspection is required to close the permit.

Insurance Impact: Installing an impact-rated garage door can lower your wind insurance premium. Carriers often offer a 15–25% discount on the wind portion of your policy. This savings helps offset the installation cost over time. Always notify your carrier after installation.

HOA: Many Miami neighborhoods have strict aesthetic guidelines. You may need HOA approval for color, style, and hardware. Some communities allow only wood or specific steel colors. Get written approval before ordering to avoid delays.

Season: Best time to install is November through April. Summer brings heavy afternoon thunderstorms that delay outdoor work. Hurricane season (June–November) keeps contractors busy with storm damage repairs. Schedule early to avoid wait times.

My Safe Florida Home: Homeowners may qualify for a grant up to $10,000 for wind mitigation improvements. Impact-rated garage doors often count toward these grants. You must complete a free inspection first to determine eligibility.

How to Save Money on Garage Door Installation in Miami

  1. Get at least 4 written quotes. Contractor pricing varies by 20–30% for identical doors in Miami. On a $7,000 job, that is $1,500–$2,000 in potential savings. Ensure each quote includes the NOA number for the specific door model.

  2. Bundle with roof or window work. If you are upgrading your roof or windows, ask for a discount on the garage door installation. Contractors often reduce travel and mobilization fees when doing multiple projects.

  3. Choose standard steel over custom wood. The price difference between steel ($6,000) and wood ($10,000) is massive. Steel looks modern and clean. It is easier to match to new construction styles in Miami.

  4. Apply for My Safe Florida Home grants. Check eligibility before signing a contract. The grant can cover up to $10,000 of the cost. You must complete the inspection first and wait for funding approval.

  5. Avoid peak hurricane season. Schedule installation in the dry season (December–April). Contractors are less busy and may offer better pricing. You also avoid weather delays that can push out completion dates.

When to Replace Your Garage Door — Warning Signs

Your door is over 15 years old. Garage doors in South Florida degrade faster due to UV exposure and salt air. A 15-year-old door may not meet current impact codes. Insurance companies may deny claims if the door fails during a storm.

Visible dents or rust. Salt air corrodes steel quickly. Rust weakens the structural integrity of the panels. Large dents compromise the wind resistance rating. Small scratches can be touched up, but deep rust means replacement.

The door opens unevenly. If one side hangs lower than the other, the tracks are bent or springs are failing. This indicates structural stress. A crooked door is more likely to blow out in high winds.

High energy bills in summer. If your garage is attached to the house, a non-insulated door lets heat inside. This forces your AC to work harder. An insulated door pays for itself in energy savings within a few years.

Noise and vibration. Old doors make grinding noises when operating. This means rollers or hinges are worn out. Safety is a concern if a panel falls off the track during a storm.

How to Hire a Garage Door Contractor in Miami

  1. Verify their Florida license. Check at MyFloridaLicense.com. You need a Certified Garage Door Contractor (CGC) or General Contractor (CGC) license.

  2. Confirm active insurance. Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability ($1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer to verify the policy is active.

  3. Check for Miami-Dade local business tax receipt. This confirms they are registered locally, not a transient contractor. It protects you if something goes wrong.

  4. Get 3–4 written estimates with line-item breakdowns — door model, NOA number, opener, labor, permit fees, and warranty terms.

  5. Check reviews on Google and BBB. Look for comments about hurricane experience. Did they handle the permits correctly? Did they meet the deadline?

  6. Never pay more than 10% upfront. Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, delivery, install complete, final inspection.

  7. Get everything in writing. Contract must include: door specs with NOA numbers, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, permit responsibility, and cleanup plan.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a hurricane garage door cost in Miami in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between $4,500 and $12,000. The average is around $7,500. Your actual cost depends on door size, insulation, opener, and whether structural repairs are needed.

Do I need a permit to replace my garage door in Miami-Dade?

Yes, always. Apply through the Miami-Dade ePlan portal. Budget $200–$500. Final inspection is required. Never let a contractor skip the permit — it creates liability issues for insurance.

How long does a garage door installation take in Miami?

A standard replacement takes 1–2 days. Custom orders or complex installs take 3–5 days. Permit approval adds 3–7 days to the start date.

Does a new garage door increase home value in Miami?

A new garage door recoups 70–80% of its cost at resale. But the bigger value in Miami’s market is insurability — a home with impact-rated doors is easier to insure and sell.

Can I install a garage door myself in Florida?

Florida’s homeowner exemption allows it if you own and occupy the home. But you still need permits, inspections, and must meet all HVHZ code requirements. Insurance companies may refuse coverage for DIY installs.

Does homeowners insurance cover garage door replacement?

Storm damage (wind, debris) is typically covered minus your deductible. Normal wear and aging is never covered. Impact-rated doors may lower your premium over time.

Bottom Line

Installing a hurricane garage door in Miami runs $4,500 to $12,000 for most homes, with double-car insulated doors at the sweet spot of cost and protection. The most important thing to know: Miami-Dade’s HVHZ requirements add cost but your new door meets some of the toughest wind standards in the country — and it keeps you insurable in a market where carriers are dropping homes with aging garage doors every day.
