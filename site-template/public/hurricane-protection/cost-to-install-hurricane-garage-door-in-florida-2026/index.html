Cost To Install Hurricane Garage Door In Florida 2026

Updated April 6, 2026 11 min read

How Much Does It Cost to Install a Hurricane Garage Door in Florida? (2026 Guide)

Quick Answer: Installing a hurricane-rated garage door in Florida costs between $3,500 and $12,000 for most homeowners. The average single-car installation runs $4,500, while a double-car unit typically lands around $7,500. Prices depend heavily on door size, insulation, and whether your location requires strict High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ) compliance.

Florida homes are uniquely vulnerable to wind pressure. Your garage door is often the weakest point in your home’s envelope. If it fails during a storm, the pressure buildup inside can blow the roof off. Installing an impact-rated door protects your structure and your wallet by preventing catastrophic damage.

Budget options usually involve single-car steel doors with a basic impact rating. They meet minimum Florida Building Code requirements but lack heavy insulation or decorative features. Ideal for utility buildings or strict budget projects.

Mid-range covers most standard residential double-car garage doors. These include steel construction with polyurethane insulation, rated for wind speeds up to 180 mph. This is the sweet spot for coastal Florida homeowners.

These prices reflect Florida market rates as of early 2026. They include the door unit, professional installation, electrical hookup for the opener, and standard disposal of the old door. They do not include structural framing repairs if the existing opening is damaged.

What Affects the Cost in Florida

1. Door Size and Configuration

Garage doors are priced by the unit, not just square footage, but size drives the material cost. A standard single-car door is roughly 8 to 10 feet wide. A double-car door is 16 to 20 feet wide. Doubling the width does not double the price, but it does increase the steel gauge and track requirements. Expect to pay $1,500 more for a double-car unit compared to a single-car unit.

2. Wind Load Rating

This is the most critical cost factor in Florida. Standard residential doors handle 120 mph winds. Florida coastal zones require ratings of 150 to 180 mph. The hardware, bracing, and anchoring systems for high-wind doors are significantly more expensive. In Miami-Dade and Broward, you must have a Miami-Dade NOA, which adds $500–$1,500 to the unit cost compared to standard impact doors.

3. Insulation and R-Value

Insulation matters for energy bills and noise reduction. A standard uninsulated door costs less but offers no thermal protection. Polyurethane foam insulation with an R-19 to R-32 rating is common in Florida. This adds $400–$800 to the total cost but lowers utility bills and reduces heat transfer into the garage.

4. Material Choice

Steel is the industry standard for durability and cost. It is available in various gauges, with 24-gauge being stronger than 28-gauge. Aluminum is lighter but less common for impact doors. Wood or wood-composite doors look better but cost significantly more. Steel runs $2,500–$5,000. Wood or composite starts at $6,000 and goes up from there.

5. Automation and Opener

Most homeowners bundle a new garage door with a new motor. A standard belt-drive opener costs $300–$600. Smart openers with Wi-Fi and camera integration cost $400–$800. If you are replacing an old motor, factor in the labor to disconnect and reset the safety sensors and photo-eye alignment.

6. Labor and Complexity

Installation labor in Florida ranges from $800 to $2,000 depending on the contractor. Complex installations require reinforcing the header or adjusting the track alignment. If the existing frame is rotted or damaged, framing repairs add $500–$1,500. Always verify if the quote includes disposal of the old door.

Steel (Insulated) is the most popular choice for Florida homeowners. It resists corrosion, handles wind loads well, and provides energy efficiency. Expect to pay $3,500–$6,000 for a double-car door with impact resistance.

Steel (Uninsulated) is the budget option. It is lighter and easier to install but offers no protection against heat or sound. In South Florida, where heat is intense, this option often leads to higher cooling costs in the garage.

Aluminum doors are lightweight and rust-resistant. They are often used in coastal areas with salt air exposure. They are durable but dent more easily than steel. Expect to pay a premium over standard steel.

Wood and Composite doors offer a custom look. They require regular maintenance to prevent warping in Florida humidity. Composite materials are better than solid wood because they resist rot and moisture better.

Fiberglass is a niche option that mimics wood but resists rot and insects. It is less common for impact doors because it can be brittle in extreme cold, though Florida rarely sees freezing temperatures.

Florida-Specific Considerations

Building Codes: The Florida Building Code 8th Edition (2023) mandates specific wind load requirements for garage doors. Coastal counties often require doors to withstand 150 mph winds or higher. The code requires continuous metal framing around the opening to support the door anchors. Failure to meet these codes can result in permit denial or fines.

Hurricane Zone: Miami-Dade and Broward counties are in the High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ). Any door installed here must have a Miami-Dade Notice of Acceptance (NOA). This document proves the door passed missile impact testing and cyclic pressure testing. Other counties follow state code but may not require the specific NOA label. Always check your county’s specific amendment.

Permits: Most Florida counties require a building permit for garage door replacement if it involves structural changes or new openings. Permit fees range from $100 to $400 depending on the county. In some jurisdictions, a permit is required for any new installation, even if replacing like-for-like. Your contractor should handle this.

Insurance Impact: Replacing a garage door with an impact-rated model can lower your homeowners insurance premium. Insurers view this as reducing the risk of wind pressure entering the home. Discounts typically range from 5% to 20% on the wind portion of your policy. If your current door is non-impact rated, your carrier may have already flagged your home for higher risk.

My Safe Florida Home: This program offers grants to Florida homeowners for hurricane hardening. While primarily focused on roofs, the program may cover garage door upgrades if they are part of a broader mitigation plan. Eligible homeowners can receive matching grants up to $10,000. Check eligibility at the My Safe Florida Home website before starting the project.

Season: The best time to install a garage door is during the dry season, November through April. Rain delays are less frequent, and contractors have more availability. Avoid scheduling during peak hurricane season (August–October). If a storm hits during installation, the project timeline extends, and materials may be damaged.

How to Save Money on Garage Door Installation in Florida

  1. Get at least 4 written quotes. Pricing varies by 20–35% for identical scopes across Florida. On a $7,000 project, that is $1,400–$2,500 in potential savings. Ensure each quote includes labor, materials, permits, and disposal.

  2. Check for My Safe Florida Home rebates. If you qualify for the grant program, you can offset the cost significantly. The program often covers wind mitigation improvements. Apply for the inspection first to see what upgrades are eligible for funding.

  3. Bundle with roof or window upgrades. If you are already replacing your roof or windows, ask the contractor if they offer a discount for multiple projects. Doing all hurricane hardening at once reduces mobilization costs and labor overhead.

  4. Choose standard steel over custom. A standard steel door with impact rating costs half as much as a custom wood door. Unless aesthetics are the top priority, steel provides the same safety benefits at a fraction of the price.

  5. Schedule in the off-season. November and March are typically slower months for contractors. You may negotiate better pricing than during the post-storm rush in late summer. Some contractors offer 5–10% discounts for scheduling in these months.

When to Install Your Garage Door — Warning Signs

Your door is over 15 years old. Most garage doors last 15 to 20 years. An older door is likely to fail during a hurricane. If you are in a coastal zone, do not wait for the door to break. Proactive replacement is cheaper than emergency repairs.

You see visible damage or warping. Look for gaps in the seals, bent tracks, or rust on the hinges. In Florida’s humidity, rust can compromise the structural integrity of the door frame. Even small gaps let wind pressure build up inside your home.

Your insurance company issued a warning. If your insurer sent a letter stating your garage door is non-compliant or high-risk, you need to act immediately. Some carriers will not renew policies on homes with non-impact doors in hurricane zones.

You are planning a roof replacement. If you are replacing your roof, this is the perfect time to replace the garage door. The roof and garage door work together to protect the structure. Installing both at once ensures they meet the same wind load standards.

You experience noise or vibration. A loose or unbalanced door vibrates heavily in the wind. This indicates worn springs or tracks. If the door rattles during a storm, it is likely to detach from its tracks and fail completely.

How to Hire a Garage Door Contractor in Florida

  1. Verify their Florida license at MyFloridaLicense.com. You need a Certified Residential Contractor (CRC) or General Contractor (CGC). Garage door specialists often work under a contractor license.

  2. Confirm active insurance. Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. If a worker falls off a ladder, you do not want to be liable for their medical bills.

  3. Check for a local business tax receipt. This confirms they are registered in your county, not a storm chaser. Storm chasers often vanish after the job is done, leaving you with no warranty.

  4. Get 3–4 written estimates with line-item breakdowns — door brand, model number, insulation R-value, opener type, labor, and timeline. Do not accept a verbal quote.

  5. Check reviews on Google and BBB. Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings. Pay attention to comments about cleanup, punctuality, and warranty support.

  6. Never pay more than 10% upfront. Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, door delivery, installation complete, final inspection passed.

  7. Get everything in writing. Contract must include: scope, door specs with NOA numbers, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, permit responsibility, and cleanup plan.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a hurricane garage door cost in Florida in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between $3,500 and $12,000. The average double-car installation is around $7,500. Your actual cost depends on door size, material, insulation, and whether your county requires strict HVHZ compliance.

Do I need a permit to install a new garage door in Florida?

Yes, usually. Most counties require a permit for new installations or replacements that involve structural changes. Permit fees range from $100 to $400. Your contractor should pull the permit — if they suggest skipping it, walk away.

How long does a garage door installation take in Florida?

A standard installation takes 4 to 8 hours for an average home. Custom doors or complex framing repairs can take 1 to 2 days. Rainy season can add delays if the job requires outdoor work during storms.

Does a new garage door increase home value in Florida?

A new impact-rated door improves curb appeal and insurability. It recoups roughly 70–80% of its cost at resale. The bigger value in Florida is safety — buyers prefer homes with modern hurricane protection features.

Can I replace my own garage door in Florida?

Florida law allows homeowners to do their own work if they own and occupy the home. However, you must still meet all building codes and get permits. Insurance companies may refuse to cover a homeowner-installed garage door if it fails during a storm.

Does homeowners insurance cover garage door replacement?

Storm damage (wind, hail, fallen trees) is typically covered minus your deductible — often 2% of insured value for hurricane claims in Florida. Normal wear and aging is never covered. If a tree crushes your door, your insurance pays. If the door is just old, you pay.

Bottom Line

Installing a hurricane garage door in Florida costs $3,500 to $12,000 for most homes, with double-car units at the sweet spot of $5,000 to $8,000. The most important thing to know: your garage door is a critical part of your home’s wind resistance system. A failure here can lead to roof damage far exceeding the cost of the door itself.
