Cost To Install Hurricane Shutters In Orlando 2026

Updated April 6, 2026 9 min read

How Much Does It Cost to Install Hurricane Shutters in Orlando? (2026 Guide)

Quick Answer: Installing hurricane shutters in Orlando costs between $5,000 and $25,000 for a typical single-family home. The average homeowner pays around $11,500 for a full home installation. Your final price depends heavily on shutter type, total window count, and Orange County permit requirements.

Orlando homeowners face serious wind risks every summer. While we are not in the High Velocity Hurricane Zone like Miami, Orange County still enforces strict wind load standards. Proper shutters protect your glass and keep your insurance premiums manageable.

These prices reflect Orlando market rates as of early 2026. They include materials, professional installation, Orange County permits, and disposal of old hardware. They do not include structural reinforcement if your window frames need repair.

What Affects the Cost in Orlando

1. Shutter Type Selection

The shutter mechanism is your primary cost driver. Storm panels are cheapest at $15–$25 per window. Accordion shutters range from $25–$45 per window. Motorized roll-down systems jump to $75–$120 per window. Manual operation is cheaper but requires physical effort during a storm.

2. Total Window Count

Orlando homes vary wildly in window count. A standard 2,000 sq ft home usually has 10 to 14 large openings. Each opening requires a separate shutter unit. Adding a garage door shutter or skylight adds $300–$1,500 per unit. More windows mean more linear footage and higher material costs.

3. Window Dimensions and Shape

Standard rectangular windows are easiest to measure and install. Arched, circular, or custom shaped windows cost more. You pay a premium for custom fabrication and specialized mounting hardware. Expect to pay 20–30% more for non-standard shapes compared to standard rectangles.

4. Labor Rates in Central Florida

Orlando labor rates are competitive but rising. Professional shutter installers charge $65–$95 per hour. This is lower than Miami rates but higher than rural North Florida. Complexity matters too. Installing on stucco or brick takes more time than vinyl siding.

5. Orange County Permits

Orange County requires a building permit for permanent shutter installations. Permit fees run $150–$500 depending on project valuation. The process includes plan review and a final inspection. Your contractor should handle this paperwork — never skip the permit.

6. Material Durability

Aluminum shutters are standard at $20–$30 per window. Stainless steel costs $40–$60 per window but resists corrosion better. Painted finishes add $5–$10 per window but are essential for curb appeal. Darker colors absorb heat and may fade faster in Florida sun.

Storm panels are metal sheets stored in a rack. They offer the best value for occasional storm protection. You must install them before the storm hits, which is difficult during high winds.

Accordion shutters slide along a track. They are popular in Orlando for their convenience. You pull them across the opening with a manual crank or motor. They stay attached year-round.

Bahama shutters fold up like an awning. They provide shade when open and protection when closed. They add architectural style to homes but cost more than basic panels.

Colonial shutters are decorative but functional. They fold shut to cover the window. They are less impact resistant than accordion or roll-down options. Best for cosmetic protection in lower wind zones.

Roll-down shutters are the premium choice. They retract into a headbox above the window. Motorized versions allow deployment from inside the house. They add security and energy efficiency year-round.

Polycarbonate panels are lightweight and transparent. They let light in while blocking debris. They are less durable than metal but offer a modern aesthetic. Good for skylights and small windows.

Florida-Specific Considerations

Building Codes: Florida Building Code 8th Edition (2023) governs all shutter installations in Orlando. Shutter systems must be tested to withstand specific wind pressures for Orange County. Look for Miami-Dade NOA or Florida Product Approval stickers on the hardware.

Wind Zones: Orlando is in Wind Zone 2 or 3, depending on your exact location. This requires design wind speeds of 130 to 150 mph. While not HVHZ, the testing standards are still rigorous. Ensure your contractor specifies the correct wind rating for your address.

Permits: Apply through the Orange County Building Division online portal. You need a site plan showing shutter locations. Turnaround is 3–7 business days for standard residential permits. Budget $150–$500 for the fee. Two inspections required: rough-in and final.

Insurance Impact: Installing shutters can save you 15–30% on the wind portion of your premium. Many insurers require documentation of the wind mitigation inspection. Keep your NOA numbers and installation receipts for your insurance agent. This is a guaranteed return on investment.

HOA: Orlando communities often have strict aesthetic rules for shutters. Some prohibit visible hardware or require specific colors. Get written HOA approval before purchasing materials. Changing shutters mid-project to meet HOA standards is extremely expensive.

Season: Hurricane season runs June through November. Install shutters by April to be ready for June. Contractor demand spikes in May and June. Schedule installations in the winter for faster turnaround and better pricing.

My Safe Florida Home: If your home was built before 2008 and is homesteaded, you may qualify for a matching grant up to $10,000 for hurricane hardening. This program covers inspections and eligible improvements including shutter installation.

How to Save Money on Hurricane Shutters in Orlando

  1. Get at least 4 written quotes. Contractor pricing varies by 20–35% for identical scope in Orlando. On a $12,000 job, that’s $2,500–$4,000 in potential savings. Make sure each quote itemizes materials, labor, permits, and disposal separately.

  2. Schedule in dry season. December through March is slower for shutter installers. You will have more leverage to negotiate and fewer weather delays. Some contractors offer 5–10% off-season discounts for winter bookings.

  3. Choose accordion over roll-down if budget is tight. The savings between accordion ($250–$450/window) and roll-down ($800–$1,200/window) can be $6,000–$8,000 on a typical home. Manual cranks are cheaper than motors.

  4. Bundle with other storm upgrades. Adding hurricane straps or upgrading your roof during the same contractor visit is 40–60% cheaper than standalone projects. The combined wind mitigation improvements can cut your insurance premium by hundreds per year.

  5. Check for My Safe Florida Home rebates. If you qualify, the grant covers a significant portion of the cost. The application process requires a pre-inspection by a certified home inspector. Use the funds for eligible shutter upgrades first.

When to Install Your Shutters — Warning Signs

You live in a flood zone. Orlando has many designated flood zones due to flat terrain and heavy rains. Insurance requirements for flood zones often overlap with wind requirements. Check your flood map status before buying.

Your insurance company is pressuring you. A non-renewal notice or letter requesting a wind mitigation inspection means the clock is ticking. Many Florida insurers are actively dropping homes without proper protection.

Your windows are older than 15 years. Single-pane or older vinyl windows are vulnerable to wind pressure. Even if you have shutters, the glass itself might fail under extreme loads. Consider upgrading windows and shutters together.

You lack storage space for panels. Storm panels require a dedicated rack system. If you do not have a garage or shed, storing panels is difficult. Accordion or roll-down systems eliminate storage needs entirely.

How to Hire a Shutter Contractor in Orlando

  1. Verify their Florida license at MyFloridaLicense.com. You need a Certified or Registered Contractor license. Check for any disciplinary actions on record.

  2. Confirm active insurance. Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify active status.

  3. Check for an Orange County local business tax receipt. This confirms they are registered locally, not a storm chaser from out of state.

  4. Get 3–4 written estimates with line-item breakdowns — material brand, track type, number of windows, permits, labor, timeline, and warranty.

  5. Check reviews on Google and BBB. Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings. Focus on punctuality and cleanup quality.

  6. Never pay more than 10% upfront. Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, track installation complete, final inspection passed.

  7. Get everything in writing. Contract must include: scope, material specs with NOA numbers, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, permit responsibility, and cleanup plan.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does hurricane shutter installation cost in Orlando in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between $5,000 and $25,000. The average is around $11,500. Your actual cost depends on shutter type, window count, complexity, and whether structural repairs are needed.

Do I need a permit to install shutters in Orange County?

Yes, for permanent installations. Apply through Orange County’s Building Division. Budget $150–$500. Two inspections required. Never let a contractor skip the permit — it can void your insurance and create title issues when you sell.

How long does shutter installation take in Orlando?

Accordion shutters take 1–3 days for an average home. Roll-down systems take 3–5 days. Rainy season can add 1–3 days of weather delays.

Do shutters increase home value in Orlando?

Shutters recoup 50–60% of their cost at resale. But the bigger value in Orlando’s market is insurability — a home with shutters is dramatically easier to sell because buyers know they are protected from wind damage.

Can I install shutters myself in Florida?

Florida’s homeowner exemption allows it if you own and occupy the home. But you still need permits, inspections, and must meet all FBC code requirements. Insurance companies may refuse to cover a homeowner-installed shutter system. For most people, it is not worth the risk.

Does homeowners insurance cover storm damage to shutters?

Wind damage to shutters is typically covered minus your deductible — often 2% of insured value for hurricane claims in Florida. Normal wear and aging is never covered. Document any damage with photos immediately.

Bottom Line

Installing hurricane shutters in Orlando runs $5,000 to $25,000 for most homes, with accordion shutters at the sweet spot of cost and performance. The most important thing to know: Orlando’s wind zone requirements are strict even if we are not in HVHZ. Proper shutters keep you insurable in a market where carriers are dropping homes without protection every day.
