Cost To Replace Roof In Florida 2026

Updated April 2, 2026 4 min read
Advertisement

A new roof is one of the most important home improvements you can make in Florida. With the state’s unique climate challenges—including intense sunlight, humidity, hurricane winds, and salt air—choosing the right roofing material and contractor is critical.

This guide covers everything you need to know about roof replacement costs in Florida for 2026, broken down by material type, location, and project specifics.

Average Cost to Replace a Roof in Florida

The average cost to replace a roof in Florida ranges from $8,000 to $30,000+ for a typical 1,500-2,000 square foot home.

Florida Roofing Materials Breakdown

Asphalt Shingles

Asphalt shingles remain the most popular choice due to their affordability and ease of installation.

Pros:

  • Lowest upfront cost
  • Easy to repair
  • Wide variety of colors and styles
  • Good for steep-slope roofs

Cons:

  • Shorter lifespan in Florida’s heat
  • Vulnerable to high winds
  • Can fade and curl over time

Average Cost: $4,000-6,000 per square installed

Metal Roofing

Metal roofing has become increasingly popular in Florida due to its durability and wind resistance.

Pros:

  • Excellent hurricane resistance (up to 140+ mph winds)
  • Energy efficient (reflects heat)
  • Lasts 40-70+ years
  • Low maintenance

Cons:

  • Higher initial cost
  • Can be noisy during rain (unless properly insulated)
  • Requires specialized installation

Average Cost: $8,000-15,000 per square installed

Tile Roofing

Clay and concrete tiles are iconic in Florida architecture and offer exceptional longevity.

Pros:

  • Extremely durable (50-100+ year lifespan)
  • Fire resistant
  • Excellent for high-wind areas
  • Adds property value

Cons:

  • Heaviest option (may need structural reinforcement)
  • Highest upfront cost
  • Fragile during installation
  • Expensive to repair

Average Cost: $10,000-25,000 per square installed

Flat Roof Options

For commercial buildings or homes with flat sections, TPO and PVC membranes are popular.

Pros:

  • Good for flat or low-slope surfaces
  • Seamless construction (no seams to leak)
  • Energy efficient (white reflectivity)
  • Good for HVAC equipment mounting

Cons:

  • Requires proper drainage
  • Vulnerable to punctures
  • Shorter lifespan than pitched roofs

Average Cost: $5,000-8,000 per square installed

Florida Location-Based Cost Variations

Roofing costs vary significantly across Florida due to local market conditions, material availability, and hurricane risk standards.

South Florida (Miami, Fort Lauderdale, Palm Beach)

South Florida has the highest roofing costs in the state due to:

  • High hurricane wind codes (must meet Miami-Dade County products)
  • Import costs (most materials shipped from elsewhere)
  • High demand ( frequent roof replacements after storms)
  • Stricter building codes (more inspections, higher labor rates)

Typical Range: $10,000-30,000+ for replacement

Central Florida (Orlando, Tampa, Jacksonville)

Central Florida has moderate pricing with good material availability.

Typical Range: $8,000-25,000 for replacement

North Florida (Gainesville, Tallahassee)

North Florida has lower costs closer to national averages.

Typical Range: $7,000-20,000 for replacement

Additional Cost Factors

Roof Complexity

FactorCost Increase
Multiple layers (tear-off required)+15-25%
Complex design (valleys, dormers)+20-40%
Steep slope (over 6:12)+25-50%
Historic preservation requirements+30-60%

Removal and Disposal

  • Tear-off and disposal: $1,500-4,000 (depending on layers)
  • Reroofing over existing (if allowed): Saves 20-30%

Permits and Inspections

  • Building permit: $500-1,500 (varies by county)
  • Wind mitigation inspection: $100-250 (may qualify for insurance discount)

Florida-Specific Considerations

Hurricane Wind Ratings

All roofing materials in Florida must meet specific wind speed ratings:

MaterialMinimum Wind Rating
Asphalt Shingles110 mph (most areas), 140+ mph (coastal)
Metal Roofing140-180+ mph
Tile Roofing140+ mph (properly installed)

Insurance Discounts

Many Florida insurance companies offer discounts for:

  • Impact-resistant roofing
  • Wind mitigation features
  • Newer roofs (0-10 years)

Potential Savings: $200-500 per year on premiums

Getting Multiple Quotes

We recommend getting at least 3 quotes from licensed, insured contractors.

Questions to ask:

  1. Are you licensed and insured in Florida?
  2. Do you have experience with my roof type and local codes?
  3. What’s your warranty on labor and materials?
  4. Can you provide references from recent projects?
  5. How do you handle storm damage claims?

Conclusion

Replacing your roof in Florida is a significant investment, but the right choice will protect your home for decades. Consider your budget, how long you plan to stay in the home, and the specific climate challenges in your area.

Quick Tips:

  • Get multiple quotes (at least 3)
  • Check contractor licenses at MYFLORIDA.com
  • Ask about warranty coverage
  • Consider long-term maintenance costs
  • Ask about insurance discounts for new roofs
