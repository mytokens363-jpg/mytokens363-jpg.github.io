# How Much Does It Cost to Resurface a Pool in Fort Lauderdale? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** A standard pool resurfacing project in Fort Lauderdale costs between **$4,500 and $15,000** for most homeowners, with the average paying around **$8,500**. The biggest factors driving your price are pool size, finish material, and Broward County’s strict safety and drainage codes.

Fort Lauderdale pools sit in a unique environment where salt air, high humidity, and fluctuating water tables accelerate wear. That means you often need resurfacing sooner than in other states. Choosing the right finish protects your investment against these specific coastal conditions.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$4,500 – $6,500** | White plaster, 10x20 pool |
| Mid-Range | **$6,500 – $9,500** | Quartz aggregate, standard size |
| High-End | **$9,500 – $15,000+** | Pebble or tile, large/complex |

**Budget** gets you standard white plaster on a smaller, rectangular pool. It is the most affordable option but requires more maintenance and fades faster under Florida sun.

**Mid-range** is the most popular choice — quartz aggregate blends sand and colored chips for better durability and texture. It handles Fort Lauderdale’s water chemistry better than plain plaster.

**High-end** covers exposed aggregate pebble finishes or full tile walls. These materials last 20+ years and resist staining, but labor costs are significantly higher due to installation complexity.

These prices reflect Fort Lauderdale market rates as of early 2026 and include water drainage, surface prep, material application, and initial fill. They do not include structural repairs to the shell if cracks are found during draining.

## What Affects the Cost in Fort Lauderdale

### 1. Pool Size

Pool resurfacing is priced per square foot of surface area. A typical Fort Lauderdale inground pool measures 15x30 feet. At **$4.00–$12.00 per sq ft** installed, a 500 sq ft pool runs roughly **$4,500–$6,000**. Larger pools or those with multiple levels cost significantly more.

### 2. Material Choice

This is the single biggest price lever you control. Standard white plaster is the cheapest option at **$4.00–$6.00 per sq ft**. Quartz aggregate jumps to **$6.00–$8.50 per sq ft** for better durability. Pebble finishes are the premium choice at **$9.00–$12.00 per sq ft** but last decades longer.

### 3. Labor Rates in Fort Lauderdale

Pool contractor labor in Broward County runs **$60–$90 per hour**, roughly 10% above the national average. Demand peaks in spring before summer swimming season. Scheduling during the off-season can sometimes save **5–10%** on labor costs.

### 4. Permits and Inspections

Broward County requires a permit if structural repairs are made to the shell or if plumbing is changed. Cosmetic resurfacing often does not need a permit, but you must verify with the Building Department. Budget **$100–$300** if a permit is required for equipment replacement.

### 5. Site Accessibility

A pool located deep in a backyard with narrow side gates costs more to service. Equipment must be brought in by hand or small crane. Tight access can add **$500–$1,500** to the project for labor and logistics.

### 6. Drainage and Soil Conditions

Fort Lauderdale sits on a high water table. Draining a pool risks structural floating if not done correctly. Contractors must manage groundwater during the process. This specialized drainage work adds **$300–$800** to the base cost.

## Cost by Material Type

| Material | Cost per Sq Ft | Lifespan |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| White Plaster | **$4.00 – $6.00** | 7–10 years |
| Colored Plaster | **$5.00 – $7.00** | 8–12 years |
| Quartz Aggregate | **$6.00 – $8.50** | 10–15 years |
| Pebble Finish | **$9.00 – $12.00** | 15–25 years |
| Full Tile | **$15.00 – $25.00** | 25+ years |
| Coping Repair | **$20 – $40** | N/A |

**White plaster** is the budget option. It is smooth and traditional but stains easily from minerals in Fort Lauderdale water. It usually needs resurfacing within a decade.

**Colored plaster** adds pigment to the mix for a richer look. It hides stains slightly better than white plaster but still degrades faster than aggregates. Expect to pay **$5.00–$7.00 per sq ft**.

**Quartz aggregate** mixes quartz crystals into the plaster for hardness. It resists etching from chemicals and handles the salt air better than plain plaster. This is the standard upgrade for most homeowners.

**Pebble finish** uses small river stones for a textured, durable surface. It is naturally slip-resistant and handles high-traffic families well. The upfront cost is higher, but the lifespan is double that of plaster.

**Full tile** covers the entire interior with ceramic or glass. It is the most expensive option and offers a resort-like aesthetic. Best for luxury estates in areas like Las Olas or Victoria Park.

**Coping repair** involves replacing the stone or concrete around the pool edge. If your coping is loose, it must be fixed before resurfacing. This is often charged per linear foot or as a lump sum.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Florida Building Code governs all pool work in Fort Lauderdale. Safety barriers and fencing requirements are strictly enforced. Even during resurfacing, the pool perimeter must remain compliant with barrier height and gate latch standards.

**Coastal Climate:** Salt air accelerates corrosion on metal fixtures and pool equipment. Your contractor should use corrosion-resistant ladders and lights. Stainless steel 316 grade is recommended over standard 304 in coastal zones.

**Permits:** Broward County requires permits for structural changes or new equipment. Apply through the Broward County ePlan portal or the Fort Lauderdale Building Department. Turnaround is 5–10 business days for standard reviews.

**Insurance Impact:** Pools are considered "attractive nuisances" by insurers. A new resurfacing job proves the pool is structurally sound. This can help maintain liability coverage and prevent premium hikes due to safety concerns.

**HOA:** Many Fort Lauderdale communities restrict pool colors and equipment placement. Some require specific coping materials that match the home’s exterior. Get written HOA approval before starting the project to avoid fines.

**Season:** Best time to resurface is November through March — cooler temps allow proper curing. Avoid peak hurricane season (June–November). Rain delays can add 1–2 weeks to the timeline during summer months.

**Water Quality:** Fort Lauderdale water varies by neighborhood, with some areas having hard water that stains plaster quickly. A water test is recommended before choosing a finish. Acid-resistant finishes perform better in high-mineral water.

## How to Save Money on Pool Resurfacing in Fort Lauderdale

1.  **Get at least 4 written quotes.** Contractor pricing varies by **20–30%** for identical scope in Fort Lauderdale. On a **$8,000** job, that’s **$1,600–$2,400** in potential savings. Make sure each quote itemizes materials, labor, drainage, and disposal separately.

2.  **Schedule in dry season.** November through March is slower for pool contractors. You’ll have more leverage to negotiate and fewer weather delays. Some contractors offer **5–10%** off-season discounts for winter projects.

3.  **Choose quartz over pebble if budget is tight.** The savings between plaster (**$4.00–$6.00/sq ft**) and quartz (**$6.00–$8.50/sq ft**) can be **$1,000–$2,000** on a typical home. It offers a good middle ground of durability and cost.

4.  **Bundle equipment upgrades.** Replacing the pump or filter during resurfacing is often cheaper than standalone. Combined labor costs can reduce the total by **15–20%**. The new equipment also improves energy efficiency and lowers utility bills.

5.  **Check for water bill rebates.** Some local utilities offer rebates for high-efficiency pumps and filters. Upgrading during resurfacing can qualify you for **$100–$500** in rebates. Ask your contractor if they handle the paperwork.

## When to Resurface Your Pool — Warning Signs

**Your pool is 10+ years old.** Plaster begins to degrade after a decade of exposure to Florida sun and chemicals. A 10-year-old pool in Fort Lauderdale has taken more UV abuse than a 15-year-old pool in a cooler climate.

**The surface feels rough or abrasive.** Run your hand along the wall. If it feels like sandpaper, the plaster is breaking down and can cut swimmers’ feet. This is a safety hazard that requires immediate attention.

**Stains won’t brush off.** Dark spots or rust-colored stains that survive brushing indicate the plaster is porous. Water chemistry is penetrating the surface. This often means the finish has failed and needs replacement.

**Visible cracks or leaks.** Hairline cracks are normal, but structural cracks allow water to escape into the soil. Leaks increase your water bill and can damage the surrounding landscape.

## How to Hire a Pool Contractor in Fort Lauderdale

1.  **Verify their Florida license.** You need a Certified Pool Contractor (CPB) or a General Contractor (CGC) license with pool certification. Check at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com).

2.  **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum **$1 million**) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify active status.

3.  **Check for a Broward County local business tax receipt.** This confirms they are registered locally, not a storm chaser from out of state.

4.  **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — material brand, surface prep, number of days, drainage plan, and warranty.

5.  **Check reviews on Google and Yelp.** Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings. Specifically look for comments about cleanup and timeline adherence.

6.  **Never pay more than 20% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, drain complete, final inspection passed.

7.  **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, material specs, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, and cleanup plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a pool resurface cost in Fort Lauderdale in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$4,500 and $15,000**. The average is around **$8,500**. Your actual cost depends on pool size, material choice, complexity, and whether structural repairs are needed.

### Do I need a permit to resurface my pool in Broward County?

Usually no, unless structural repairs are made. Apply through Broward County's ePlan portal if plumbing changes. Budget **$100–$300** for the fee if required. Never let a contractor skip the permit if structural work is involved.

### How long does a pool resurface take in Fort Lauderdale?

Standard plaster takes **2–4 days** for an average home. Pebble or tile takes **4–7 days**. Rainy season can add 2–5 days of weather delays for curing.

### Does a new pool surface increase home value in Fort Lauderdale?

A new pool recoups **50–60%** of its cost at resale. But the bigger value in Fort Lauderdale's market is safety — a smooth, crack-free pool is easier to insure and sell.

### Can I resurface my own pool in Florida?

No, Florida requires licensed professionals for pool structural and surface work. You still need permits and inspections. Insurance companies may refuse to cover a DIY pool resurfacing job. For most people, it is not worth the risk.

### Does homeowners insurance cover pool resurfacing?

Storm damage (wind, fallen trees) is typically covered minus your deductible. Normal wear and aging is never covered. Check your policy for specific "maintenance" exclusions.
