# How Much Does It Cost to Resurface a Pool in Sarasota? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** Resurfacing a pool in Sarasota costs between **$5,000 and $20,000** for most standard in-ground pools, with the average homeowner paying around **$12,500**. The biggest factors driving your price are the pool size, finish material choice, and Sarasota County permit requirements.

Sarasota homeowners face unique challenges with pool maintenance due to the high water table and coastal salt air. These conditions accelerate wear on plaster finishes, often shortening the lifespan compared to inland Florida homes.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$5,000 – $8,000** | White plaster, under 300 sq ft |
| Mid-Range | **$8,000 – $13,000** | Quartz aggregate, 300–500 sq ft |
| High-End | **$13,000 – $20,000+** | Pebble finish, tile coping included |

**Budget** gets you standard white plaster on a smaller pool with basic drain replacement. It meets code but uses entry-level materials that stain easily in Sarasota's mineral-rich water.

**Mid-range** is what most Sarasota homeowners choose — quartz aggregate finishes that are smoother and more durable. Covers pools in the 300 to 500 sq ft range with new plumbing drains.

**High-end** covers pebble or stone finishes on larger or geometric pools. Includes upgraded coping, new tile at the waterline, and extensive equipment repairs.

These prices reflect Sarasota market rates as of early 2026 and include materials, labor, drain replacement, and standard permits. They do not include major structural repairs if damage is found during draining.

## What Affects the Cost in Sarasota

### 1. Pool Size

Pool resurfacing is priced per square foot of surface area. A typical Sarasota home has a pool between 300 and 600 sq ft of surface. At **$15–$25 per sq ft** installed depending on material, a 300 sq ft pool runs roughly **$5,000–$7,500** while a 600 sq ft pool pushes **$12,000–$18,000**.

### 2. Material Choice

This is the single biggest price lever you control. Standard white plaster is the most common option at **$15–$20 per sq ft** installed. Quartz aggregate — popular in Southwest Florida — jumps to **$20–$30**. Pebble finish is the premium choice at **$25–$40 per sq ft** but lasts 20–30 years.

### 3. Labor Rates in Sarasota

Pool contracting labor in Sarasota County runs **$65–$95 per hour**, roughly 10–15% above the national average. Demand stays high year-round from storm damage repairs and aging inventory issues. After a tropical storm, rates can spike another 10–20% due to backlog.

### 4. Permits and Inspections

Sarasota County requires a building permit for any pool resurfacing work. Permit fees run **$150–$500** depending on project value. The process includes a pre-drain inspection and final inspection. Your contractor should pull the permit — if they suggest skipping it, walk away.

### 5. Coping and Decking

Most resurfaces include replacing the coping stones around the pool edge. Concrete coping adds **$1,000–$2,500** to the project. If your deck needs sealing or repair, that adds another **$2,000–$5,000** depending on the material.

### 6. Water Table and Soil

Sarasota sits on limestone bedrock with a high water table. This makes draining difficult during rainy season. Contractors may need to install dewatering pumps or use water displacement techniques. This adds **$500–$1,500** to the project cost during summer months.

## Cost by Material Type

| Material | Cost per Sq Ft | Lifespan |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| White Plaster | **$15.00 – $20.00** | 7–10 years |
| Quartz Aggregate | **$20.00 – $30.00** | 10–15 years |
| Pebble Finish | **$25.00 – $40.00** | 20–30 years |
| Tile Waterline | **$30.00 – $50.00** | 25+ years |
| Fiberglass Patch | **$25.00 – $45.00** | 10–15 years |
| Gunite Repair | **$40.00 – $60.00** | 50+ years |

**White plaster** is the budget option. It is smooth but stains easily from minerals in Sarasota water. Best for rentals or budget-conscious homeowners.

**Quartz aggregate** hits the sweet spot — mixed with quartz crystals for extra hardness. Rated for 10–15 years and the best cost-to-value ratio. This is what most Sarasota homeowners pick.

**Pebble finish** is the classic Southwest Florida look. Rated to 20–30 years and lasts longer in salt air. Expect to pay **$25.00–$40.00 per sq ft**. It is rougher on feet than plaster.

**Tile waterline** is premium — 25+ year lifespan, 180 mph rating, and the aesthetic that fits high-end Sarasota neighborhoods. Expect to pay **$30.00–$50.00 per sq ft**.

**Fiberglass patch** offers maximum durability at 10–15 years with smooth surface. Best for homeowners who want a "last finish you'll ever buy" solution. Higher upfront cost pays off over decades.

**Gunite repair** systems are specific to structural cracks common in older Sarasota pools. Different product category entirely from standard resurfaces.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Florida Building Code Chapter 3 (Plumbing) governs all pool work in Sarasota. Key requirements include new main drains, proper bonding, and specific barrier heights. These go beyond what most states require for safety.

**Hurricane Zone:** Sarasota County is in Wind Zone 2, not the High Velocity Hurricane Zone. All pool equipment must be anchored properly to resist wind uplift. Equipment pads need concrete curbs to prevent floating during flood events.

**Permits:** Apply through Sarasota County Building Division or City of Sarasota Building Department. Turnaround is 10–15 business days for a standard residential permit. Budget **$150–$500** for the fee. One inspection required before backfill.

**Insurance Impact:** This is the big one. A new pool finish resets the clock on liability coverage. Many Florida insurers require updated pool inspections every 5 years. Upgrading to a safety cover during resurfacing can qualify you for wind mitigation discounts of **5–10%** on your premium.

**HOA:** Many Sarasota communities restrict pool colors, coping materials, and lighting. Tile is often required in deed-restricted communities. Get written HOA approval before signing a contractor agreement — changing materials mid-job is extremely expensive.

**Season:** Best time to resurface a pool here is October through April — dry season, lower humidity, fewer storm damage backlogs. Avoid peak hurricane season (August–October). Rainy season (May–October) brings daily afternoon thunderstorms that delay projects.

**My Safe Florida Home:** If your home was built before 2008 and is homesteaded, you may qualify for a free wind inspection and matching grants up to **$10,000** toward hurricane hardening improvements. Pool safety barriers may qualify for additional local grants.

## How to Save Money on Pool Resurfacing in Sarasota

1. **Get at least 4 written quotes.** Contractor pricing varies by 20–35% for identical scope in Sarasota. On a **$12,000** job, that's **$2,500–$4,000** in potential savings. Make sure each quote itemizes materials, labor, drain replacement, and permits separately.

2. **Schedule in dry season.** October through April is slower for pool contractors. You'll have more leverage to negotiate and fewer weather delays. Some contractors offer **5–10%** off-season discounts.

3. **Choose quartz aggregate over pebble if your HOA allows it.** The savings between plaster (**$15–$20/sq ft**) and pebble (**$25–$40/sq ft**) can be **$3,000–$8,000** on a typical home.

4. **Bundle safety upgrades with the resurface.** Adding a new safety barrier or upgraded fencing during a resurface is **40–60% cheaper** than standalone projects. The combined safety improvements can also cut your insurance premium by hundreds per year.

5. **Check for insurance claim eligibility.** If your existing pool has storm damage, your insurance may cover part or all of the repair. File the claim before signing a contractor agreement. Be cautious of contractors who offer to "handle the insurance" — work with your adjuster directly.

## When to Resurface Your Pool — Warning Signs

**Your pool finish is 10+ years old.** Plaster finishes in Southwest Florida degrade faster than in cooler climates due to intense UV, heat cycling, and salt air. A 10-year-old pool in Sarasota has taken more abuse than a 15-year-old pool in the Midwest.

**Your insurance company is pressuring you.** A non-renewal notice or letter requesting a pool inspection means the clock is ticking. Many Florida insurers are actively dropping homes with aging pools or unsafe barriers.

**Visible cracks or staining on the surface.** Check the waterline for rust streaks or gray spots. Piles of plaster dust in the filter mean the finish is disintegrating. Rough spots on the floor mean the plaster is failing.

**Interior water stains or equipment issues.** Brown ceiling spots, bubbling paint, or damp insulation point to pool leaks. In Sarasota's humidity, even small leaks lead to mold fast. Multiple leaks usually mean the whole system has failed.

## How to Hire a Pool Contractor in Sarasota

1. **Verify their Florida license** at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a Certified Pool Contractor (CPC), Registered Pool Contractor (RPC), or General Contractor (CGC) license.

2. **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify.

3. **Check for a Sarasota County local business tax receipt.** This confirms they're registered locally, not a storm chaser from out of state.

4. **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — material brand, plaster type, number of squares, drain replacement, permits, labor, timeline, and warranty.

5. **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings.

6. **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, drain complete, mid-project, final inspection passed.

7. **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, material specs with warranty numbers, dates, payment schedule, permit responsibility, and cleanup plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a pool resurface cost in Sarasota in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$5,000 and $20,000**. The average is around **$12,500**. Your actual cost depends on pool size, material choice, complexity, and whether structural repairs are needed.

### Do I need a permit to resurface my pool in Sarasota County?

Yes, always. Apply through Sarasota County's Building Division portal or at City Hall. Budget **$150–$500**. One inspection required. Never let a contractor skip the permit — it can void your insurance and create title issues when you sell.

### How long does a pool resurface take in Sarasota?

Plaster resurfaces take **3–5 days** for an average pool. Pebble finishes take **5–7 days**. Rainy season can add 2–5 days of weather delays.

### Does a new pool finish increase home value in Sarasota?

A new pool recoups **60–70%** of its cost at resale. But the bigger value in Sarasota's market is insurability — a home with a new pool is dramatically easier to sell because buyers can actually get insurance on it.

### Can I resurface my own pool in Florida?

Florida's homeowner exemption allows it if you own and occupy the home. But you still need permits, inspections, and must meet all safety code requirements. Insurance companies may refuse to cover a homeowner-installed pool finish. For most people, it's not worth the risk.

### Does homeowners insurance cover pool resurfacing?

Storm damage (wind, hail, fallen trees) is typically covered minus your deductible — often **2% of insured value** for hurricane claims in Florida. Normal wear and aging is never covered.
