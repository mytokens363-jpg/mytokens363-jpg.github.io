# How Much Does It Cost to Resurface a Pool in Florida? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** A full pool resurfacing in Florida costs between **$5,000 and $15,000** for most standard-sized pools, with the average homeowner paying around **$9,500**. The biggest factors driving your price are pool surface area, material choice, and the condition of the underlying structure.

Florida's intense sun and high water table take a heavy toll on pool finishes. What looks like a simple stain might actually be structural degradation requiring a full resurface rather than a patch.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$5,000 – $7,500** | White plaster, standard size |
| Mid-Range | **$7,500 – $11,000** | Quartz or pebble, standard size |
| High-End | **$11,000 – $15,000+** | Tile accents, large pool, repairs |

**Budget** covers standard white plaster resurfacing on a typical 15x30 foot gunite pool. Includes drain replacement and basic cleaning. It meets code but uses entry-level materials.

**Mid-range** is what most Florida homeowners choose — quartz aggregate or pebble finishes. These last longer and resist staining better in our hard water. Covers homes in the 200 to 400 sq ft pool area range.

**High-end** covers custom tile waterlines, upgraded coping, or extensive structural repairs needed before resurfacing. Includes premium aggregate blends and enhanced equipment integration.

These prices reflect Florida market rates as of early 2026 and include materials, labor, water disposal, and basic cleaning. They do not include major structural cracks or equipment upgrades unless specified.

## What Affects the Cost in Florida

### 1. Pool Size and Shape

Pool resurfacing is priced per square foot of surface area, not just water volume. A standard rectangular pool in Orlando is often 15x30 feet, totaling about 450 square feet of surface. At **$12–$25 per sq ft** installed depending on material, a basic pool runs roughly **$5,400–$11,250** while a large lagoon-style pool pushes **$15,000–$25,000**.

### 2. Material Choice

This is the single biggest price lever you control. White plaster is the cheapest option at **$12–$18 per sq ft** installed. Aggregate finishes — like quartz or pebble — jump to **$18–$28 per sq ft** but last twice as long. Custom tile waterlines add another **$15–$30 per linear foot**.

### 3. Labor Rates in Florida

Pool contracting labor in major markets like Miami or Tampa runs **$60–$90 per hour**, roughly 15–20% above the national average. Demand stays high from hurricane damage repairs and insurance-mandated safety upgrades. After a storm, rates can spike another 10–20% due to backlog.

### 4. Permits and Inspections

Most Florida counties require a building permit for pool resurfacing, especially if plumbing or structural work is involved. Permit fees run **$150–$500** depending on project value and county. The process includes a pre-start inspection and final inspection. Your contractor should pull the permit.

### 5. Structural Repairs

Old pools often hide cracks behind the finish. If you find structural cracks during the drain process, repairs add **$1,000–$5,000** to the project. This includes epoxy injections or rebar replacement. Flat rates rarely cover hidden damage.

### 6. Water Disposal

Florida has strict regulations on draining pool water. You cannot simply dump it into storm drains due to chlorine and debris content. Proper disposal via sewer or evaporation costs **$500–$1,500** depending on local utility rules and truck fees.

## Cost by Material Type

| Material | Cost per Sq Ft | Lifespan |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| White Plaster | **$12 – $18** | 10–15 years |
| Colored Plaster | **$14 – $22** | 12–18 years |
| Quartz Aggregate | **$18 – $24** | 15–20 years |
| Pebble Finish | **$20 – $28** | 20–25 years |
| Tile Waterline | **$15 – $30** | 30+ years |
| Vinyl Liner | **$35 – $55** | 10–15 years |

**White plaster** is the budget option. It stains easily in Florida's hard water and roughs up quickly. Best for homeowners on a tight budget or selling a rental property.

**Colored plaster** hits the mid-tier — adds pigment to hide stains better than white. Still susceptible to etching but looks cleaner longer. Expect to pay **$14–$22 per sq ft**.

**Quartz aggregate** mixes crushed stone with plaster for maximum durability. It feels smoother than pebble and resists algae growth. This is the standard upgrade for most Florida homeowners.

**Pebble finish** uses larger river stones for a natural look. It is the most durable option at 20–25 years but can be rough on bare feet. Expect to pay **$20–$28 per sq ft**.

**Tile waterline** is purely aesthetic but protects the bond beam from water damage. It adds luxury and prevents algae growth at the water level. Expect to pay **$15–$30 per linear foot**.

**Vinyl liner** is specific to vinyl pool walls, not gunite. Different construction entirely from resurfacing. Requires full liner replacement rather than plaster application.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Florida Building Code Chapter 4504 governs all pool safety barriers and construction. Key requirements include self-closing gates, specific fence heights, and anti-entrapment drain covers. These go beyond what most states require.

**Permits:** Apply through your local county building department. Miami-Dade uses ePlan portal, while Orange County uses their online permit system. Turnaround is 5–15 business days for a standard residential permit. Budget **$150–$500** for the fee. Inspections required before and after resurfacing.

**Insurance Impact:** A pool increases your liability exposure. Insurers often raise premiums by **$300–$600 per year** for homes with pools. A new resurface ensures safety features meet current code, preventing non-renewal due to safety hazards.

**HOA:** Many Florida communities restrict pool colors, finishes, and equipment placement. Tile is often required in deed-restricted communities. Get written HOA approval before signing a contractor agreement — changing materials mid-job is extremely expensive.

**Season:** Best time to resurface a pool here is November through March — dry season, lower humidity, fewer storm delays. Avoid peak hurricane season (August–October). Rainy season (May–October) brings daily afternoon thunderstorms that delay projects.

**High Water Table:** Central and South Florida often have high water tables. If you drain a pool during wet season, groundwater can push the shell out of place. Contractors must add water back immediately after curing. This adds **$200–$500** in labor time.

**Soil Conditions:** Sinkholes are a risk in parts of Florida. If you live in a sinkhole-prone zone, the contractor must inspect the shell for voids before resurfacing. Structural voids cost extra to fill with grout or foam.

## How to Save Money on Pool Resurfacing in Florida

1. **Get at least 4 written quotes.** Contractor pricing varies by 20–35% for identical scope in Florida. On a **$9,000** job, that's **$1,800–$3,000** in potential savings. Make sure each quote itemizes materials, labor, drain, and disposal separately.

2. **Schedule in dry season.** November through March is slower for pool contractors. You'll have more leverage to negotiate and fewer weather delays. Some contractors offer **5–10%** off-season discounts.

3. **Choose quartz over pebble if budget is tight.** The savings between plaster (**$12–$18/sq ft**) and pebble (**$20–$28/sq ft**) can be **$2,000–$4,000** on a typical home. Quartz offers 80% of the durability for half the price.

4. **Bundle equipment upgrades.** Upgrading pumps or heaters during a resurface is **40–60% cheaper** than standalone projects. The combined energy efficiency improvements can also cut your utility bill by hundreds per year.

5. **Check for pool safety compliance.** If your pool lacks a barrier, you may be fined by the county. Adding safety upgrades during resurfacing prevents code violations. Be cautious of contractors who skip safety features.

## When to Resurface Your Pool — Warning Signs

**Your pool is 10–15 years old.** Plaster finishes in South Florida degrade faster than in cooler climates due to intense UV, heat cycling, and salt air. A 15-year-old pool in Miami has taken more abuse than a 30-year-old pool in the Midwest.

**Visible stains that won't scrub.** Surface staining that resists acid washing means the plaster is porous. The finish has lost its integrity and cannot be cleaned effectively.

**Rough texture on feet.** If the pool feels like sandpaper, the plaster has etched away. This is dangerous for children and pets walking barefoot. Multiple rough spots usually mean the whole system has failed.

**Chipping or delamination.** If chunks of plaster are falling off, the bond to the gunite shell is broken. This exposes the shell to water damage. Immediate resurfacing is required to prevent structural failure.

## How to Hire a Pool Contractor in Florida

1. **Verify their Florida license** at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a Certified or Registered Contractor license for pool construction (C10).

2. **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify.

3. **Check for a local business tax receipt.** This confirms they're registered locally, not a storm chaser from out of state.

4. **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — material brand, finish type, number of square feet, drain, permits, labor, timeline, and warranty.

5. **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings.

6. **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, drain complete, mid-project, final inspection passed.

7. **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, material specs, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, permit responsibility, and cleanup plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a pool resurface cost in Florida in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$5,000 and $15,000**. The average is around **$9,500**. Your actual cost depends on pool size, material choice, complexity, and whether structural repairs are needed.

### Do I need a permit to resurface my pool in Florida?

Yes, usually. Apply through your county building department. Budget **$150–$500**. Two inspections required. Never let a contractor skip the permit — it can void your insurance and create title issues when you sell.

### How long does a pool resurface take in Florida?

Plaster resurfaces take **3–5 days** for an average pool. Aggregate finishes take **5–7 days**. Rainy season can add 2–5 days of weather delays.

### Does a new pool surface increase home value in Florida?

A new pool resurface recoups **60–70%** of its cost at resale. But the bigger value in Florida's market is safety — a home with a code-compliant pool is dramatically easier to sell because buyers can actually insure it.

### Can I resurface my own pool in Florida?

No, Florida law requires licensed contractors for structural pool work. You still need permits, inspections, and must meet all safety code requirements. Insurance companies may refuse to cover a DIY pool. For most people, it's not worth the risk.

### Does homeowners insurance cover pool resurfacing?

Storm damage (wind, hail, fallen trees) is typically covered minus your deductible — often **2% of insured value** for hurricane claims in Florida. Normal wear and aging is never covered.
