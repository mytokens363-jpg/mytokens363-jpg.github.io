# How Much Does It Cost to Paint House Exterior in Florida? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** Painting the exterior of a single-family home in Florida costs between **$4,500 and $12,000** for most properties, with the average project landing around **$7,200**. The final price depends heavily on siding material, the amount of prep work required, and whether your home sits in the High Velocity Hurricane Zone.

Florida weather destroys paint faster than anywhere else in the country. Intense UV rays, salt air, and frequent rain mean your house needs specific primers and higher-quality coatings than other states. Skipping quality materials here leads to peeling and mold within two years.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$4,500 – $6,000** | 1,200 sq ft stucco, 1 coat |
| Mid-Range | **$6,000 – $9,000** | 2,000 sq ft, full prep, 2 coats |
| High-End | **$9,000 – $12,000+** | 2,500+ sq ft, brick/wood, premium paint |

**Budget** covers simple stucco homes in North Florida with minimal repairs. It includes pressure washing, caulking, and a single coat of standard acrylic latex paint.

**Mid-range** is the standard for most South Florida homeowners. This includes thorough mold treatment, high-pressure washing, detailed caulking, and two coats of premium paint on stucco or wood siding.

**High-end** applies to historic homes, brick facades, or properties in Miami-Dade requiring specialized adhesion primers. It covers complex trim, multiple stories, and the highest grade weather-resistant coatings.

These prices reflect Florida market rates as of early 2026. They include labor, materials, prep work, and cleanup. They do not include structural repairs like replacing rotted wood or stucco patches before painting.

## What Affects the Cost in Florida

### 1. Square Footage

Painting costs are often calculated per square foot of exterior surface area. A typical Florida ranch home has 1,500 to 2,500 sq ft of exterior wall. At **$2.00–$4.50 per sq ft**, a 1,500 sq ft house costs **$3,000–$6,750** while a 2,500 sq ft home runs **$5,000–$11,250**.

### 2. Siding Material

Material dictates prep time and paint type. Stucco is porous and requires more paint and primer. Wood siding needs extensive sanding and caulking. Brick requires specific masonry paint that costs more than standard latex. Aluminum siding needs etching primers for adhesion.

### 3. Prep Work Intensity

Florida homes need aggressive prep. Mold and mildew are constant threats. Pressure washing is mandatory, usually costing **$0.10–$0.20 per sq ft** if done separately. Scraping loose paint and sanding rough spots adds labor hours. If mold remediation is needed, add **$500–$1,500** to the total.

### 4. Height and Access

Single-story homes are cheaper to paint. Two-story homes require scaffolding or large ladders. This increases setup time and safety insurance costs. Adding **$1,000–$2,500** is common for homes with second stories or steep roofs that need touch-ups.

### 5. Trim and Details

Complex trim with decorative molding requires hand-brushing rather than spraying. This slows down the crew significantly. Homes with shutters, columns, or intricate window frames can add **$1,000–$3,000** to the labor bill compared to a flat wall design.

### 6. Location and Travel

South Florida contractors charge more than North Florida crews due to higher cost of living and insurance rates. A contractor in Miami-Dade might charge **$65–$85 per hour** versus **$50–$70 per hour** in Tallahassee. Travel fees apply if the job is outside their standard service area.

## Cost by Material Type

| Material | Cost per Sq Ft | Lifespan |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Stucco | **$2.00 – $3.50** | 5–10 years |
| Brick | **$2.50 – $4.00** | 10–15 years |
| Wood Siding | **$3.00 – $5.00** | 5–8 years |
| Aluminum Siding | **$2.00 – $3.50** | 8–12 years |
| Hardie Board | **$2.50 – $4.50** | 10–15 years |

**Stucco** is the most common Florida surface. It absorbs water easily so quality primer is non-negotiable. Standard acrylic latex lasts 5–10 years before fading or chalking.

**Brick** requires masonry-grade paint that breathes to let moisture escape. If you paint brick, you generally cannot paint over it again without stripping. Expect **$2.50–$4.00 per sq ft**.

**Wood Siding** is popular in coastal areas but rots easily. It requires sanding and sealing every few years. Paint jobs on wood are labor-intensive due to the need for caulking gaps.

**Aluminum Siding** was common in the 1970s and 80s. It oxidizes over time, so etching primer is required. Without it, paint will peel within a year of application.

**Hardie Board** (fiber cement) is the modern standard. It resists rot and bugs better than wood. Paint adheres well but still requires proper priming. Lifespan matches stucco but looks sharper.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** The Florida Building Code requires specific mold-resistant primers in coastal zones. In coastal construction zones, all exterior paint systems must meet ASTM standards for mold resistance. This prevents fungal growth behind the paint film.

**Hurricane Zone:** Miami-Dade and Broward County have strict adhesion standards for exterior coatings. If your home is in the High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ), contractors must use products with Miami-Dade NOA approval. This ensures paint won't blow off during high winds.

**Permits:** Most Florida cities do not require building permits for cosmetic painting. However, some historic districts like St. Augustine or Key West require approval for color changes. Check with your local building department before starting work.

**Insurance Impact:** A fresh paint job helps maintain your home's insurability. Mold damage claims can skyrocket if exterior paint fails and water gets inside. Some carriers offer small discounts for updated exteriors that reduce maintenance risk.

**HOA:** Homeowners Associations are very strict about paint colors in Florida. Many communities require approval for color changes before work begins. Changing a color without HOA approval can lead to fines or being forced to repaint.

**Season:** Avoid June through November. High humidity and daily thunderstorms delay drying times. The best time to paint is December through April. Dry air and lower humidity ensure proper curing.

**My Safe Florida Home:** While painting itself isn't a hardening improvement, you may qualify for a free wind inspection. If they find issues with your siding or flashing, repairs might be covered by grants. Check eligibility at [My Safe Florida Home](https://www.mysafefloridahome.gov).

## How to Save Money on Exterior Painting in Florida

1. **Get at least 4 written quotes.** Contractor pricing varies by **20–30%** for identical scope. On a **$7,000** job, that is **$1,400–$2,100** in potential savings. Ensure each quote lists prep steps, brand of paint, and number of coats.

2. **Schedule in dry season.** December through April is slower for painters. You have more leverage to negotiate and fewer weather delays. Some contractors offer **5–10%** off-season discounts during this window.

3. **Do your own landscaping prep.** Move furniture, cover plants, and clear walkways before the crew arrives. This saves them labor time. They might charge **$100–$200** for moving heavy items if you don't do it yourself.

4. **Choose standard colors.** Custom tinting adds cost to every gallon. Stick to standard manufacturer colors available at big box stores. This keeps material costs down and ensures future touch-ups are easy.

5. **Bundle with pressure washing.** Many crews offer a discount if you combine pressure washing with painting. Cleaning the house first ensures better paint adhesion and costs **$300–$600** separately. Bundling saves roughly **$150–$300**.

## When to Paint Your Exterior — Warning Signs

**Your paint is peeling or chalking.** Run your hand over the wall. If paint rubs off on your fingers, the binder has failed. This exposes the surface to water and UV damage. It is time to repaint immediately.

**You see black or green mold.** Dark spots indicate moisture retention. Mold eats into stucco and wood. Painting over active mold traps moisture and causes rot underneath. Clean and treat the area before painting.

**The trim looks cracked or rotted.** Wood trim rots from the inside out. If you can push a screwdriver into the wood, it needs replacement. Painting over rot will not stop it from spreading.

**Your siding is fading.** Faded paint means UV protection is gone. Colors look washed out. Faded surfaces are more susceptible to water intrusion. Replace the topcoat to protect the substrate.

## How to Hire an Exterior Painting Contractor in Florida

1. **Verify their Florida license.** Check at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a Certified General Contractor (CGC), Certified Residential Contractor (CRC), or Certified Painting Contractor (if applicable).

2. **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify coverage is active.

3. **Check for local business tax receipt.** This confirms they are registered locally, not a storm chaser. Look for the specific county tax receipt number on their contract.

4. **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — surface prep, primers, paint brand, number of coats, and warranty terms.

5. **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints about weather delays or mold recurrence.

6. **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, prep complete, final inspection passed.

7. **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, material specs, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, and cleanup plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does exterior painting cost in Florida in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$4,500 and $12,000**. The average is around **$7,200**. Your actual cost depends on siding material, house size, and prep work required for mold or salt damage.

### Do I need a permit to paint my house in Florida?

Usually no. Cosmetic painting does not require a building permit in most cities. However, historic districts and HOAs may require approval for color changes. Always check local rules first.

### How long does an exterior paint job take in Florida?

A standard single-story home takes **3–5 days**. Two-story homes take **5–7 days**. Rainy season can add 2–5 days of weather delays. Plan for the dry season.

### Does a new paint job increase home value in Florida?

Painting recoups **85–100%** of its cost at resale. But the bigger value in Florida's market is curb appeal and protection. Fresh paint signals maintenance to buyers and insurers.

### Can I paint my own exterior in Florida?

Yes, homeowners can paint their own homes. But you still must meet all safety and code requirements. Insurance companies may refuse to cover a DIY exterior job if it fails and causes water damage.

### Does homeowners insurance cover exterior paint?

Storm damage (wind, hail) is typically covered if it causes structural damage. Normal wear and tear, fading, or peeling due to age is never covered. Maintenance is your responsibility.
