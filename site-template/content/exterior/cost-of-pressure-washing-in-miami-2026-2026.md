# How Much Does It Cost to Pressure Wash a House in Miami? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** A complete exterior pressure washing service in Miami costs between **$400 and $1,500** for most single-family homes, with the average homeowner paying around **$850**. The biggest factors driving your price are square footage, surface material type, and Miami-Dade County's strict environmental runoff regulations.

Miami homes face unique challenges compared to the rest of the country. Constant humidity, salt air, and intense UV exposure create rapid algae growth and mold accumulation. Standard pressure washing often damages stucco or wood, so soft wash techniques are required to protect the exterior finish while cleaning effectively.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$400 – $600** | Driveway, walkways, small patios |
| Mid-Range | **$600 – $1,000** | Full exterior soft wash, 2,000 sq ft |
| High-End | **$1,000 – $1,500+** | Multi-story, stucco, complex landscaping |

**Budget** covers basic hard surface cleaning like concrete driveways or walkways without chemical treatments. It usually involves high-pressure rinsing only and excludes siding or roof cleaning.

**Mid-range** includes a full soft wash treatment for stucco siding, windows, and fences. This uses low-pressure chemical solutions to kill mold and algae without damaging the surface. Covers homes in the 2,000 to 3,000 sq ft range.

**High-end** handles multi-story homes requiring scaffolding or lifts, plus complex landscaping protection. Includes roof cleaning, gutters, and specialized stain removal for heavy salt or water damage.

These prices reflect Miami market rates as of early 2026 and include labor, equipment, chemicals, and cleanup. They do not include repairs to damaged stucco or wood if discovered during cleaning.

## What Affects the Cost in Miami

### 1. Square Footage

Cleaning cost scales directly with the surface area exposed to the elements. A small bungalow with 1,200 sq ft of exterior surface costs roughly **$400–$600**. A large Mediterranean-style home with 3,500 sq ft pushes the price to **$1,200–$1,500**. Contractors measure by the square foot of siding and driveway combined.

### 2. Surface Type

Stucco requires soft washing to avoid etching. Concrete allows for higher pressure. Wood decks need gentle treatment to prevent splintering. Stucco cleaning averages **$0.25–$0.40 per sq ft** while concrete driveways run **$0.20–$0.35 per sq ft**. Switching between surface types increases labor time.

### 3. Access Difficulty

Single-story homes are straightforward to clean. Two-story homes require ladders or scaffolding, which adds **$200–$400** to the labor cost. Tight backyards with limited vehicle access may require dragging hoses manually, increasing time by **30–60 minutes**.

### 4. Water Source

Contractors usually bring their own water tank or hook into your meter. If they use your meter, expect to pay for the water usage separately at **$5–$15** depending on flow. If they use a tank, the fuel cost is included in the quote. Metered water is more common in Miami.

### 5. Environmental Compliance

Miami-Dade County regulates chemical runoff to protect local waterways. Contractors must use biodegradable surfactants and capture wastewater where possible. This compliance adds **$100–$200** to the cost compared to unregulated regions. You may need to sign a waiver acknowledging runoff rules.

### 6. Seasonality

Demand peaks in March and April before hurricane season. Scheduling in May or June might save **5–10%** on the total price. Rainy season (June–October) causes delays, so contractors often charge a premium to guarantee scheduling during dry windows.

**Concrete driveway** cleaning is the most common request. It removes oil stains and tire marks. High-pressure washing works well here but can etch old concrete if not done carefully. Expect to see results immediately.

**Stucco siding** is the most common exterior finish in Miami. It is porous and absorbs moisture quickly. Soft washing kills the mold at the root without blasting the finish off. Doing this annually prevents structural water damage.

**Wood deck** cleaning requires bleach solutions to remove black mold and gray oxidation. High pressure splinters wood fibers. Proper sealing after cleaning extends the life of the deck by several years.

**Tile roof** cleaning is delicate work. Walking on tiles can crack them. Contractors use ground-based soft wash systems to remove black streaks without physical contact. This protects the warranty on your roofing material.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Miami-Dade County does not require a building permit for standard residential pressure washing. However, the Environmental Protection Division regulates chemical usage. You cannot dump high-chemical runoff into storm drains. Contractors must use neutralized solutions and contain wastewater if possible.

**Hurricane Zone:** While pressure washing is not a structural improvement, it maintains the integrity of the building envelope. Mold growth behind stucco can hide water intrusion. Keeping the exterior clean helps inspectors identify leaks before a storm. This protects your claim eligibility during hurricane season.

**Permits:** No permit is needed for residential cleaning unless it involves structural repairs. Commercial properties may need a business tax receipt for the service provider. Always verify the contractor has a current Miami-Dade business license to operate legally within the county.

**Insurance Impact:** Regular maintenance like pressure washing helps prevent mold claims. Many Florida insurers require proof of maintenance for older homes. A clean exterior reduces the risk of water damage and mold remediation costs. Documenting regular cleaning can support your home value.

**HOA:** Most Miami communities have strict rules about exterior appearance. Some prohibit high-pressure washing that creates runoff. You may need written approval from the architectural committee before starting work. Violating these rules can lead to fines or forced re-cleaning.

**Season:** Best time to pressure wash is November through April. The dry season ensures surfaces dry quickly and chemicals work effectively. Avoid June through October when humidity prevents proper drying. Rain can wash away cleaning agents before they kill the mold.

**My Safe Florida Home:** This program offers free wind inspections for homeowners. While it does not cover cleaning costs, the inspection report may recommend surface maintenance to protect the building envelope. Use the inspection report to justify maintenance expenses to your insurance company.

## How to Save Money on Pressure Washing in Miami

1. **Bundle services together.** Combining gutter cleaning with pressure washing saves **10–15%** on the total bill. Contractors travel to the site once and can switch equipment quickly. Ask for a package deal on exterior maintenance.

2. **Schedule in the off-season.** March and April are peak demand times. Booking in late May or September might get you **5–10%** off the standard rate. Contractors have more availability and want to keep their crews working.

3. **Clear the perimeter yourself.** Remove furniture, grills, and plants from the work area before they arrive. This saves the contractor time setting up and moving items. Some companies offer discounts for prepped sites.

4. **Get multiple quotes.** Pricing varies by **20–30%** between contractors in Miami. On a **$800** job, this difference is **$160–$240**. Ensure each quote specifies soft wash vs. pressure wash to compare apples to apples.

5. **Check for HOA compliance first.** If you clean without HOA approval, you might be forced to redo the work or pay fines. Verify rules upfront to avoid wasted money. This step costs nothing but prevents future headaches.

## When to Pressure Wash Your Home — Warning Signs

**You see black streaks on the stucco.** These are Gloeocapsa magma algae, common in humid climates. They hold moisture against the wall and can cause paint to peel. Address this within **6 months** to prevent deeper damage.

**Gutters are overflowing with debris.** Clogged gutters cause water to run down the exterior walls. This creates staining and potential water intrusion behind the siding. Clean gutters every **6–12 months** in Miami.

**Neighbors are getting fines.** HOAs often send violation notices for dirty exteriors. A clean home maintains property values in the neighborhood. Ignoring these notices can lead to liens or forced contractor hiring at your expense.

**Insurance agent requests maintenance.** Some carriers require proof of regular maintenance for older homes. A pressure washing receipt can serve as documentation. Keep records of all exterior work for **3–5 years**.

## How to Hire a Pressure Washing Contractor in Miami

1. **Verify their Florida license.** Check the Division of Professions at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a Certified or Registered contractor license for work over **$2,500**.

2. **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum **$1 million**) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify the policy is active.

3. **Check for a Miami-Dade business tax receipt.** This confirms they are registered locally, not a transient worker. Verify the receipt is current and covers exterior cleaning services.

4. **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — soft wash chemicals, pressure wash only, surface area, labor hours, and cleanup plan.

5. **Check reviews on Google and Yelp.** Look for patterns in complaints about damage or missed spots. Positive reviews about professionalism are more valuable than star ratings.

6. **Never pay more than 50% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, completion, final inspection passed.

7. **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, chemical types used, water source, timeline, warranty terms, and cleanup plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does pressure washing cost in Miami in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$400 and $1,500**. The average is around **$850**. Your actual cost depends on square footage, surface type, and access difficulty.

### Do I need a permit to pressure wash my house in Miami-Dade?

No, residential permits are not required. However, you must follow environmental runoff rules. Commercial cleaning requires a business tax receipt. Never let a contractor dump chemicals into storm drains.

### How often should I pressure wash my stucco in Miami?

Annually is recommended due to humidity. Stucco absorbs moisture quickly and supports mold growth. Waiting longer than **12 months** allows black algae to set in deep.

### Does pressure washing increase home value in Miami?

A clean exterior improves curb appeal significantly. It can recoup **10–15%** of the cost at resale if it prevents visible damage. More importantly, it keeps your home insurable by reducing mold risk.

### Can I pressure wash my own home in Florida?

Yes, if you own the home. But be careful with stucco. High pressure can etch the finish and void warranties. Soft wash is safer for Miami homes. Check HOA rules before starting.

### Does homeowners insurance cover pressure washing?

No, routine maintenance is never covered. However, mold damage caused by lack of maintenance may be denied. Regular cleaning helps maintain your claim eligibility for water damage.
