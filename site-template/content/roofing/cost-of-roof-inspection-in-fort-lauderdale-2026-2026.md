# How Much Does It Cost to Roof Inspection in Fort Lauderdale? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** A professional roof inspection in Fort Lauderdale costs between **$150 and $500** for most homeowners. The average price sits around **$275** for a standard visual and structural assessment. Prices jump higher for detailed moisture scans or comprehensive hurricane compliance reports.

Fort Lauderdale homeowners need inspections for insurance renewals, real estate transactions, or suspected storm damage. A simple visual check from the ground costs less but often misses critical issues. Broward County requires specific documentation for insurance claims and permit applications, which dictates the depth of your inspection.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$150 – $200** | Ground visual, basic report |
| Mid-Range | **$200 – $350** | Walking roof, moisture scan |
| High-End | **$350 – $500+** | Thermal imaging, structural eval |

**Budget** covers a ground-level visual inspection using binoculars or basic drone footage. It identifies obvious issues like missing shingles or sagging. This often satisfies a quick real estate query but rarely meets insurance company requirements for detailed documentation.

**Mid-range** is the standard choice for insurance renewals and maintenance planning. It involves walking the roof to check flashings, vents, and underlayment condition. Includes a basic moisture reading with a pin meter to detect hidden leaks.

**High-end** adds thermal imaging cameras and structural engineering assessments. Useful for older homes or those with water damage history. Includes detailed digital photos and compliance documentation for Florida Building Code or insurance mitigation credits.

These prices reflect Fort Lauderdale market rates as of early 2026 and include travel within Broward County. They do not include repair estimates unless requested separately. Some contractors waive the inspection fee if you sign a repair contract with them.

## What Affects the Cost in Fort Lauderdale

### 1. Roof Size and Square Footage

Inspections are often priced by square footage rather than a flat fee. A small 1,200 sq ft bungalow might cost **$150–$200**. A large 3,000 sq ft estate with complex geometry runs **$350–$500**. Inspectors charge more for larger surface areas because they need more time to walk every section.

### 2. Roof Accessibility and Safety

Flat roofs or steep pitches increase the difficulty. If you need special equipment like scaffolding or lift platforms, add **$100–$200** to the cost. Fort Lauderdale homes near the Intracoastal often have taller two-story structures requiring safety harnesses and fall protection gear.

### 3. Inspection Purpose

Insurance companies want different data than homebuyers. A wind mitigation inspection for insurance costs less than a pre-purchase structural evaluation. Insurance forms (OIR B1-1802) require specific measurements of roof-to-wall connections. Structural evaluations cost more because they involve digging into the framing.

### 4. Location in Fort Lauderdale

Coastal homes face higher corrosion rates from salt air. Inspectors spend more time checking metal components like flashing and fasteners in coastal zones. Inland Fort Lauderdale neighborhoods might have slightly lower rates due to easier access and less weather-related wear.

### 5. Report Detail Level

A simple text summary costs less than a full digital package with annotated photos. Some inspectors charge extra for a PDF report formatted for insurance carriers. If you need a certified engineer's stamp, expect to pay **$400–$1,000** extra for the structural certification.

### 6. Seasonal Timing

Demand spikes after hurricane season (November). Scheduling a roof inspection in Fort Lauderdale during the dry winter months (December–April) is cheaper. Summer rains can delay inspections, and some contractors charge rush fees during peak storm recovery times.

## Cost by Material Type

| Material | Inspection Difficulty | Cost Impact |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|
| Asphalt Shingles | **Easy** | **Base Rate** |
| Concrete Tile | **Moderate** | **+20%** |
| Clay Tile | **Hard** | **+30%** |
| Metal Roofing | **Easy** | **Base Rate** |
| Flat TPO/EPDM | **Moderate** | **+15%** |
| Wood Shake | **Hard** | **+25%** |

**Asphalt shingles** are the most common roof in Fort Lauderdale. Inspectors can walk them easily without damaging the surface. They check for granule loss, curling, and blistering. This is the baseline pricing for inspections.

**Concrete tile** requires careful walking techniques to avoid cracking. Inspectors often use specialized pads or walk on the battens rather than tiles. This extra care increases labor time by 20%. Tile also hides underlying rot more easily, requiring more probing.

**Clay tile** is fragile and heavy. Many inspectors use drones for the initial assessment before walking the perimeter. Walking requires extreme caution to avoid breakage. The inspection fee reflects the higher risk of liability and slower pace.

**Metal roofing** is durable but shows fastener issues clearly. Inspectors check for loose screws and sealant degradation. It is relatively quick to inspect compared to tile, but corrosion checks in coastal areas take extra time.

**Flat TPO or EPDM** membranes require a different skill set. Inspectors look for blisters, wrinkles, and seam integrity. They often use moisture meters to detect water trapped under the membrane. Flat roofs in mid-century Fort Lauderdale are common and need specific attention.

**Wood shake** is rare in modern builds but exists in historic districts. Inspectors check for moss, rot, and insect damage. These materials degrade faster in Florida humidity, requiring more thorough documentation.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Florida Building Code 8th Edition (2023) sets standards for roof inspections. Inspectors must verify if the roof meets current wind uplift requirements. Older homes built before 2001 may not have hurricane straps. Documenting this affects insurance eligibility and retrofit costs.

**Hurricane Zone:** Fort Lauderdale is inside the High Velocity Hurricane Zone. Inspections must verify if roof coverings have Miami-Dade NOA approval. If your roof was installed without proper fastener schedules, the inspector will note it. This impacts your ability to claim wind damage later.

**Permits:** Broward County does not require a permit for a visual inspection alone. However, if the inspection leads to repairs, you need a permit. The inspection report often serves as the engineering documentation required for the permit application.

**Insurance Impact:** Florida insurers demand detailed inspections for homes over 15 years old. A negative inspection can lead to non-renewal or higher premiums. A positive inspection with a wind mitigation form can save **15–45%** on your annual premium.

**HOA Restrictions:** Many Fort Lauderdale communities have strict rules about roof color and material. An inspection report might flag non-compliant materials. You need written HOA approval before changing roofing types. Fines for non-compliance can reach **$100+ per day**.

**Season:** Hurricane season runs June to November. Inspectors are booked solid after major storms. Scheduling in January or February ensures faster turnaround. Rain delays inspections and pushes back report delivery by several days.

**My Safe Florida Home:** This state program offers free wind inspections for eligible homeowners. If you qualify, you get a grant up to **$10,000** for mitigation. The inspection is part of the application process. It verifies roof deck attachment and coverings.

## How to Save Money on Roof Inspection in Fort Lauderdale

1. **Bundle with repair estimates.** Many roofing companies waive the inspection fee if you hire them for repairs. This is common for storm damage claims. Ask specifically if the fee is credited against the final contract.

2. **Compare three licensed contractors.** Pricing varies by 20% for identical scopes in Broward County. Get written quotes from a general contractor, a roofing specialist, and a structural engineer. Check their licenses at MyFloridaLicense.com.

3. **Schedule in dry season.** December through April has less rain. Contractors are more available and less likely to charge rush fees. Avoid August and September when demand is highest after storm season.

4. **Check for insurance discounts.** Some insurance companies partner with inspection firms for discounted rates. Ask your agent if they have a preferred vendor list. You might save **$50–$100** off the standard rate.

5. **Use drone technology for initial checks.** Some companies offer a preliminary drone scan for **$50**. If issues are found, they can do a detailed follow-up. This helps narrow down the scope before paying for a full walk-up.

## When to Replace Your Roof — Warning Signs

**Your insurance company is requesting it.** A letter from your carrier asking for an inspection means they are evaluating risk. Ignoring this can lead to policy cancellation. Many Florida carriers will not renew without a current inspection report.

**Your roof is over 15 years old.** Asphalt shingles in South Florida degrade faster due to UV exposure. Salt air accelerates corrosion on metal components. A 15-year-old roof has likely passed its useful life expectancy for insurance purposes.

**Visible granule loss or sagging.** Check your gutters after heavy rain. Piles of dark sand-like granules indicate shingle failure. Sagging areas suggest structural decking rot. Both are urgent issues requiring immediate professional assessment.

**Water stains in the attic.** Brown spots on ceiling drywall or damp insulation indicate active leaks. In Fort Lauderdale's humidity, leaks lead to mold quickly. You need a professional to trace the source before mold remediation costs spiral.

## How to Hire a Roofing Contractor in Fort Lauderdale

1. **Verify their Florida license** at MyFloridaLicense.com. You need a Certified Roofing Contractor (CCC), Registered Roofing Contractor (RC), or General Contractor (CGC) license.

2. **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify.

3. **Check for a Broward County local business tax receipt.** This confirms they are registered locally, not a storm chaser from out of state.

4. **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — inspection type, report format, turnaround time, and warranty.

5. **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings. Focus on how they handle follow-up issues.

6. **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, tear-off complete, mid-project, final inspection passed.

7. **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, material specs with NOA numbers, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, permit responsibility, and cleanup plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a roof inspection cost in Fort Lauderdale in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$150 and $500**. The average is around **$275**. Your actual cost depends on roof size, material complexity, and whether you need a detailed engineering report.

### Do I need a permit to get a roof inspection in Broward County?

No, a standard visual inspection does not require a building permit. However, if the inspection identifies damage requiring repairs, you need a permit. The inspection report often serves as the engineering documentation for that permit.

### How long does a roof inspection take in Fort Lauderdale?

A standard visual inspection takes **1–2 hours** for an average home. Detailed inspections with thermal imaging take **3–4 hours**. You will receive the report within **2–5 business days** depending on contractor workload.

### Does a roof inspection increase home value in Fort Lauderdale?

A clean inspection report adds marketability. It reassures buyers that the roof is sound. In Fort Lauderdale's competitive market, a new roof inspection can help close deals faster during hurricane season.

### Can I inspect my own roof in Florida?

You can walk your own roof if you are comfortable, but it voids insurance claims if you damage it. Florida's homeowner exemption allows it if you own and occupy the home. For insurance purposes, a licensed inspector is usually required.

### Does homeowners insurance cover the inspection cost?

Standard policies do not cover routine maintenance inspections. However, if you file a storm damage claim, the insurance adjuster's inspection is free. You may pay for a second opinion if you disagree with their findings.
