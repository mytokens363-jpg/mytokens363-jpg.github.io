# How Much Does It Cost to Get Termite Treatment in Orlando? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** Professional termite treatment in Orlando costs between **$500 and $2,500** for initial protection, with annual inspections and maintenance ranging from **$100 to $300**. The biggest factors driving your price are the treatment method, the size of your home, and whether you have a slab foundation or crawlspace.

Orlando sits in a high-risk termite zone where the sandy soil and humidity create ideal conditions for subterranean colonies. Your treatment needs to account for this aggressive environment to ensure long-term protection for your structure.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$500 – $800** | Spot treatment, minor infestation |
| Mid-Range | **$800 – $1,500** | Full perimeter liquid barrier |
| High-End | **$1,500 – $2,500+** | Baiting system or fumigation |

**Budget** covers spot treatments for isolated colonies or small infestations detected early. This usually involves drilling specific holes in the slab and injecting termiticide directly into the active area. It is not a preventative measure for the whole home.

**Mid-range** is the standard approach for most Orlando homeowners — a full perimeter liquid barrier around the foundation. This creates a chemical wall that termites cannot cross. It includes drilling, injection, and a one-year warranty on the chemical barrier.

**High-end** covers baiting systems or whole-structure fumigation. Baiting uses stations placed around the property to eliminate colonies underground. Fumigation (tenting) is for severe drywood infestations and requires vacating the home for days.

These prices reflect Orlando market rates as of early 2026 and include the initial treatment, labor, chemicals, and initial warranty coverage. They do not include structural repair costs if damage is found during the inspection phase.

## What Affects the Cost in Orlando

### 1. Home Size and Square Footage

Termite pricing is often calculated based on the linear footage of your home's foundation or total square footage. A standard 1,500 sq ft Orlando ranch will cost less to treat than a 3,000 sq ft estate. Expect to pay **$2.50 to $4.00 per linear foot** of perimeter for liquid treatment. Larger homes naturally require more chemicals and more time to drill and inject.

### 2. Treatment Method Choice

This is the single biggest price lever you control. Liquid termiticides create a barrier and are the most common choice at **$500–$1,500** for standard homes. Baiting systems cost more upfront at **$1,000–$2,500** because they require ongoing monitoring and station maintenance. Fumigation is the premium option at **$2,000–$5,000+** but is reserved for severe drywood infestations.

### 3. Foundation Type

Orlando homes vary widely in foundation design. Slab-on-grade foundations require drilling through concrete, which adds labor time and equipment costs. Homes with crawlspaces or basements allow for easier access to soil but may require additional vapor barriers. Slab drilling can add **$100–$300** to the base price depending on concrete thickness.

### 4. Labor Rates in Orlando

Licensed pest control technicians in Central Florida run **$40–$65 per hour**. This is higher than the national average due to the specialized licensing required by the state. Demand peaks in spring when swarmers appear, which can push prices up **10–15%** for emergency treatments during peak season.

### 5. Infestation Severity

A preventative inspection and light treatment costs less than treating an active colony. If your inspection reveals extensive mud tubes or structural damage, the scope expands to include wood replacement. Severe infestations may require multiple visits over several months to fully eliminate the colony.

### 6. Local Soil Conditions

Orlando's soil is sandy and acidic, which affects how long termiticides last. Sandy soil allows chemicals to drain faster, requiring more frequent re-treatments or higher concentrations of product. This natural factor is baked into the pricing to ensure the barrier lasts the full warranty period.

## Cost by Treatment Method

| Method | Cost Estimate | Frequency |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| Liquid Barrier | **$500 – $1,500** | Every 1–2 years |
| Baiting System | **$1,000 – $2,500** | Annual monitoring |
| Spot Treatment | **$300 – $600** | As needed |
| Fumigation | **$2,500 – $5,000+** | One-time only |
| Inspection Only | **$100 – $200** | Annual |
| Wood Treatment | **$10 – $20 per sq ft** | As needed |

**Liquid barrier** is the most common choice. It involves trenching around the foundation and injecting termiticide into the soil. This creates an invisible shield that kills termites on contact. Most warranties require annual inspections to maintain validity.

**Baiting systems** use stations placed around the property perimeter. Termites feed on the bait and carry it back to the colony to eliminate the queen. This method is chemical-light but requires monthly or quarterly checks to ensure bait consumption.

**Spot treatment** targets visible activity without treating the whole perimeter. It is a temporary fix for isolated problems but does not protect against new colonies entering the untreated areas. Best for renters or temporary fixes.

**Fumigation** (tenting) fills the entire structure with gas to kill drywood termites in the wood itself. This is the only way to treat termites inside the wood without removing it. It requires professional setup and vacating the home for 24–72 hours.

**Inspection only** is the lowest cost entry point. A licensed inspector looks for signs of activity and damage. This is required annually to maintain most warranty agreements and insurance eligibility.

**Wood treatment** involves painting or spraying borates directly onto exposed wood. This is often used on framing during construction or on existing wood that cannot be treated with soil barriers. It prevents new infestations but does not kill active colonies.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Florida Building Code Section 3003 addresses termite protection for new construction. While existing homes are not retrofitted for code, new renovations must meet these standards. This includes termite shields and physical barriers at foundation penetrations. Your contractor should follow these standards even on older homes.

**Insurance Impact:** Standard Florida homeowners insurance policies **do not cover** termite damage. Termite damage is considered a maintenance issue, not a sudden insurable event. You must pay for the treatment and any repairs out of pocket. However, a termite inspection is often required before closing on a home sale.

**Permits:** Orange County does not require a building permit for termite treatment. However, the company must hold a valid Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services (FDACS) license. Never hire an unlicensed applicator — they cannot legally sell termiticides or offer warranties.

**Season:** Spring is peak swarm season in Orlando (March through May). Subterranean termites swarm when the ground warms up. Treatments scheduled in late winter or early spring often cost less because contractors are less booked than during active swarm emergencies.

**HOA:** Many Orlando communities have strict rules about exterior baiting stations or chemical applications near water features. Check your HOA covenants before installing perimeter bait stations. Some communities require treatment plans to be pre-approved by the architectural review committee.

**My Safe Florida Home:** This program offers grants for wind mitigation, not termite protection. Termite damage is structural but falls outside the scope of hurricane hardening. Focus your grant funds on roof straps and windows instead.

**Soil Conditions:** Orlando has a high water table and sandy soil. This affects chemical retention. Liquid barriers in sandy soil may break down faster than in clay soil, requiring more frequent re-treatments or higher concentration products to maintain the barrier integrity.

## How to Save Money on Termite Treatment in Orlando

1. **Get at least 4 written quotes.** Pest control pricing varies by 20–30% in Orlando. On a **$1,500** treatment, that's **$300–$450** in potential savings. Compare liquid barrier costs against baiting systems to find the best value for your specific situation.

2. **Schedule in off-season.** December through February is slower for pest control. You will have more leverage to negotiate and fewer weather delays. Some companies offer **5–10%** discounts for winter appointments.

3. **Bundle with other services.** Many Orlando companies offer discounts if you bundle termite treatment with general pest control (ants, spiders, roaches). This can save **$100–$200** annually on your total maintenance bill.

4. **Maintain your warranty.** Most warranties require annual inspections. If you skip the inspection, you void the warranty and risk a full re-treatment cost later. Staying current saves you thousands in structural damage repairs down the road.

5. **Prevent access points.** Seal cracks in your foundation and remove wood-to-soil contact around your home. This reduces the likelihood of infestation and keeps your treatment costs lower by reducing the frequency of required follow-ups.

## When to Treat Your Home — Warning Signs

**You see swarmers indoors.** Flying termites inside your home, especially near windows or lights, mean an active colony is nearby. This is a clear sign you need immediate treatment. Do not wait for the next season to schedule an inspection.

**Mud tubes on foundation walls.** Subterranean termites build pencil-sized tubes of mud to travel between the soil and wood. These tubes are the most reliable sign of an active infestation. Find them on your foundation or piers and document them for the inspector.

**Hollow-sounding wood.** Tap on baseboards, door frames, or floor joists. If the wood sounds hollow or papery, termites may have eaten the interior. This indicates the colony has been there for years and damage is already done.

**Discarded wings.** After swarmers mate, they shed their wings. Piles of wings on your window sills or near doors are a dead giveaway. This usually happens in the spring after a rainstorm in Orlando.

## How to Hire a Termite Control Contractor in Orlando

1. **Verify their FDACS license.** Check the license status at the Florida Department of Agriculture website. You need a Category 3 (Structural Pest Control) license. This ensures they are trained in chemical handling and safety.

2. **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum **$1 million**) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify the policy is current.

3. **Check for an Orange County business tax receipt.** This confirms they are registered locally, not a storm chaser from out of state. Local contractors are more accountable to Orlando residents.

4. **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — inspection fee, treatment cost, chemical type, warranty duration, and follow-up visit schedule.

5. **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints about missed appointments or incomplete treatments.

6. **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, inspection complete, treatment complete.

7. **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, chemical brand, warranty terms, permit responsibility, and cleanup plan. Ensure the warranty specifies if it is transferable to new owners.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does termite treatment cost in Orlando in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$500 and $2,500** for initial treatment. The average is around **$1,200** for a standard liquid barrier. Your actual cost depends on home size, foundation type, and treatment method chosen.

### Do I need a permit for termite treatment in Orange County?

No building permit is required for termite treatment. However, the pest control company must hold a valid FDACS license. You should verify their license before they start work to ensure legality.

### How long does a termite treatment last in Orlando?

Liquid barriers typically last **5 to 10 years** depending on soil conditions. Baiting systems require **annual monitoring** to ensure the colony is eliminated. Annual inspections are required to keep most warranties valid.

### Does homeowners insurance cover termite damage in Florida?

No, standard policies exclude termite damage. Termite damage is considered a maintenance issue. You must pay for the treatment and repairs yourself. Some flood insurance policies cover structural damage but not termites.

### Can I do termite treatment myself in Florida?

You can buy retail termiticides, but you cannot legally apply them for profit. For structural protection, a licensed applicator is required to apply most professional-grade chemicals. DIY products rarely provide the same warranty coverage.

### Does a termite inspection affect home sales?

Yes, most buyers require a termite inspection before closing. A clean report speeds up the sale. A negative report can delay closing until the seller completes treatment. Budget for an inspection fee of **$100–$200** during the sale process.
