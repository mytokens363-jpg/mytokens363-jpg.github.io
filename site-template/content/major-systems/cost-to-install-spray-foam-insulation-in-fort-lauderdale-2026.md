# How Much Does It Cost to Install Spray Foam Insulation in Fort Lauderdale? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** Installing spray foam insulation in Fort Lauderdale costs between **$3,000 and $12,000** for most single-family homes, with the average project running around **$6,500**. The biggest factors driving your price are house size, attic accessibility, and the choice between open-cell and closed-cell foam.

Fort Lauderdale homes face unique challenges that standard insulation cannot handle. High humidity, salt air, and intense sun degrade traditional fiberglass quickly. Spray foam seals the envelope, stops air leaks, and adds moisture resistance that fits our coastal climate.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$3,000 – $5,000** | Open cell, attic floor only |
| Mid-Range | **$5,000 – $8,500** | Open cell attic + walls |
| High-End | **$8,500 – $12,000+** | Closed cell, full envelope |

**Budget** projects usually cover attic floor insulation using open-cell foam. This option is less dense and cheaper but does not provide a vapor barrier. It suits homes that already have good exterior moisture control.

**Mid-range** adds wall cavities to the attic application. This improves soundproofing and thermal performance significantly. It is the most common choice for 1,800 to 2,500 square foot Fort Lauderdale homes.

**High-end** involves closed-cell foam for the entire building envelope. It adds structural strength and acts as a flood barrier. This is ideal for coastal properties where flood zones and high humidity are concerns.

These prices reflect Fort Lauderdale market rates as of early 2026. They include materials, labor, safety equipment, and cleanup. They do not include structural repairs or HVAC modifications that might be needed after installation.

## What Affects the Cost in Fort Lauderdale

### 1. House Size and Square Footage

Insulation is priced by the square footage of the area being treated. A typical Fort Lauderdale ranch home has an attic size of 1,500 to 2,500 sq ft. Open-cell foam runs **$1.50–$2.50 per sq ft**, while closed-cell runs **$3.00–$5.00 per sq ft**. A 2,000 sq ft attic with open cell costs roughly **$3,000–$5,000**.

### 2. Foam Type Selection

This is the single biggest price lever you control. Open-cell foam is softer and cheaper, providing an R-value of **R-3.5 per inch**. Closed-cell foam is rigid and expensive, providing **R-6.0 to R-7.0 per inch**. Closed cell also blocks moisture, which is critical in Broward County.

### 3. Labor Rates in Fort Lauderdale

Insulation contractors in South Florida charge **$60–$90 per hour**. This is higher than the national average due to specialized training and safety requirements. Isocyanates require certified applicators with proper PPE. Labor makes up about **40–50%** of the total project cost.

### 4. Permits and Inspections

Broward County Building Department requires a permit for insulation work that affects the building envelope. Permit fees run **$150–$400** depending on the scope. The process includes a plan review and final inspection to verify R-values match code.

### 5. Existing Insulation Removal

Old fiberglass or batts often need removal before foam application. This adds **$1,000–$3,000** to the project if the attic is heavily contaminated. Some contractors charge extra for hazardous material disposal if asbestos is suspected in older homes.

### 6. HVAC Integration

Spray foam changes the airflow dynamics of your home. You may need to resize ductwork or upgrade your HVAC unit. This adds **$2,000–$5,000** if your system was previously sized for leaky air conditions. A manual J load calculation is required for code compliance.

## Cost by Material Type

| Material | Cost per Sq Ft | R-Value per Inch |
|---------|---------------|------------------|
| Open Cell | **$1.50 – $2.50** | **R-3.5** |
| Closed Cell | **$3.00 – $5.00** | **R-6.0+** |
| Dense Pack | **$2.00 – $3.50** | **R-3.7** |

**Open-cell foam** is water-based and expands quickly. It fills cracks and crevices well but absorbs moisture over time. It is best for interior walls in dry climates, though common in Fort Lauderdale attics.

**Closed-cell foam** is oil-based and creates a vapor barrier. It adds structural rigidity to roof decks and walls. It is the preferred choice for coastal Florida homes to prevent mold and rot.

**Dense-pack cellulose** is a recycled paper alternative. It costs less than foam but does not seal air as effectively. It is a budget option for attic floors where air sealing is less critical.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Florida Building Code 8th Edition (2023) Chapter 13 mandates specific R-values for energy conservation. Attics in Zone 2 (Florida) require **R-30 minimum**. Walls require **R-13 minimum**. Spray foam must be listed for use in the state and meet fire block requirements.

**HVHZ and Moisture:** While insulation isn't in the HVHZ structural category, moisture control is vital. Fort Lauderdale is a coastal zone. Closed-cell foam prevents salt air intrusion and reduces the risk of mold in walls. Open-cell foam must be paired with a separate vapor barrier in some cases.

**Permits:** Apply through Broward County's ePlan online portal. The Building Division reviews energy compliance. Turnaround is 5–10 business days. Budget **$150–$400** for the fee. Two inspections are required: pre-cover and final.

**Insurance Impact:** Upgrading insulation improves your home's energy rating. Some insurers offer discounts for homes with updated energy efficiency profiles. A wind mitigation inspection report might also change if the roof deck is sealed, potentially lowering premiums by **5–15%**.

**HOA:** Many Fort Lauderdale communities have deed restrictions on exterior work. If you are installing foam on exterior walls or modifying soffits, you need HOA approval. Failing to get this approval can lead to fines or forced removal.

**Season:** Best time to install is winter (December–March). Contractors are less busy, and the foam cures faster in moderate heat. Avoid peak hurricane season (August–October) when emergency repairs take priority.

**My Safe Florida Home:** This program focuses on wind mitigation. Insulation upgrades are not a primary rebate item under the current **$10,000** cap. However, energy efficiency improvements often qualify for separate utility rebates from FPL or the state energy office.

## How to Save Money on Insulation Installation in Fort Lauderdale

1.  **Get at least 4 written quotes.** Contractor pricing varies by **20–30%** for identical scope in Fort Lauderdale. On a **$6,000** job, that's **$1,200–$1,800** in potential savings. Make sure each quote itemizes foam type, R-value, and labor separately.

2.  **Schedule in dry season.** December through March is slower for insulation contractors. You'll have more leverage to negotiate and fewer weather delays. Some contractors offer **5–10%** off-season discounts for scheduling flexibility.

3.  **Choose open-cell for attics only.** If you live in a dry inland area of Fort Lauderdale, open-cell attic foam is cheaper. The savings between open cell (**$1.50/sq ft**) and closed cell (**$4.00/sq ft**) can be **$3,000–$5,000** on a large home.

4.  **Bundle with air sealing.** Ask for air sealing of penetrations during the foam job. Adding foam around outlets and ducts is often **30–40% cheaper** than a standalone air sealing project. The combined energy savings improve comfort immediately.

5.  **Check for utility rebates.** FPL and other utilities often offer energy efficiency rebates for insulation upgrades. Check the current program list before signing a contract. Rebates can cover **$500–$1,500** of the project cost depending on the program.

## When to Install Your Insulation — Warning Signs

**Your energy bills are rising.** If your cooling costs are up **20–30%** year-over-year without rate hikes, your insulation is likely failing. Fort Lauderdale summers are brutal, and old insulation loses R-value quickly in the heat.

**You feel hot air in the attic.** If you spend time in the attic and feel heat radiating from the roof deck, your insulation is insufficient. This means your HVAC is working overtime to fight the heat gain.

**You have mold or mildew.** Musty smells or visible mold in walls indicate air leakage and moisture intrusion. Spray foam seals these leaks and creates a dry environment that prevents mold growth.

**Your HVAC system is old.** If your AC is 15+ years old, new insulation might not fix the efficiency gap. However, sealing the home first ensures the new system works as intended.

**You see daylight through ceiling.** If you can see light coming through the ceiling joists, you have massive air leaks. Spray foam will seal these gaps instantly and stop the energy loss.

## How to Hire an Insulation Contractor in Fort Lauderdale

1.  **Verify their Florida license** at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a Certified or Registered Contractor license. Specialized foam installers often hold additional certifications.

2.  **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify coverage is active.

3.  **Check for safety training.** Foam application involves hazardous chemicals. Ask about their safety protocols and PPE usage. Certified applicators follow strict guidelines to prevent sensitization.

4.  **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — foam brand, thickness, R-value, prep work, permits, labor, and warranty.

5.  **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings. Pay attention to mentions of cleanup and odor issues.

6.  **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, prep complete, foam installed, final inspection passed.

7.  **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, foam specs with fire ratings, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, and cleanup plan.

### Does spray foam help with hurricane protection?

Closed-cell foam adds structural strength to walls and roofs. It can improve wind resistance ratings by sealing the sheathing. It also prevents water intrusion during heavy rains.
