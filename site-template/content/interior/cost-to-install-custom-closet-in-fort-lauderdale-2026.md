# How Much Does It Cost to Install Custom Closet in Fort Lauderdale? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** A full custom closet installation in Fort Lauderdale costs between **$3,500 and $15,000** for most bedrooms, with the average homeowner paying around **$7,500**. The biggest factors driving your price are the square footage, material selection, and whether you need structural wall modifications.

Fort Lauderdale's interior market has seen steady growth in 2026 as homeowners prioritize organization and storage efficiency over square footage expansion. Custom solutions fit better than generic systems and add tangible value to your resale portfolio.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$3,500 – $5,500** | Standard kit, laminate, small closet |
| Mid-Range | **$5,500 – $10,000** | Semi-custom, wood veneer, walk-in |
| High-End | **$10,000 – $15,000+** | Full custom, solid wood, lighting |

**Budget** gets you a pre-fabricated system or semi-custom kit installed on-site. It uses laminate particle board and basic hardware. Ideal for standard reach-in closets under 50 square feet.

**Mid-range** includes semi-custom carpentry with wood veneer finishes and upgraded shelving. Covers walk-in closets or rooms requiring new shelving layouts. Includes basic lighting integration.

**High-end** covers full bespoke carpentry with solid wood, glass doors, and integrated electrical. Designed for master suites or large walk-ins exceeding 100 square feet. Includes luxury hardware and finishes.

These prices reflect Fort Lauderdale market rates as of early 2026 and include materials, labor, and disposal. They do not include new electrical wiring for lighting or structural wall framing if required.

## What Affects the Cost in Fort Lauderdale

### 1. Closet Dimensions

Closets are priced by linear foot or square footage. A standard reach-in closet runs **10–15 linear feet**. A walk-in closet often measures **80–150 square feet**. Larger dimensions require more shelving, rods, and doors. A 100 sq ft walk-in costs roughly **$1,000** more than a 50 sq ft closet using the same materials.

### 2. Material Choice

This is the single biggest price lever you control. Laminate is the budget choice at **$150–$250 per linear foot** installed. Veneered plywood jumps to **$300–$500**. Solid hardwood is the premium option at **$600–$900 per linear foot**. Material choice directly impacts durability and resistance to humidity.

### 3. Labor Rates in Fort Lauderdale

Carpentry labor in Broward County runs **$75–$100 per hour**, roughly 15–20% above the national average. Skilled carpenters who can handle joinery command higher rates. Demand stays high year-round from new construction and renovation projects. Peak season (fall) can push rates to the top of the range.

### 4. Design Complexity

A simple shelf-and-rod layout is straightforward to install. Add drawers, tie racks, jewelry trays, and island units and labor climbs. Complex designs can add **$2,000–$4,000** to the project. Custom moldings and crown molding add aesthetic value but require skilled finishing.

### 5. Electrical and Lighting

Most closets are wired by default, but adding LED lighting requires an electrician. If you need new switches or dimmers, factor in **$500–$1,500** for electrical work. Low-voltage lighting is easier to install but still requires a licensed pro in Florida.

### 6. HOA Restrictions

Many Fort Lauderdale communities restrict interior changes visible from outside. If your closet involves a new door or changes exterior trim, the HOA may review plans. Get written approval before starting. Some communities restrict hardware finishes or door colors.

## Cost by Material Type

| Material | Cost per Linear Foot | Lifespan |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Laminate | **$150 – $250** | 10–15 years |
| Veneer | **$300 – $500** | 15–25 years |
| Plywood | **$350 – $550** | 20–30 years |
| Solid Wood | **$600 – $900** | 30+ years |
| Wire Shelving | **$100 – $200** | 20+ years |
| Glass Doors | **$200 – $400** | 25+ years |

**Laminate** is the most common budget option. It resists moisture better than raw wood but can chip over time. Best for guest closets or rental properties where cost is the priority.

**Veneer** offers the look of wood with a plywood core. It is more stable in Florida's humidity than solid wood. This is the sweet spot for most Fort Lauderdale homeowners seeking value.

**Plywood** is the workhorse of custom closets. It holds screws better than particle board and is less prone to swelling. Expect **$350–$550 per linear foot** for a quality plywood build.

**Solid wood** is the premium choice. Mahogany, cherry, or oak add significant home value. It expands and contracts with humidity changes, requiring precise joinery to prevent gaps. Expect to pay **$600–$900 per linear foot**.

**Wire shelving** is an alternative for storage-only closets. It is durable and allows airflow, preventing musty smells. Best for pantries or utility rooms rather than master bedrooms.

**Glass doors** add a high-end look and visibility. They are prone to fingerprints and scratches but look sleek. **$200–$400** depends on the glass thickness and hardware quality.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Humidity Control:** Fort Lauderdale has high humidity year-round. Wood expands in summer and contracts in winter. Custom closets need expansion gaps and proper sealing to prevent warping. Use moisture-resistant finishes like catalyzed lacquer or polyurethane.

**Building Permits:** Standard closet installations generally do not require a building permit in Broward County unless you alter plumbing or electrical. If you are framing new walls for a closet, a permit is required. Budget **$100–$300** for permit fees if structural work is needed.

**HOA Approval:** Many Fort Lauderdale neighborhoods have deed restrictions on interior finishes. Some require specific wood tones or prohibit certain hardware styles. Submit photos to your HOA before ordering materials to avoid delays.

**Insurance Impact:** Closets do not directly affect homeowners insurance premiums. However, a high-quality closet system adds to the home's overall replacement cost value. Ensure your insurer knows the value of your built-ins when you renew.

**Termites:** South Florida is a high-risk termite zone. Solid wood closets can attract termites if not treated. Use termite-resistant wood or apply borate treatments during installation. Inspect your closet annually for mud tubes.

**Season:** Best time to install is dry season (December–April). Lower humidity means wood stabilizes faster during curing. Avoid peak hurricane season (August–October) when contractors are often busy with storm repairs.

**My Safe Florida Home:** This program focuses on exterior hardening like roofs and windows. It does not offer rebates for interior closet installations. Use funds for the exterior, and budget separately for interior upgrades.

## How to Save Money on Closet Installation in Fort Lauderdale

1. **Keep the existing layout.** Moving walls or plumbing costs money. Stick to the existing footprint to save **$1,000–$3,000** in framing and electrical work.

2. **Choose plywood over solid wood.** Veneered plywood offers 80% of the look at 50% of the cost. It is more stable in Florida humidity than solid hardwood.

3. **Buy materials during sales.** Laminate and hardware often go on sale in January. Lock in prices before the spring rush drives labor costs up.

4. **Do the prep work yourself.** Remove old shelving and clear the room. This saves the contractor time, which can reduce labor charges by **10–15%**.

5. **Bundle multiple rooms.** If you need closets in three bedrooms, ask for a volume discount. Contractors often offer **5–10%** off for multi-room projects.

## When to Install Your Closet — Warning Signs

**Your current closet is damaged.** If doors are broken, shelves are sagging, or rods are bent, it is time to replace. In Florida, warped wood is common due to humidity swings.

**You are moving into a new home.** New homes often come with builder-grade closets that lack quality. Upgrading before you move in is easier than moving later.

**Your family is growing.** Kids need more storage for toys and clothes. A new closet system organizes clutter and reduces stress in the bedroom.

**You are selling your home.** A custom closet is a high-return investment. It signals to buyers that the home is well-maintained and ready for occupancy.

## How to Hire a Closet Contractor in Fort Lauderdale

1. **Verify their Florida license** at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a Certified or Registered Contractor (CCC or CRC) for cabinetry work.

2. **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify.

3. **Check for a Broward County local business tax receipt.** This confirms they are registered locally, not a storm chaser from out of state.

4. **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — material brand, finish type, hardware specs, labor, timeline, and warranty.

5. **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings.

6. **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, materials arrive, installation complete, final inspection passed.

7. **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, material specs, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, and cleanup plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a custom closet cost in Fort Lauderdale in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$3,500 and $15,000**. The average is around **$7,500**. Your actual cost depends on square footage, material choice, and whether electrical work is needed.

### Do I need a permit to install a closet in Broward County?

Generally no, unless you are framing new walls or moving plumbing. Standard shelving systems do not require a permit. Budget **$100–$300** only if structural changes are involved.

### How long does a closet installation take in Fort Lauderdale?

Standard systems take **1–3 days**. Custom carpentry takes **5–10 days**. Rainy season can delay delivery of materials if they arrive via truck.

### Does a new closet increase home value in Fort Lauderdale?

A new closet recoups **50–60%** of its cost at resale. The bigger value in Fort Lauderdale's market is livability — a functional closet makes the home feel larger and more organized.

### Can I install a closet myself in Florida?

You can install pre-fabricated systems yourself. But custom carpentry requires licensed trades for electrical or structural work. DIY is common for shelving but risky for structural changes.

### Does homeowners insurance cover closet installation?

Storm damage (water, wind) is typically covered if it damages the closet. Normal wear and tear is never covered. Ensure your policy covers built-in cabinetry at replacement cost.
