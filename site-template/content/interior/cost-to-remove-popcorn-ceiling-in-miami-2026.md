# How Much Does It Cost to Remove Popcorn Ceiling in Miami? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** A full popcorn ceiling removal in Miami costs between **$2,500 and $5,500** for most homes, with the average homeowner paying around **$3,800**. The biggest factors driving your price are square footage, asbestos testing requirements, and whether you need drywall repair or a new texture finish.

Miami homes often have textured ceilings installed in the 1970s and 80s. Removing them is more than just scraping paint. You must account for environmental safety and the building code requirements that govern interior renovations in Miami-Dade County.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$2,500 – $3,500** | Removal only, no repair |
| Mid-Range | **$3,500 – $4,500** | Removal + skim coat + paint |
| High-End | **$4,500 – $5,500+** | Removal + repair + new texture |

**Budget** covers scraping and cleaning the existing texture without replacing the drywall surface. It leaves a smooth but imperfect finish that usually requires heavy sanding before painting.

**Mid-range** includes the removal process plus a skim coat to smooth the drywall. This also includes one coat of primer and one coat of paint over the entire ceiling surface.

**High-end** is for homes with significant damage or those wanting a specific texture like knockdown. It includes drywall patching, skim coating, texturing, and multiple paint coats for a flawless look.

These prices reflect Miami market rates as of early 2026 and include materials, labor, dust containment, and cleanup. They do not include major structural repairs or asbestos abatement if hazardous material is found.

## What Affects the Cost in Miami

### 1. Ceiling Square Footage

Ceiling work is priced by the square foot. A typical Miami single-family home has a ceiling area between 1,200 and 2,000 sq ft. At **$2.00–$4.00 per sq ft** for removal, a 1,200 sq ft ceiling runs roughly **$2,400–$4,800** while a 2,000 sq ft ceiling pushes **$4,000–$8,000**.

### 2. Asbestos Testing and Abatement

This is the single biggest variable in older Miami homes. Any home built before 1980 likely contains asbestos in the texture. Testing costs **$250–$500** per sample. If asbestos is found, professional abatement is required, adding **$3.00–$6.00 per sq ft** to the project.

### 3. Labor Rates in Miami-Dade

Labor in Miami is among the highest in the Southeast. Skilled ceiling contractors charge **$60–$90 per hour** for specialized work. Demand spikes after hurricane seasons when water damage repairs often involve ceiling work. Weekend work may incur a **10–15%** premium.

### 4. Drywall Condition and Repairs

Popcorn texture often hides water damage or cracks. If the drywall is soft or crumbling, you must replace sections before texturing. Drywall replacement adds **$3.00–$6.00 per sq ft** for materials and installation. This is not included in standard removal quotes.

### 5. Ceiling Height and Access

Standard 8-foot ceilings are easy to reach with ladders. High vaulted ceilings or areas with 10+ foot drops require scaffolding or scissor lifts. Scaffolding rental and setup can add **$500–$1,500** to the total project cost.

### 6. Finish Type Selection

You cannot leave the ceiling bare after removal. You must choose a new finish. Smooth skim coats are standard. Textured finishes like knockdown or orange peel require additional materials and labor. Texturing adds **$0.50–$1.50 per sq ft** over a smooth finish.

## Cost by Finish Type

| Finish Type | Cost per Sq Ft | Notes |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Removal Only | **$1.50 – $2.50** | No new finish applied |
| Smooth Skim | **$2.50 – $3.50** | Paint-ready surface |
| Knockdown | **$3.00 – $4.00** | Popular modern look |
| Orange Peel | **$2.75 – $3.75** | Light texture, easy to match |
| Full Abatement | **$4.00 – $6.00** | Includes asbestos removal |
| Vaulted Ceiling | **$4.00 – $7.00** | Requires scaffolding |

**Removal only** is rare. It leaves a rough surface that is difficult to paint without visible imperfections. Most homeowners skip this option for a better long-term result.

**Smooth Skim** is the most common choice for a modern look. It involves applying a thin layer of joint compound over the entire ceiling and sanding it perfectly flat. This takes the most labor time.

**Knockdown** is a popular texture in Miami. It involves spraying compound and flattening it with a trowel. It hides minor imperfections better than smooth finishes but requires a skilled hand.

**Orange Peel** is the easiest texture to match if you keep existing walls. It is less labor-intensive than knockdown but still requires spraying equipment and masking.

**Full Abatement** applies only if asbestos is detected. It requires licensed hazardous material professionals, negative air pressure containment, and special disposal fees.

**Vaulted Ceiling** work requires special equipment to reach high areas safely. The time required for setup and teardown increases the labor cost significantly.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Asbestos Regulations:** Miami-Dade County follows strict EPA and Florida DEP rules for older homes. Any home built before 1980 must be tested before demolition or removal begins. If asbestos is found, you must hire a certified abatement contractor. They must submit a notification to the county at least 10 working days before starting work.

**Building Code:** Florida Building Code Chapter 7 covers interior finishes. While simple texture removal doesn't always need a permit, if you replace drywall or alter fire-rated assemblies, a permit is required. The Miami-Dade County Building Department requires a permit for any interior renovation affecting more than 200 sq ft of surface area.

**Permits:** Apply through the Miami-Dade County ePlan portal. Fees run **$150–$400** depending on the project scope. If asbestos is involved, you must show proof of abatement before the interior permit is issued. Turnaround time is typically 3–5 business days for standard work.

**Insurance Impact:** A new ceiling can affect your flood insurance if water damage caused the popcorn texture. If you are filing a claim for water damage, document the ceiling condition before removal. Insurance may cover the cost if the removal is part of a covered loss repair.

**Ventilation:** Miami's humidity can cause mold on new drywall if not dried properly. Contractors must use dehumidifiers during the skim coat drying process. Failure to control moisture can lead to mold growth within weeks, which is a major issue for home resale.

**My Safe Florida Home:** If you are doing major renovations, you may qualify for grants to improve home safety. While this program focuses on wind mitigation, improvements like upgrading electrical wiring during ceiling work can qualify for rebates up to **$10,000**.

**HOA Restrictions:** Many Miami neighborhoods have strict design guidelines. If your HOA requires specific paint colors or finishes, you must get approval before starting. Violating these rules can result in fines or being forced to redo the work.

## How to Save Money on Popcorn Ceiling Removal in Miami

1. **Test for asbestos first.** Don't pay for abatement if your home is post-1980. Testing costs **$250–$500** but saves thousands if you find no asbestos. Buy a kit from a local hardware store or hire a private inspector.

2. **Bundle with painting.** Contractors often give a **10–15% discount** if you combine ceiling removal with a full interior paint job. You save on setup time and dust containment costs since you are already working in the room.

3. **Schedule in dry season.** Miami's rainy season runs from May to October. Scheduling work from November to April avoids weather delays and humidity issues that slow down drying times. Contractors are more available and may offer lower rates.

4. **Keep the texture if possible.** If the texture is in good condition, consider leaving it. Removing it can cost **$3,000+**. Painting over existing popcorn texture is cheaper and adds no value to the home's marketability in most cases.

5. **Get 3–4 written quotes.** Pricing varies widely between contractors in Miami. On a **$4,000** job, the difference between the lowest and highest quote can be **$1,000**. Ensure every quote includes the same scope of work to compare apples to apples.

6. **Do the prep work yourself.** Moving furniture and covering floors is time-consuming for contractors. If you do the prep work, some contractors will reduce their labor estimate by **$200–$500**. Just ensure you don't damage anything before they start.

## When to Remove Your Popcorn Ceiling — Warning Signs

**Your ceiling has water stains.** Brown rings or bubbling paint indicate active leaks. Removing the texture exposes the damage. You must fix the roof or plumbing leak before any cosmetic work begins.

**You suspect asbestos.** If your home was built before 1980, the texture likely contains asbestos. Do not scrape it yourself. Disturbing asbestos fibers creates a health hazard that requires professional remediation.

**Your home is selling soon.** A smooth ceiling looks modern and clean to buyers. It can increase perceived value by **5–10%** compared to dated textured ceilings. However, it is not always necessary for a quick sale.

**You hate the dust.** Removal creates massive amounts of drywall dust. It settles everywhere even with containment. If you plan to stay in the home during the work, you will need to vacate for at least 2–3 days.

**You have high ceilings.** If your ceilings are over 10 feet, the removal process is dangerous. Professional scaffolding is required. Attempting this DIY can lead to serious injury and liability issues.

## How to Hire a Ceiling Contractor in Miami

1. **Verify their Florida license** at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a Certified or Registered Certified General Contractor (CGC). Interior finishers often work under a GC license.

2. **Check for asbestos certification.** If testing shows asbestos, the contractor must be state-certified for hazardous material abatement. Ask for their license number and verify it on the state portal.

3. **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify coverage is active.

4. **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — testing fees, removal, drywall repair, paint, and cleanup. Do not accept a lump sum without knowing what is included.

5. **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints about dust control and cleanup. Ceiling work is messy, and poor cleanup is a common complaint.

6. **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, removal complete, paint finish, final walkthrough.

7. **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, materials brand, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, and cleanup plan. Include a clause for asbestos discovery costs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does popcorn ceiling removal cost in Miami in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$2,500 and $5,500**. The average is around **$3,800**. Your actual cost depends on square footage, asbestos testing, and whether drywall repair is needed.

### Do I need a permit to remove popcorn ceiling in Miami-Dade?

Usually no, unless you are replacing drywall. If you are just scraping texture, a permit is not required. If you patch drywall or change fire ratings, you need a permit from the Building Department. Budget **$150–$400**.

### How long does popcorn ceiling removal take in Miami?

A standard single-family home takes **2–4 days**. This includes setup, removal, drying time, and painting. If asbestos abatement is required, the process can take **1–2 weeks** due to containment requirements.

### Is popcorn ceiling removal worth the cost in Miami?

It adds value for resale in modern neighborhoods. Buyers prefer smooth ceilings. However, if you are staying long-term, the cost of **$3,000–$5,000** may not recoup fully at sale. It is mostly for aesthetic preference.

### Can I remove popcorn ceiling myself?

Yes, but it is dangerous. If asbestos is present, DIY removal is illegal and hazardous. Even without asbestos, the dust creates health risks. Most professionals recommend hiring a pro for **$2.50–$4.00 per sq ft**.

### Does homeowners insurance cover ceiling removal?

No, cosmetic upgrades are never covered. If the removal is part of a water damage claim, the insurance may cover the repair portion. Check your policy before starting any work related to damage.
