# How Much Does It Cost to Repair Drywall in Miami? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** Professional drywall repair in Miami costs between **$350 and $1,800** for most residential projects, with the average homeowner paying around **$750**. The biggest factors driving your price are the extent of the damage, the need for moisture-resistant materials, and the humidity control required during installation.

Miami's high humidity and salt air environment mean standard drywall fails faster than in other parts of the country. This requires specific materials and techniques to prevent mold growth, which adds cost but protects your home's health.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$350 – $550** | Small patch, standard drywall |
| Mid-Range | **$550 – $1,100** | Medium patch, moisture resistant |
| High-End | **$1,100 – $1,800+** | Large repair, textured, mold treatment |

**Budget** covers minor nail holes or small cracks under 2 square feet. It uses standard white drywall and basic joint compound with a simple texture match. Labor is often a flat rate for small jobs.

**Mid-range** handles larger damage like a hole from a door frame or water damage up to 10 square feet. This includes moisture-resistant green board, proper taping, and texture matching to existing walls. It ensures the repair lasts in Miami's climate.

**High-end** covers extensive water damage, large holes, or areas requiring complex texture matching (like knockdown or orange peel). It includes mold remediation if present, priming, and painting to blend with the surrounding wall.

These prices reflect Miami market rates as of early 2026 and include materials, labor, disposal, and cleanup. They do not include structural framing repairs if the wall studs are compromised.

## What Affects the Cost in Miami

### 1. Size of Damage

Contractors charge by the square foot for labor and materials. A single 4x8 sheet costs **$15–$25** before labor. Repairing a small patch takes **1–2 hours** of labor, while replacing a whole wall takes **8–12 hours**. The bigger the hole, the more waste you generate and the more materials you need to match.

### 2. Moisture Resistance Requirements

Miami humidity demands specific materials. Standard drywall rots quickly in high humidity. You will likely need **green board** or **purple board** rated for moisture resistance. This material costs **$1.50–$2.50 per sq ft** more than standard drywall but prevents future mold issues.

### 3. Labor Rates in Miami

Drywall contractors in Miami charge **$45–$75 per hour** for skilled labor. This is higher than the national average due to the demand for hurricane-ready construction and specialized moisture control knowledge. Seasonal demand spikes after storm season (October) can push rates toward the high end.

### 4. Texture Matching

Miami homes often feature specific textures like knockdown, orange peel, or smooth skim coat. Matching an existing texture requires skill and time. Simple textures cost **$0.50–$1.00 per sq ft** to match. Complex custom textures or stucco finishes on drywall add **$2.00–$4.00 per sq ft**.

### 5. Painting and Priming

Most repairs require painting the entire wall to avoid a visible patch. Painting adds **$2.00–$4.00 per sq ft** to the project. If the wall has multiple coats of paint or a specific sheen, the prep time increases. High-quality primer is essential to block stains from water damage.

### 6. Permits and Inspections

Cosmetic repairs under 100 square feet usually do not require a permit in Miami. However, structural changes or new wall construction require a building permit from the City of Miami Building Department. Permit fees run **$150–$400** for structural work. Always check if your repair involves load-bearing walls.

## Cost by Material Type

| Material | Cost per Sq Ft | Lifespan |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Standard Drywall | **$1.00 – $1.50** | 20–30 years |
| Moisture Resistant | **$2.50 – $3.50** | 30–40 years |
| Fire Rated | **$3.50 – $4.50** | 40+ years |
| Sound Dampening | **$4.00 – $5.00** | 40+ years |

**Standard drywall** is the most common choice for living rooms and bedrooms. It is easy to work with but susceptible to water damage. Best for areas with low humidity exposure and no flood risk.

**Moisture resistant** drywall (green or purple board) is essential for bathrooms, kitchens, and coastal areas. It resists mold growth better than standard board. In Miami, this should be the minimum standard for any repair near plumbing or exterior walls.

**Fire rated** drywall (Type X) is required by code in garages and between units in multi-family buildings. It contains glass fibers to resist fire spread. It costs slightly more but ensures compliance with Miami-Dade fire codes.

**Sound dampening** drywall is thicker and denser, reducing noise transmission. Popular in condos and townhomes where wall thickness is limited. It adds significant mass to the wall but increases material costs by **50%** compared to standard board.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Florida Building Code 8th Edition (2023) governs all structural drywall work in Miami. Key requirements include proper fastener spacing (12 inches on edges, 16 inches on studs) and the use of corrosion-resistant screws. In flood zones, drywall must be elevated above Base Flood Elevation (BFE) if it is below the first floor.

**Hurricane Zone:** While drywall isn't wind-rated like windows, Miami-Dade County requires specific fastening schedules in High Velocity Hurricane Zones (HVHZ) for exterior walls. This ensures the wall assembly stays intact during extreme wind pressure. Your contractor must verify if your home is in an HVHZ overlay.

**Permits:** Apply through the City of Miami's ePermitting portal or the Miami-Dade County Building Department. Turnaround is 3–7 business days for standard residential permits. Budget **$150–$400** for structural work. Cosmetic patching under 100 sq ft typically does not require a permit.

**Insurance Impact:** Water damage from plumbing leaks is typically covered, but wind-driven rain damage depends on your policy. Filing a claim for drywall repair can impact your premiums. If the damage is due to lack of maintenance, insurance will deny the claim. Document all damage with photos before starting repairs.

**HOA:** Many Miami condo associations restrict wall colors and textures. Some require specific paint sheens or prohibit certain finishes. Get written approval from your HOA board before purchasing materials. Changing textures mid-job can lead to fines or forced removal.

**Season:** Best time to repair drywall here is November through March — dry season, lower humidity, fewer storm damage backlogs. Avoid peak hurricane season (August–October). Humidity spikes in summer slow down drying times for joint compound, potentially delaying the project by days.

**My Safe Florida Home:** If your home was built before 2008 and is homesteaded, you may qualify for a free wind inspection and matching grants up to **$10,000** toward hurricane hardening improvements. While drywall repair isn't always covered, securing sheathing during a broader retrofit might qualify.

## How to Save Money on Drywall Repair in Miami

1. **Get at least 4 written quotes.** Contractor pricing varies by **20–35%** for identical scope in Miami. On a **$1,000** job, that's **$200–$350** in potential savings. Make sure each quote itemizes materials, labor, texture matching, and painting separately.

2. **Bundle with other interior work.** Combine drywall repair with painting or flooring installation. Contractors often offer **5–10%** off labor when bundling services. This reduces mobilization costs and cleanup time.

3. **Choose standard textures over custom.** Knockdown and orange peel are easier to match than custom stucco or troweled finishes. Standard textures cost **$0.50–$1.00 per sq ft** less to replicate. Ask your contractor which texture is most common in your neighborhood.

4. **Do the prep work yourself.** If you can move furniture and cover floors, you save on labor time. Contractors charge for prep time even if you do it. Removing outlet covers and baseboards before they arrive can save **$50–$100**.

5. **Check for insurance claim eligibility.** If your drywall damage is from a covered peril like a pipe burst or wind, your insurance may cover part or all of the repair. File the claim before signing a contractor agreement. Be cautious of contractors who offer to "handle the insurance" — work with your adjuster directly.

## When to Repair Drywall — Warning Signs

**Your walls have water stains.** Brown or yellow rings on the ceiling or walls indicate active or past leaks. In Miami's humidity, these stains often hide mold behind the paint. Do not paint over water stains without fixing the source first.

**You see cracking around corners.** Cracks at the corners of windows or doors often signal settling or foundation movement. Small hairline cracks are normal, but widening cracks over time require structural assessment.

**The wall feels soft or spongy.** Press on the wall gently. If it feels soft, the drywall or studs have absorbed too much moisture. This indicates active rot or mold that needs immediate removal and replacement.

**You hear hollow sounds.** Tap the wall with a knuckle. A hollow sound usually means the drywall is detached from the studs. This can happen in older Miami homes due to humidity expansion and contraction.

**You smell mustiness.** A persistent earthy smell often means hidden mold growth behind the drywall. This is common in bathrooms and kitchens with poor ventilation. The smell persists even after cleaning surfaces.

## How to Hire a Drywall Contractor in Miami

1. **Verify their Florida license** at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a Certified General Contractor (CGC) or Registered General Contractor (CGR). Drywall is often a specialty under a general license.

2. **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify. Florida law requires this for all licensed contractors.

3. **Check for a Miami-Dade local business tax receipt.** This confirms they're registered locally, not a storm chaser from out of state. Verify the expiration date on the receipt.

4. **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — material brand, underlayment type, number of squares, tear-off, permits, labor, timeline, and warranty.

5. **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings. Search for specific keywords like "mold" or "water damage" in the reviews.

6. **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, tear-off complete, mid-project, final inspection passed.

7. **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, material specs with NOA numbers, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, permit responsibility, and cleanup plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does drywall repair cost in Miami in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$350 and $1,800**. The average is around **$750**. Your actual cost depends on damage size, material choice, and whether painting is included.

### Do I need a permit to repair drywall in Miami?

Cosmetic repairs under 100 sq ft usually do not need a permit. Structural changes or new walls require a permit from the City of Miami. Budget **$150–$400** for permit fees if applicable.

### How long does drywall repair take in Miami?

Small patches take **1–2 days**. Large wall replacements take **3–5 days**. Humidity can extend drying times for joint compound by 24–48 hours during rainy season.

### Does drywall repair increase home value in Miami?

A repaired wall improves curb appeal and prevents further damage. It recoups **70–80%** of its cost at resale by preventing mold claims during inspections. Buyers worry about hidden water damage.

### Can I repair drywall myself in Florida?

Florida's homeowner exemption allows it if you own and occupy the home. But you still need permits for structural work and must meet all code requirements. Insurance companies may refuse to cover DIY repairs if they fail inspection.

### Does homeowners insurance cover drywall repair?

Water damage from sudden leaks is typically covered minus your deductible. Normal wear and tear or lack of maintenance is never covered. Wind-driven rain coverage depends on your specific policy terms.
