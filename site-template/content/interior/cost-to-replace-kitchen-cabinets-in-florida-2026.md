# How Much Does It Cost to Replace Kitchen Cabinets in Florida? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** Replacing kitchen cabinets in Florida costs between **$5,000 and $25,000** for most projects, with the average homeowner paying around **$12,000**. The biggest factors driving your price are the cabinet material, kitchen layout complexity, and the high humidity requiring moisture-resistant construction.

Florida's extreme humidity and frequent water intrusion risks mean standard cabinets often fail faster here than in other states. You need materials that resist swelling and mold, which pushes costs up compared to national averages.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$5,000 – $9,000** | Stock laminate, DIY install |
| Mid-Range | **$9,000 – $16,000** | Semi-custom, professional install |
| High-End | **$16,000 – $25,000+** | Full custom, solid wood |

**Budget** options rely on stock cabinets from big box stores. These are usually particle board or laminate. You might save on labor by installing them yourself, but the lifespan is shorter.

**Mid-range** is what most Florida homeowners choose. You get semi-custom sizes and plywood boxes that resist humidity better. Professional installation ensures proper leveling and sealing against water.

**High-end** involves fully custom millwork or solid wood species like maple or cherry. These fit perfectly around unique Florida home layouts. Prices climb with complex features like pull-outs and soft-close hardware.

These prices reflect Florida market rates as of early 2026 and include materials, labor, removal of old units, and disposal. They do not include countertop replacement or major plumbing relocations.

## What Affects the Cost in Florida

### 1. Cabinet Size and Layout

Kitchen cabinets are priced per linear foot of run. A standard 10-foot galley kitchen runs **$5,000–$9,000** for stock units. Expanding to an island or L-shape adds **$3,000–$7,000** to the material bill. Larger kitchens in newer Florida developments often need more cabinetry to reach the square footage.

### 2. Material Choice

This is the single biggest price lever you control. Laminate is the cheapest at **$30–$60 per linear foot** installed. Plywood boxes with veneer finish run **$60–$120 per linear foot**. Solid wood like maple or oak is the premium choice at **$120–$200 per linear foot**. Moisture resistance increases with material quality.

### 3. Labor Rates in Florida

Cabinet installation labor in Florida runs **$45–$85 per hour**, roughly 15–20% above the national average. Skilled carpenters are in high demand for custom work in Miami and Tampa. After a major storm, labor costs can spike another **10–20%** due to backlog.

### 4. Removal and Disposal

Old cabinets often contain mold or pests in Florida's humid climate. Removal and disposal costs run **$500–$1,500** depending on the volume. Some landfills charge extra for construction debris. Contractors usually include this in the total quote.

### 5. Moisture Protection

Florida requires extra prep to prevent water damage. This includes sealing cabinet backs and sides with waterproofing membranes. You might also need to install new plumbing traps or venting. These upgrades add **$300–$800** to the project but protect your investment.

### 6. Permits and Inspections

Most cosmetic cabinet replacements do not require a permit in Florida. However, if you move plumbing or electrical lines, you need a building permit. Permit fees run **$150–$400** depending on the county. Check with your local building department before starting work.

| Material | Cost per Linear Foot | Lifespan |
|---------|----------------------|----------|
| Laminate | **$30 – $60** | 10–15 years |
| MDF | **$50 – $80** | 15–20 years |
| Plywood | **$80 – $120** | 20–30 years |
| Solid Wood | **$120 – $200** | 30–50 years |

**Laminate** is the budget king. It resists water well but chips easily. Best for rentals or budget-conscious homeowners in humid zones.

**MDF** offers a smooth paintable surface. It swells if it gets wet, so it needs good sealing. Mid-range option for painted finishes.

**Plywood** boxes are the standard for quality. They hold screws better and resist humidity better than particle board. This is what most contractors recommend for Florida.

**Solid wood** cabinets are beautiful and durable. They expand and contract with Florida humidity but last decades. Expect to pay **$120–$200 per linear foot**.

**Custom cabinetry** fits your exact space and needs. It includes specialized features like spice racks or waste bins. Prices range from **$150–$300 per linear foot** depending on design.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Florida Building Code Chapter 13 requires moisture-resistant materials in wet areas. Cabinet backsplash and toe kicks must be sealed to prevent water wicking. Ensure your contractor follows these standards to avoid future damage.

**Humidity Control:** Florida humidity averages **70% or higher** in summer. Cabinets in wet areas like dishwashers need ventilation gaps. Install moisture-resistant paint or finish on all exposed wood.

**Permits:** County permit processes vary widely. Miami-Dade requires strict inspections for any structural change. Orange County is faster but still requires permits for plumbing. Always verify local rules before starting.

**Insurance Impact:** Water damage claims often cover cabinet replacement if caused by a sudden event. However, wear and tear from humidity is never covered. A new roof or plumbing repair might trigger a cabinet replacement claim.

**HOA:** Many Florida communities restrict exterior or visible cabinet colors. Some require specific hardware finishes. Get HOA approval before purchasing materials to avoid costly mid-project changes.

**Season:** Best time to replace cabinets is November through April. Humidity is lower, making installation and curing easier. Summer projects risk warping wood during the curing phase.

**My Safe Florida Home:** While this program focuses on wind mitigation, the free inspection can identify water intrusion points that ruin cabinetry. Use the inspection to find leaks before installing new units.

## How to Save Money on Cabinet Replacement in Florida

1.  **Keep the existing layout.** Moving sinks or stoves costs **$1,000–$3,000** in plumbing and gas work. Stick to the current footprint to save on labor and materials.

2.  **Refinish instead of replace.** If your cabinet boxes are solid, painting them saves **50–70%** of the cost. Refinishing costs **$2,000–$4,000** versus **$10,000+** for new units.

3.  **Order stock cabinets online.** Big box stores ship stock cabinets cheaper than local showrooms. You save **$1,000–$2,000** on materials, though installation still costs the same.

4.  **Do the demo yourself.** Removing old cabinets and disposal costs **$500–$1,000**. If you have help, you can save this amount. Just be careful not to damage walls or plumbing.

5.  **Bundle with countertop work.** Installing new countertops at the same time saves on labor mobilization fees. Contractors often give **5–10%** off when bundling multiple services.

## When to Replace Your Cabinets — Warning Signs

**Your cabinets are 20+ years old.** Cabinet doors warp and hinges loosen over time. In Florida's heat, particle board joints fail faster than in cooler climates.

**Visible water damage or mold.** Swollen wood, black spots, or musty smells mean water intrusion. Mold remediation is required before new cabinets can be installed safely.

**Doors don't close properly.** Misaligned doors indicate the cabinet box has shifted. This often happens on older homes with foundation settling.

**Hardware is broken or missing.** Drawer slides that stick or doors that sag are signs of wear. Replacing just hardware costs **$200–$500** but won't fix structural issues.

## How to Hire a Cabinet Contractor in Florida

1.  **Verify their Florida license** at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a Certified or Registered Contractor license. Check for any disciplinary actions.

2.  **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum **$1 million**) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify.

3.  **Check for a local business tax receipt.** This confirms they are registered locally, not a storm chaser from out of state.

4.  **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — material brand, finish type, dimensions, labor, timeline, and warranty.

5.  **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints about moisture damage or delays.

6.  **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, delivery, install complete.

7.  **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, material specs, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, and cleanup plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a cabinet replacement cost in Florida in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$5,000 and $25,000**. The average is around **$12,000**. Your actual cost depends on layout size, material choice, and whether plumbing is moved.

### Do I need a permit to replace cabinets in Florida?

Usually no, unless you move plumbing or electrical lines. If you do, permit fees run **$150–$400**. Check with your local building department to confirm.

### How long does a cabinet replacement take in Florida?

Stock cabinets take **2–4 days** to install. Custom units take **1–3 weeks** due to shipping times. Humidity can delay curing for painted finishes.

### Does replacing cabinets increase home value in Florida?

Kitchen remodels recoup **60–70%** of their cost at resale. But the bigger value in Florida's market is avoiding mold and water damage issues during inspection.

### Can I replace my own cabinets in Florida?

Yes, as a homeowner doing your own work. But you still need permits for plumbing changes and must meet all building codes. It is not worth the risk if you lack experience.

### Does homeowners insurance cover cabinet replacement?

Water damage from sudden events like pipe bursts is typically covered. Normal wear and tear from humidity is never covered. File the claim before starting demolition.
