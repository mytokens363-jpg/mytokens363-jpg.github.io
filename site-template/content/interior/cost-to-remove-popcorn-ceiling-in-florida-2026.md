# How Much Does It Cost to Remove Popcorn Ceiling in Florida? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** Removing a popcorn ceiling in Florida costs between **$2.00 and $5.00 per square foot** for most homes. The average homeowner pays around **$3.50 per square foot** for removal and refinishing. The biggest factors driving your price are the age of your home, asbestos testing requirements, and the finish type you choose for the new surface.

Florida has a massive housing stock built between 1950 and 1980, which means a huge percentage of homes contain asbestos in their textured ceilings. This adds a layer of complexity you won't find in many other states. You need to test before you scrape.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$2.00 – $3.00** | Test, scrape, smooth finish |
| Mid-Range | **$3.00 – $4.50** | Test, scrape, knockdown texture |
| High-End | **$4.50 – $6.00+** | Asbestos abatement, new drywall |

**Budget** covers homes built after 1980 with no asbestos. It includes wet scraping, patching, and a standard smooth paint finish.

**Mid-range** is the most common choice. It assumes asbestos testing is negative but adds a new texture like orange peel or knockdown for durability.

**High-end** applies to homes with confirmed asbestos. This requires certified abatement crews, containment barriers, and specialized disposal. It often involves removing the old drywall entirely.

These prices reflect Florida market rates as of early 2026. They include labor, materials, testing fees, and disposal. They do not include structural repairs if damage is found under the texture.

## What Affects the Cost in Florida

### 1. Asbestos Testing

Homes built before 1980 in Florida are assumed to contain asbestos until proven otherwise. Testing costs **$100–$300** per sample. You need at least three samples from different rooms. If asbestos is found, removal jumps to **$8.00–$15.00 per sq ft**. Never skip this step.

### 2. Ceiling Height

Standard Florida homes have 8-foot ceilings. Luxury homes and ranches often have 10-foot ceilings. Higher ceilings require ladders or scaffolding, adding **$0.50–$1.00 per sq ft** to labor. Stairwells and vaulted ceilings cost even more due to access difficulty.

### 3. New Finish Type

Removing the texture is only half the job. You must finish the surface. Smooth finishes are cheapest at **$1.00–$2.00 per sq ft**. Knockdown textures hide imperfections better and cost **$1.50–$2.50 per sq ft**. Orange peel is a middle ground for **$1.25–$2.25 per sq ft**.

### 4. Lighting and Electrical

Popcorn ceilings often hide wiring. Relocating a light fixture requires pulling new wire through the attic or walls. This adds **$150–$300 per fixture**. If you need to move a fan or add recessed lighting, costs climb significantly.

### 5. Furniture and Flooring Protection

Professional crews protect floors with rosin paper or heavy plastic. They cover furniture or move it to the center of the room. This prep work takes time but prevents dust damage. Expect **$200–$500** included in the labor quote for this service.

### 6. Labor Rates in Florida

Interior labor in Florida runs **$45–$75 per hour**. Coastal markets like Miami and Tampa pay more than inland areas like Ocala or Gainesville. High demand during the dry season (January–May) can push rates to the higher end.

## Cost by Finish Type

| Finish Type | Cost per Sq Ft | Lifespan |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Smooth | **$1.00 – $2.00** | 10–15 years |
| Orange Peel | **$1.25 – $2.25** | 10–15 years |
| Knockdown | **$1.50 – $2.50** | 15–20 years |
| Skip Trowel | **$2.00 – $3.00** | 15–20 years |
| Stucco | **$2.50 – $4.00** | 20+ years |

**Smooth** finishes are the quickest to apply but show every imperfection. They require significant sanding after taping. Best for modern, minimalist homes.

**Orange peel** adds a slight texture that hides joint lines well. It is the most common replacement for popcorn in Florida homes. Easy to touch up later.

**Knockdown** flattens the orange peel slightly for a softer look. It is popular in mid-century modern renovations. Slightly more labor-intensive than orange peel.

**Skip trowel** is a hand-applied texture that looks rustic. It requires a skilled hand to apply consistently. Best for high-end custom homes.

**Stucco** is a cement-based texture that mimics exterior finishes. It is the most durable option but also the most expensive. Rarely used indoors unless specific aesthetic is required.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Asbestos Regulations:** Florida follows federal EPA rules for asbestos. Any contractor handling asbestos must be certified by the Florida Department of Environmental Protection. They must follow specific containment procedures including negative air pressure machines and HEPA filtration. Never hire a general handyman for asbestos work.

**Humidity and Drying:** Florida's humidity slows down drying times for joint compound and paint. During the rainy season (May–October), drying can take **30–50% longer** than in dry climates. Contractors schedule extra time between coats to prevent bubbling or cracking.

**Permits:** Most interior cosmetic work does not require a permit in Florida. However, if you move electrical fixtures or change wall configurations, you need a permit. Check with your county building department. Miami-Dade County has stricter inspection requirements than rural counties.

**Insurance:** Mold is a major concern in Florida. If popcorn texture hid water damage, removing it exposes the problem. Insurance rarely covers cosmetic removal. If you find mold during the process, remediation is an extra cost. Document everything before starting.

**HVAC Dust Control:** Dust from scraping is fine and spreads easily through ducts. Professional crews seal off vents with plastic and tape. They run air scrubbers during the work. This protects your AC system from clogging. It is a critical step for health.

**My Safe Florida Home:** This program focuses on wind mitigation, not ceilings. However, if you open up a ceiling for insulation, you may qualify for rebates. Insulation upgrades often happen during major renovations. Check eligibility for tax credits on energy efficiency.

**Seasonal Timing:** The best time to remove ceilings is winter. Humidity is lower, and drying is faster. Summer heat and humidity can cause paint to blister if not applied correctly. Schedule work between November and March for the best results.

## How to Save Money on Popcorn Ceiling Removal

1. **Test before you hire.** If you know your home is post-1980, you can skip the testing fee. Many older homes in Florida are post-1980. Confirm the build date with your county records first.

2. **Keep existing fixtures.** Moving lights adds labor and material costs. If you keep the same light locations, the contractor can just patch the holes. This saves **$150–$300 per fixture**.

3. **Do it room by room.** Removing ceilings in one house at a time keeps costs down. You can rent equipment and manage the disruption better. It also spreads the cost over time.

4. **Choose orange peel over smooth.** Smooth finishes show more flaws and require more sanding. Orange peel hides imperfections better and is slightly cheaper to apply. It is the standard for a reason.

5. **Bundle with painting.** If you are painting the walls anyway, ask for a bundle discount. Contractors often reduce the per-square-foot price for combined jobs. You save on setup and cleanup time.

6. **Check for rebates.** If you add insulation while opening the ceiling, you may qualify for My Safe Florida Home rebates. This offsets the cost of the renovation. Check the program website for current limits.

7. **Get multiple quotes.** Contractor pricing varies by **20–30%** in Florida. On a **$5,000** job, that is **$1,000** in savings. Ensure each quote includes testing, disposal, and finishing.

## When to Remove Your Ceiling — Warning Signs

**Your ceiling has water stains.** Popcorn texture absorbs moisture. If you see brown spots or bubbling, the texture is compromised. Removing it reveals the damage underneath. Fix the leak first before starting.

**You have health concerns.** If you have respiratory issues, asbestos dust is a risk. Even non-asbestos dust can trigger allergies. Removing the texture reduces the surface area for dust accumulation.

**You want to update the look.** Popcorn ceilings are dated. Removing them modernizes the home instantly. It increases resale value and makes rooms feel larger and brighter.

**You need to access wiring.** If you plan to upgrade your electrical system, the ceiling is the easiest access point. Removing texture saves time compared to cutting holes in drywall.

**You have a leak history.** If the roof leaks often, the ceiling texture is likely damaged. Smooth finishes show water damage better than popcorn. This helps you catch future leaks early.

## How to Hire a Ceiling Removal Contractor in Florida

1. **Verify their Florida license** at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a Certified or Registered Contractor license. General contractors can do this work.

2. **Check for asbestos certification.** If your home is pre-1980, the crew must have specific asbestos training. Ask for their certification number before signing.

3. **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million). Call the insurer directly to verify active status.

4. **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns. Include testing, removal, finishing, disposal, and cleanup. Never accept a verbal quote.

5. **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints about dust control or unfinished work. Florida contractors are heavily regulated.

6. **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, removal complete, finish complete.

7. **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, timeline, payment schedule, warranty terms, and asbestos handling plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does popcorn ceiling removal cost in Florida in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$2.00 and $5.00 per square foot**. The average is around **$3.50 per square foot**. Your actual cost depends on asbestos testing, ceiling height, and finish type.

### Do I need a permit to remove popcorn ceiling in Florida?

Usually no. Cosmetic interior work does not require a permit. However, moving electrical fixtures requires a permit. Check with your county building department to be sure.

### How long does popcorn ceiling removal take in Florida?

A standard 2,000 sq ft home takes **2–4 days**. Drying time adds **1–2 days** depending on humidity. Rainy season can extend the timeline by a few extra days.

### Does removing popcorn ceiling increase home value in Florida?

Yes. Modern finishes recoup **60–70%** of their cost at resale. The bigger value is in marketability. Buyers prefer smooth or lightly textured ceilings over popcorn.

### Can I remove popcorn ceiling myself in Florida?

Yes, if you are not dealing with asbestos. DIY costs are lower but labor-intensive. You need a sprayer, scraper, and proper protection. Dust control is difficult without experience.

### Does homeowners insurance cover popcorn ceiling removal?

No. Insurance covers sudden damage like storms or leaks. Cosmetic updates are homeowner expenses. If asbestos is found, remediation is rarely covered by standard policies.
