# How Much Does It Cost to Remodel a Kitchen in Fort Lauderdale? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** A full kitchen remodel in Fort Lauderdale costs between **$25,000 and $120,000** for most homeowners, with the average project landing around **$55,000**. The biggest factors driving your price are the scope of layout changes, cabinet quality, and Broward County's strict permit and inspection requirements.

Fort Lauderdale homes often feature open-concept designs that require structural adjustments or plumbing relocations. This adds complexity compared to a standard cosmetic update. Your budget needs to account for licensed labor rates that are 15% higher than the national average.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$25,000 – $35,000** | Cosmetic, refacing, new counters |
| Mid-Range | **$35,000 – $75,000** | Full cabinets, appliances, layout |
| High-End | **$75,000 – $120,000+** | Luxury finishes, structural changes |

**Budget** projects usually keep the existing layout. You get new cabinet refacing, laminate or quartz counters, and a fresh coat of paint. Plumbing and electrical stay in the same spots.

**Mid-range** is the standard for most homeowners upgrading for resale. This includes new custom or semi-custom cabinets, stone countertops, tile flooring, and new major appliances. It often involves moving the sink or stove slightly.

**High-end** covers luxury materials like imported stone, smart appliances, and custom millwork. It frequently requires moving walls, updating HVAC for ventilation, and installing high-end lighting systems.

These prices reflect Fort Lauderdale market rates as of early 2026. They include materials, licensed labor, permits, disposal, and cleanup. They do not include structural repairs found behind walls during demolition.

## What Affects the Cost in Fort Lauderdale

### 1. Kitchen Size

Kitchen remodels are priced per square foot of cabinetry and square footage of the room. A standard 10x12 kitchen is **120 sq ft**. A small galley might be **80 sq ft**. A large gourmet kitchen can exceed **250 sq ft**. Larger spaces require more cabinetry and more countertop linear footage.

### 2. Cabinet Quality

Cabinets are the single biggest expense, often consuming **30–50%** of your total budget. Stock cabinets from big-box stores run **$100–$200 per linear foot**. Semi-custom options range from **$250–$500 per linear foot**. Full custom cabinetry from local shops starts at **$600+ per linear foot**.

### 3. Countertop Materials

Granite is the standard choice at **$50–$80 per sq ft** installed. Quartz is the modern favorite at **$60–$100 per sq ft**. Marble and exotic stone push **$100–$150 per sq ft**. Laminate is the budget option at **$20–$40 per sq ft** but looks dated quickly.

### 4. Labor Rates in Fort Lauderdale

Licensed General Contractors in Broward County charge **$75–$125 per hour** for project management. Specialized trades like plumbers and electricians run **$80–$100 per hour**. These rates are higher than the national average due to insurance costs and demand during hurricane season.

### 5. Permits and Inspections

Broward County requires permits for almost any kitchen remodel involving plumbing, electrical, or structural changes. Permit fees typically run **$500–$2,500** depending on the project value. The process includes plan review and inspections for plumbing and electrical rough-ins before walls close up.

### 6. Layout Changes

Moving a sink or gas line requires re-plumbing through concrete slab foundations common in South Florida. This is expensive and time-consuming. Moving a wall requires a structural engineer and a new beam. Structural changes can add **$5,000–$15,000** to the project immediately.

## Cost by Material Type

| Material | Cost Range | Notes |
|---------|------------|-------|
| Stock Cabinets | **$100 – $200/lf** | Builder grade, standard sizes |
| Semi-Custom | **$250 – $500/lf** | More finish options, better hardware |
| Custom Cabinets | **$600+ /lf** | Made to fit, high-end wood |
| Laminate Counters | **$20 – $40/sq ft** | Budget, easy to clean |
| Quartz Counters | **$60 – $100/sq ft** | Durable, non-porous |
| Granite Counters | **$50 – $80/sq ft** | Natural stone, varied patterns |

**Stock cabinets** are pre-manufactured and available immediately. They are functional but lack the customization of higher tiers. Best for rental properties or tight budgets.

**Semi-custom cabinets** offer more door styles and finish options. They fit standard rough openings but allow for some customization in height and depth. This is the sweet spot for most Fort Lauderdale homes.

**Custom cabinets** are built on-site or in a local shop to exact specifications. You get the exact height you need and specialized storage solutions. Expect to pay a premium for this level of detail.

**Laminate counters** are the most affordable surface. They resist stains well but can chip or burn. They do not add much resale value compared to stone.

**Quartz counters** are engineered stone made from resin and crushed quartz. They are non-porous and resist staining better than granite. They are becoming the standard for modern kitchens.

**Granite counters** are natural stone slabs cut from quarries. Each piece is unique. They require sealing to prevent staining. They add significant value to the home.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Florida Building Code 8th Edition (2023) governs all plumbing and electrical work in Fort Lauderdale. Kitchen plumbing must use specific pipe materials resistant to corrosion from local water quality. Electrical circuits for kitchens require GFCI protection near water sources.

**Permits:** Apply through Broward County's ePlan online portal or at the Fort Lauderdale Building Department. Turnaround is 5–10 business days for a standard residential permit. Budget **$500–$2,500** for fees. Two inspections required: rough-in and final.

**Insurance Impact:** Water damage is a major concern in Florida. Upgrading to water-resistant flooring or installing leak detection systems can lower your premium. A new kitchen increases home value, which may raise your insured value.

**HOA:** Many Fort Lauderdale neighborhoods have deed restrictions on exterior colors or noise levels during work hours. Some communities restrict the type of appliances allowed or require specific cabinet finishes. Get written HOA approval before starting.

**Season:** Best time to remodel is December through April — dry season, lower humidity, fewer storm delays. Avoid peak hurricane season (August–October). Rainy season (May–October) brings daily thunderstorms that can flood basements or delay material delivery.

**Flood Zones:** Fort Lauderdale has many coastal and flood zones. If your home is in a Special Flood Hazard Area, you may need to raise electrical outlets above flood levels. This adds cost to the electrical rough-in phase.

**My Safe Florida Home:** If your home was built before 2008 and is homesteaded, you may qualify for a free wind inspection and matching grants. While this focuses on wind, it can apply to general home hardening upgrades that improve overall safety.

## How to Save Money on Kitchen Remodeling in Fort Lauderdale

1. **Keep the plumbing layout.** Moving the sink and stove requires breaking the concrete slab. This is labor-intensive and expensive. Keep fixtures in their current locations to save **$3,000–$8,000**.

2. **Reface existing cabinets.** If the boxes are solid, new doors and drawer fronts cost half as much as full replacement. This updates the look without the demolition cost.

3. **Choose quartz over granite.** Quartz is often cheaper than high-end granite and doesn't require sealing. It offers a similar look with better durability for daily cooking.

4. **Do the demo yourself.** If you are handy, tear out the old cabinets and flooring yourself. Just make sure to hire a pro for electrical and plumbing connections. This can save **$2,000–$4,000** in labor.

5. **Get 4 written quotes.** Contractor pricing varies by **20–35%** for identical scope in Fort Lauderdale. On a **$50,000** job, that's **$10,000–$15,000** in potential savings. Compare line items carefully.

## When to Remodel Your Kitchen — Warning Signs

**Your cabinets are 15+ years old.** Wood cabinets in South Florida degrade faster due to humidity. Doors may warp, hinges may sag, and finishes may peel. This is a sign of wear that affects usability.

**Your appliances are inefficient.** Old refrigerators and dishwashers use more electricity and water. Upgrading to Energy Star models saves money on utility bills immediately.

**The layout is inefficient.** If you can't open the dishwasher while the oven is open, you need a layout change. An island or better workflow adds functionality to your daily life.

**There are water stains or mold.** Check under the sink and around the dishwasher. Moisture damage in cabinets indicates a leak that could affect the subfloor.

**You are planning to sell.** A modern kitchen is the number one feature buyers look for. A dated kitchen can delay a sale or force you to lower your asking price.

## How to Hire a Kitchen Contractor in Fort Lauderdale

1. **Verify their Florida license** at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a Certified General Contractor (CGC) or a Certified Plumbing Contractor (CPC) if they do plumbing.

2. **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify.

3. **Check for a Broward County local business tax receipt.** This confirms they are registered locally, not a storm chaser from out of state.

4. **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — material brand, demolition, permits, labor, timeline, and warranty.

5. **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings. Pay attention to comments about cleanup and timeline adherence.

6. **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, rough-in complete, final inspection passed.

7. **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, material specs with model numbers, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, permit responsibility, and cleanup plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a kitchen remodel cost in Fort Lauderdale in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$25,000 and $120,000**. The average is around **$55,000**. Your actual cost depends on layout changes, material quality, and whether structural work is needed.

### Do I need a permit to remodel my kitchen in Broward County?

Yes, if you move plumbing, electrical, or walls. Apply through Broward County's ePlan portal. Budget **$500–$2,500**. Never let a contractor skip the permit — it can void your insurance and create title issues.

### How long does a kitchen remodel take in Fort Lauderdale?

Standard remodels take **6–10 weeks**. Custom cabinets add **4–6 weeks** for fabrication. Rainy season can add **1–2 weeks** of weather delays if materials are stored outside.

### Does a kitchen remodel increase home value in Fort Lauderdale?

A kitchen remodel recoups **60–75%** of its cost at resale. But the bigger value in Fort Lauderdale's market is marketability — a modern kitchen sells faster than a dated one in competitive neighborhoods.

### Can I remodel my own kitchen in Florida?

You can do cosmetic work yourself if you own and occupy the home. But you cannot do plumbing or electrical work without a license. You still need permits and inspections for structural changes.

### Does homeowners insurance cover kitchen remodels?

Normal wear and tear is not covered. However, sudden damage like a burst pipe flooding the kitchen is typically covered minus your deductible. Flood damage requires separate flood insurance.
