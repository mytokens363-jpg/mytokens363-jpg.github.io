# How Much Does It Cost to Remove Popcorn Ceiling in Fort Lauderdale? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** Removing a popcorn ceiling in Fort Lauderdale costs between **$3,000 and $8,000** for most single-family homes, with the average homeowner paying around **$5,500**. The biggest factors driving your price are square footage, whether asbestos testing is required, and the final finish texture you choose.

Fort Lauderdale homes built before 1980 almost always contain asbestos in the texture. This changes the entire scope of work from simple scraping to regulated abatement. Proper containment and disposal add cost, but they protect your family from dangerous airborne fibers.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$3,000 – $4,500** | Removal + Smooth Finish, No Asbestos |
| Mid-Range | **$4,500 – $6,500** | Removal + Texture + Paint, Pre-1980 Home |
| High-End | **$6,500 – $8,000+** | Asbestos Abatement + High-End Finish |

**Budget** covers straightforward removal in homes built after 1980 with no asbestos. It includes scraping the texture, patching minor holes, and applying a smooth skim coat. Labor is the main driver here.

**Mid-range** is the most common scenario for Fort Lauderdale. This includes testing, removal, and applying a new texture like knockdown or orange peel. It also includes primer and two coats of paint for the ceiling.

**High-end** involves certified asbestos abatement for older homes. This requires negative air pressure containment, specialized PPE, and disposal fees. The finish is usually high-gloss smooth or custom texture work.

These prices reflect Fort Lauderdale market rates as of early 2026 and include labor, materials, disposal, and cleanup. They do not include structural repairs if you find water damage or rot during the removal process.

## What Affects the Cost in Fort Lauderdale

### 1. Home Age and Asbestos

This is the single biggest cost variable. Homes built before 1980 in Broward County often contain asbestos in the texture. Testing costs **$150–$300** per sample. If positive, you need abatement, which adds **$2.00–$5.00 per sq ft** to the project. Post-1980 homes skip this step entirely.

### 2. Ceiling Square Footage

Contractors price by the square foot. A typical Fort Lauderdale home has **1,200 to 2,000 sq ft** of ceiling space. At **$2.50–$4.00 per sq ft**, a 1,200 sq ft room runs roughly **$3,000–$4,800**. Larger open floor plans or multi-story homes push the total over **$6,000**.

### 3. Final Finish Choice

You don't have to leave a ceiling perfectly smooth. Smooth finish is the cheapest at **$1.00–$2.00 per sq ft**. Knockdown texture costs **$1.50–$2.50 per sq ft** because it requires more skill. Orange peel is slightly cheaper but still requires spray equipment.

### 4. Repair and Patching

Removing texture damages the drywall underneath. You will likely need joint compound to fill screw holes and cracks. Extensive damage requires a full skim coat, adding **$1.00–$3.00 per sq ft**. This is common in older Fort Lauderdale homes where drywall has settled.

### 5. Furniture and Flooring

Contractors must cover all furniture and floors to prevent dust damage. This adds time to the job. If you have fragile antiques or expensive rugs, you may need to move them yourself. Some contractors charge **$200–$500** extra for extensive floor protection.

### 6. Accessibility

High ceilings or vaulted ceilings require scaffolding or scissor lifts. This equipment rental adds **$300–$800** to the project. Standard 8-foot ceilings are standard work. Anything above 10 feet requires special rigging and safety protocols.

## Cost by Finish Type

| Finish Type | Cost per Sq Ft | Lifespan |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Smooth Finish | **$1.00 – $2.00** | 20+ years |
| Knockdown | **$1.50 – $2.50** | 15–20 years |
| Orange Peel | **$1.20 – $2.00** | 15–20 years |
| Popcorn (Re-texture) | **$2.00 – $3.00** | 10–15 years |

**Smooth finish** is the most popular choice after removal. It creates a modern, clean look that matches current Florida design trends. It requires skilled taping and sanding to hide imperfections.

**Knockdown texture** flattens the sprayed texture with a knife. It hides minor drywall imperfections better than smooth finish. This is a good middle ground between modern and traditional styles.

**Orange peel** is a light spray texture that mimics the look of citrus skin. It is faster to apply than knockdown and covers more flaws. It is often found in new construction homes in Fort Lauderdale.

**Popcorn (Re-texture)** is rare nowadays. Some homeowners choose to put the old texture back on if the cost of removal is too high. It is generally not recommended due to dust and potential asbestos concerns.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Asbestos Regulations:** Broward County enforces strict asbestos handling rules. If testing is positive, you must hire a licensed asbestos contractor, not a general painter. Improper handling can lead to fines and health risks. Always test before removing.

**Humidity and Drying:** South Florida humidity slows down drying times for joint compound. In summer, drying can take **2–3 times longer** than in winter. This extends the project timeline by several days. Contractors may charge more for rush jobs in humid months.

**Mold Risk:** Removing texture can expose mold hidden in the drywall. Fort Lauderdale's coastal air brings high moisture. If you find black mold, remediation costs **$2,000–$5,000** extra. Inspect the substrate before applying new paint.

**Building Permits:** Broward County typically does not require a permit for cosmetic interior work like texture removal. However, if you are adding or moving electrical fixtures, a permit is required. Check with the City of Fort Lauderdale Building Department.

**Insurance Implications:** A new ceiling can help with mold claims. Some Florida insurers require proof of remediation before covering water damage. Document the work with photos and receipts. Keep the asbestos test report for your records.

**HOA Restrictions:** Many Fort Lauderdale communities have design guidelines. Some HOAs restrict ceiling textures or require specific paint colors. Get written approval from your HOA before starting work to avoid fines.

**Seasonal Timing:** Best time to remove popcorn ceilings is during the dry season, December through April. Lower humidity means faster drying and less risk of mold growth. Avoid rainy season when moisture levels spike.

## How to Save Money on Popcorn Ceiling Removal in Fort Lauderdale

1. **Get at least 4 written quotes.** Contractor pricing varies by **20–30%** for identical scope in Fort Lauderdale. On a **$5,000** job, that's **$1,000–$1,500** in potential savings. Make sure each quote itemizes testing, removal, and finish separately.

2. **Test for asbestos yourself first.** A DIY test kit costs **$50–$100**. If negative, you save the **$300** professional testing fee. If positive, you know abatement is needed upfront.

3. **Move furniture yourself.** Contractors charge for labor to move and protect items. Clear the room completely before they arrive. This can save **$100–$300** on labor time.

4. **Choose knockdown over smooth.** Smooth finish requires more sanding labor to get perfectly flat. Knockdown texture hides minor imperfections, reducing sanding time and cost.

5. **Bundle with painting.** If you need the walls painted anyway, do the ceiling at the same time. Painters often discount the ceiling prep if it's part of a larger interior paint job.

6. **Avoid peak season.** Contractors are busiest in winter. Scheduling in late spring or early summer might offer better rates due to lower demand.

## When to Remove Your Popcorn Ceiling — Warning Signs

**Your home was built before 1980.** Asbestos is present in the vast majority of homes built during this era. Removing it without testing violates health safety standards. Get a lab test before any work begins.

**You see water stains or bubbling.** These signs indicate roof leaks or plumbing issues. Removing the ceiling without fixing the leak will result in new damage. Fix the source first, then repair the ceiling.

**You notice cracks in the drywall.** Large cracks suggest structural settling. Patching the texture won't fix the underlying wall movement. Consult a structural engineer if cracks are wider than 1/8 inch.

**Your insurance company requests a claim.** If a claim is filed for mold or water damage, a full ceiling replacement might be covered. Check your policy limits before paying out of pocket.

**The texture is peeling off.** Loose texture creates dust and debris hazards. It also indicates the adhesive is failing. This requires full removal rather than spot repairs.

## How to Hire a Ceiling Contractor in Fort Lauderdale

1. **Verify their Florida license** at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a Certified General Contractor (CGC) or Certified Pool/Spa Contractor (CPC) for major work. Painters need a registered certificate.

2. **Confirm asbestos certification.** If your home is pre-1980, the contractor must be licensed by the Florida Department of Environmental Protection. Ask for their EPA ID number.

3. **Check for a Broward County local business tax receipt.** This confirms they are registered locally. Do not hire storm chasers who cannot provide local proof of business.

4. **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns. Scope must include testing, containment, removal, patching, texture application, and painting.

5. **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for comments about dust control and cleanup. Poor cleanup is a common complaint with texture removal.

6. **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, removal complete, texture applied, final cleanup.

7. **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, materials, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, and asbestos disposal plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does popcorn ceiling removal cost in Fort Lauderdale in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$3,000 and $8,000**. The average is around **$5,500**. Your actual cost depends on square footage, asbestos testing, and finish type.

### Do I need a permit to remove popcorn ceiling in Broward County?

Generally, no. Cosmetic interior work does not require a building permit. However, if you move electrical fixtures, you need a permit. Check with the City of Fort Lauderdale Building Department.

### How long does popcorn ceiling removal take in Fort Lauderdale?

A standard 1,500 sq ft home takes **2–4 days**. Asbestos abatement adds **3–5 days** for containment setup and testing. Humidity can extend drying times by **1–2 days**.

### Does removing popcorn ceiling increase home value in Fort Lauderdale?

Yes, it modernizes the home. Updated homes sell faster in the Fort Lauderdale market. Buyers prefer smooth ceilings over dated 1970s textures.

### Can I remove popcorn ceiling myself in Florida?

You can, but it is dangerous if asbestos is present. Wet scraping reduces dust but creates mold risks in humid Florida. Professional abatement is recommended for pre-1980 homes.

### Does homeowners insurance cover ceiling removal?

No. Cosmetic updates are not covered. However, if removal is required due to covered water damage or mold, it may be included in that claim.
