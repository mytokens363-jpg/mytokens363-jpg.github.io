# How Much Does It Cost to Replace Kitchen Cabinets in Tampa? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** Full kitchen cabinet replacement in Tampa costs between **$3,000 and $15,000** for most standard homes, with the average homeowner paying around **$8,500**. The biggest factors driving your price are cabinet material, kitchen square footage, and whether you are moving plumbing or electrical lines.

Tampa homes often feature open floor plans that require specific cabinet configurations to maintain flow. High humidity levels also dictate material choices that standard mainland builders might overlook.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$3,000 – $5,500** | Stock cabinets, basic laminate |
| Mid-Range | **$5,500 – $10,000** | Semi-custom, plywood boxes |
| High-End | **$10,000 – $15,000+** | Custom wood, soft-close hardware |

**Budget** gets you stock cabinets from big box retailers installed by general handymen. It works for rental properties or quick flips where resale value isn't the primary goal.

**Mid-range** is what most Tampa homeowners choose — semi-custom cabinets with soft-close hinges, plywood boxes instead of particleboard, and better finishes. Covers standard 10x10 to 12x12 kitchens.

**High-end** covers custom-built cabinetry from local shops. Includes solid wood, specialized storage solutions, and premium hardware like Blum or Hettich systems.

These prices reflect Tampa market rates as of early 2026 and include materials, labor, removal, and disposal. They do not include new countertops or flooring unless specified in the contract.

## What Affects the Cost in Tampa

### 1. Kitchen Size

Kitchen costs are typically calculated by linear feet or by the box. A standard 10x10 kitchen uses about **20 to 30 linear feet** of cabinet run. At **$150–$300 per linear foot** for mid-range, a basic setup runs roughly **$3,000–$6,000**. Larger kitchens with islands require more material and labor, pushing the total higher.

### 2. Material Choice

This is the single biggest price lever you control. Stock laminate cabinets are the cheapest option at **$100–$150 per linear foot** installed. Solid wood or painted plywood boxes jump to **$300–$500 per linear foot**. The price difference is substantial because wood requires more machining and finishing time.

### 3. Labor Rates in Tampa

Cabinet installation labor in Hillsborough County runs **$60–$90 per hour**, roughly 10% above the national average. Demand stays steady year-round from new construction and remodels. During peak spring season, rates can spike another 10% due to high demand.

### 4. Permits and Inspections

Hillsborough County usually does not require a permit for cosmetic cabinet replacement. However, if you move plumbing or electrical lines, you need a permit. Permit fees run **$50–$150** depending on the scope. Always check with the county before starting to avoid fines.

### 5. Layout Changes

Moving walls or plumbing is expensive. Adding an island or changing the sink location adds **$1,000–$3,000** to the project. Plumbing moves require a licensed plumber and may need a permit. Electrical moves for under-cabinet lighting also add cost.

### 6. Hardware and Features

Soft-close hinges and drawer slides are standard in mid-range but add to the budget. Pull-out shelves and spice racks add convenience but cost extra. Upgrading to full-extension slides adds **$200–$500** to the total project cost.

## Cost by Material Type

| Material | Cost per Linear Ft | Lifespan |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Laminate | **$100 – $150** | 10–15 years |
| MDF | **$150 – $250** | 15–20 years |
| Plywood | **$250 – $400** | 20–30 years |
| Oak | **$300 – $500** | 25–40 years |
| Maple | **$350 – $550** | 30–40 years |
| Cherry | **$450 – $700** | 40+ years |

**Laminate** is the budget option. It resists moisture well but chips easily. Best for rentals or tight budgets where durability isn't the main concern.

**MDF** hits the sweet spot for painted finishes. It doesn't swell like wood but can swell if water leaks. Painted MDF cabinets are popular in Tampa modern kitchens.

**Plywood** is the industry standard for durability. Boxes are built from Baltic birch or hardwood plywood. It holds screws better and resists humidity better than particleboard.

**Oak** is the classic choice. It takes stain well and is very strong. However, the grain pattern is visible even after staining. Expect to pay **$300–$500 per linear foot**.

**Maple** offers a smooth grain that paints beautifully. It is harder than oak and resists dents better. This is the most common wood for painted cabinets in Tampa.

**Cherry** is premium — 40+ year lifespan with a rich color that darkens over time. It is softer than maple so it dents easily. Expect to pay **$450–$700 per linear foot**.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Florida Building Code 8th Edition (2023) governs all residential construction in Tampa. Cabinet anchoring is required to resist overturning during earthquakes, though rare in Florida. More importantly, moisture resistance is critical due to high humidity levels year-round.

**Humidity:** Tampa has a humid subtropical climate. Wood cabinets can swell or warp if not properly sealed. Use moisture-resistant plywood for box construction rather than particleboard. Look for products rated for high-humidity environments.

**Permits:** Hillsborough County Building Division handles permits. Cosmetic work usually requires none. If you move gas lines for a cooktop, you need a gas permit. Budget **$50–$150** for any necessary permits. Check with your contractor before starting.

**Insurance:** Standard homeowners insurance covers sudden water damage from leaks. However, it does not cover gradual wear or humidity damage. If a pipe bursts and ruins cabinets, filing a claim is necessary. Flood insurance is separate and only covers flood zone properties.

**HOA:** Many Tampa communities restrict cabinet colors and styles in deed-restricted neighborhoods. Some HOAs require specific finishes or prohibit open shelving. Get written HOA approval before signing a contract. Changing materials mid-job is extremely expensive.

**Season:** Best time to replace cabinets is late fall through early spring. Summer humidity makes paint curing difficult. Rainy season (June–November) can delay delivery of materials. Schedule installation during drier months for faster completion.

**My Safe Florida Home:** This program offers grants for wind mitigation and flood hardening. While cabinets aren't a direct grant item, elevating cabinets in flood zones can qualify for flood mitigation rebates. Check eligibility if you are in a flood zone.

## How to Save Money on Cabinet Replacement in Tampa

1.  **Get at least 4 written quotes.** Contractor pricing varies by 20–35% for identical scope in Tampa. On a **$8,000** job, that's **$1,600–$2,800** in potential savings. Make sure each quote itemizes materials, labor, removal, and disposal separately.

2.  **Keep the existing layout.** Moving plumbing and electrical is the biggest cost driver. Keeping the sink and stove in the same spot saves **$1,000–$3,000**. It also avoids the need for permits and inspections.

3.  **Choose stock cabinets over custom.** Stock cabinets from big box stores cost **$100–$150 per linear foot**. Custom cabinets run **$400–$700 per linear foot**. The savings can be **$5,000+** on a full kitchen.

4.  **Refinish instead of replace.** If the cabinet boxes are solid, refacing or painting them costs **$1,500–$3,000**. This extends the life of the cabinetry without the full replacement cost. Best for cabinets in good structural condition.

5.  **Do the demo yourself.** Removing old cabinets and disposing of debris saves labor costs. You can save **$500–$1,000** by doing the prep work. Ensure the contractor allows this before starting the job.

## When to Replace Your Cabinets — Warning Signs

**Your cabinets are 15–20 years old.** Kitchen cabinets in Florida degrade faster due to humidity. A 20-year-old cabinet in Tampa has taken more abuse than a 20-year-old cabinet in a dry climate. Check for warping or loose joints.

**Your insurance company is pressuring you.** Water damage claims from plumbing leaks can force a replacement. If the cabinets are damaged by a pipe burst, you may need to file a claim. Don't wait until the damage spreads.

**Visible water damage or mold.** Check inside drawers and under the sink for swelling. Mold growth in humid environments is a health hazard. Multiple swollen areas usually mean the whole system has failed.

**Hardware failure.** If drawers don't close or doors sag, the structure is compromised. Soft-close mechanisms wear out over time. Replacing hardware is easy, but replacing the box is costly.

## How to Hire a Cabinet Contractor in Tampa

1.  **Verify their Florida license** at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a Certified General Contractor (CGC) or Certified Building Contractor (CBC) license.

2.  **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify.

3.  **Check for a Hillsborough County local business tax receipt.** This confirms they are registered locally, not a storm chaser from out of state.

4.  **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — material brand, box construction, number of linear feet, removal, permits, labor, timeline, and warranty.

5.  **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings.

6.  **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, delivery, mid-project, final inspection passed.

7.  **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, material specs with NOA numbers, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, permit responsibility, and cleanup plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does cabinet replacement cost in Tampa in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between **$3,000 and $15,000**. The average is around **$8,500**. Your actual cost depends on kitchen size, material choice, layout changes, and whether plumbing moves are needed.

### Do I need a permit to replace cabinets in Hillsborough County?

No, usually not for cosmetic work. If you move plumbing or electrical, you need a permit. Budget **$50–$150**. Check with the county before starting to avoid fines.

### How long does cabinet replacement take in Tampa?

Stock cabinets take **3–5 days** for installation. Custom cabinets take **2–3 weeks**. Rainy season can add 2–5 days of weather delays for paint curing or delivery.

### Does new cabinets increase home value in Tampa?

New cabinets recoup **60–70%** of their cost at resale. But the bigger value in Tampa's market is functionality — a home with modern storage is dramatically easier to sell because buyers can actually use the space.

### Can I replace my own cabinets in Florida?

Florida's homeowner exemption allows it if you own and occupy the home. But you still need permits for plumbing/electrical and must meet all FBC code requirements. Insurance companies may refuse to cover a homeowner-installed kitchen. For most people, it's not worth the risk.

### Does homeowners insurance cover cabinet replacement?

Storm damage (wind, hail, fallen trees) is typically covered minus your deductible — often **2% of insured value** for hurricane claims in Florida. Normal wear and aging is never covered. Water leaks are covered if sudden and accidental.
