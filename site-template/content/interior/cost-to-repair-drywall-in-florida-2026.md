# How Much Does It Cost to Repair Drywall in Florida? (2026 Guide)

**Quick Answer:** Most homeowners pay between **$400 and $8,000** for drywall repairs in Florida. Minor patch jobs range from **$400–$1,200** while major water damage restoration runs **$3,500–$8,000**. Your final price depends on damage size, cause, texture matching, and regional labor rates across the state.

Florida’s humidity and hurricane season make drywall repairs unique compared to the rest of the country. Water intrusion is common, and mold growth can happen fast if the substrate isn’t treated properly. You also need to consider texture matching to keep resale value high in communities with strict finish standards.

## Average Cost Breakdown

| Cost Level | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Budget | **$400 – $1,200** | Small patches, minor holes |
| Mid-Range | **$1,200 – $3,500** | Room sections, texture matching |
| High-End | **$3,500 – $8,000+** | Water damage, mold remediation |

**Budget** covers small repairs like nail holes, doorknob impacts, or minor cracks. This involves taping, mudding, sanding, and painting a single section. It assumes the underlying structure is sound and dry.

**Mid-range** handles larger sections where a wall needs cutting out and patching. This often involves matching orange peel or knockdown textures common in Florida homes. It includes sanding, priming, and painting the entire wall for seamless blending.

**High-end** applies to significant water damage or mold issues. It requires removing wet insulation, drying the cavity, treating for mold, and installing new moisture-resistant drywall. This is the most expensive option due to the remediation steps involved.

These prices reflect Florida market rates as of early 2026. They include materials, labor, disposal, and standard paint. They do not include structural framing repairs if wood rot is found behind the walls.

## What Affects the Cost in Florida

### 1. Damage Size

Drywall is priced by the square foot or by the patch size. A standard patch under 4 square feet usually costs **$300–$600**. A section repair covering 50 to 100 square feet ranges from **$1,000–$2,500**. Whole room replacements run **$3,500–$8,000** depending on ceiling height and access.

### 2. Cause of Damage

Water damage costs more than impact damage. Water requires drying time, mold testing, and often moisture-resistant green board or cement board. Impact damage from furniture or sports just needs patching. Water repairs can add **$1,500–$3,000** for remediation steps before the drywall even goes up.

### 3. Material Choice

Standard drywall costs **$0.60–$0.90 per sq ft**. Mold-resistant green board costs **$0.90–$1.20 per sq ft**. Cement board — required in wet areas like bathrooms — costs **$1.50–$2.50 per sq ft**. In South Florida, many builders use cement board in bathrooms and moisture-resistant drywall in bedrooms.

### 4. Texture Matching

Florida homes use specific textures like orange peel or knockdown. Mismatched texture lowers home value. Matching existing texture costs **$15–$30 per hour** in labor. New construction textures are easier to match than older homes with unique sanding techniques.

### 5. Labor Rates in Florida

Labor varies significantly by region. Miami-Dade labor runs **$65–$95 per hour** due to high demand and cost of living. Jacksonville and rural areas run **$45–$65 per hour**. Orlando and Tampa sit in the middle at **$55–$80 per hour**.

### 6. Permits and Inspections

Most cosmetic drywall repairs do not require a permit. However, if you remove structural framing or move plumbing/electrical behind the wall, you need a permit. Permit fees run **$100–$300** in most Florida counties. Always check with your local building department before cutting into load-bearing walls.

## Cost by Scope of Work

| Scope | Cost Range | Details |
|-------|------------|---------|
| Minor Patch | **$300 – $600** | Nail holes, small cracks |
| Section Repair | **$800 – $2,500** | 4x8 ft section, texture match |
| Full Room | **$2,500 – $5,000** | 10x10 room, paint included |
| Water Damage | **$3,500 – $8,000+** | Mold remediation, replacement |

**Minor Patch** work is quick. A contractor comes out, fills holes, sands, and paints. This is the most common repair for families with kids or pets. It usually takes less than half a day to complete.

**Section Repair** involves cutting out a damaged portion of the wall and installing a new piece. You need to blend the new drywall with the existing surface using joint compound. This is common after moving large furniture or after plumbing leaks behind the wall.

**Full Room** repairs happen when multiple walls are damaged or when you want to repaint the entire space. It includes moving furniture, protecting floors, and painting all walls. This is often bundled with other interior upgrades.

**Water Damage** is the most complex job. It involves drying the cavity, treating for mold, replacing insulation, and installing new moisture-resistant materials. You cannot just patch wet drywall; it will grow mold again. This requires specialized contractors.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

**Building Codes:** Florida Building Code 8th Edition governs all interior work. It requires fire-rated drywall in garages and between units in condos. In wet areas like showers, cement board is mandatory behind tile. Standard drywall will fail quickly in these zones.

**Humidity and Mold:** Florida’s high humidity makes mold a constant threat. Drywall installed in bathrooms or near pools must be mold-resistant. If you see black spots, the drywall is likely compromised. Remediation requires sealing the area and using antimicrobial paint.

**Insurance Claims:** Most homeowners insurance covers sudden water damage or wind damage. However, you must file a claim before starting repairs. Insurers often require an adjuster to inspect the damage first. Normal wear and tear is never covered.

**Permits:** Check with your county. Miami-Dade requires permits for any electrical or plumbing changes behind the wall. Palm Beach and Broward vary by municipality. Always get a permit if you are unsure. Unpermitted work can cause issues during a home sale.

**HVHZ:** While drywall itself is not HVHZ rated, homes in the High Velocity Hurricane Zone require specific sealing. Gaps around windows and doors must be sealed to prevent water intrusion. This often involves repairing drywall after storm damage to ensure a tight envelope.

**Season:** Hurricane season runs June through November. Rain delays can slow down repairs. The dry season from December to April is best for painting and finishing. Contractors are busier during storm season, so scheduling is harder.

**My Safe Florida Home:** If your home needs structural framing repairs due to wind damage, you may qualify for rebates. The program offers grants up to **$10,000** for home hardening. Drywall repair itself is not covered, but framing upgrades are.

## How to Save Money on Drywall Repair in Florida

1.  **Get 3–4 written quotes.** Contractor pricing varies by 20–30% in Florida. On a **$2,000** job, that’s **$400–$600** in savings. Compare materials, timeline, and cleanup plans in each quote.

2.  **Schedule in dry season.** December through April is slower for contractors. You can negotiate better rates. Rain delays are rare in winter. Spring and summer bring rain storms that stop painting and drying.

3.  **Fix the source first.** If water damage is the cause, fix the leak before repairing the wall. Otherwise, you will pay twice. A plumber might charge **$150–$300**, but that saves thousands on wall replacement.

4.  **Do the prep work yourself.** Move furniture and clear the area before the contractor arrives. This saves labor hours. Some contractors offer a **5–10% discount** if you handle the prep.

5.  **Check insurance eligibility.** If a storm caused the damage, file a claim. Insurance may cover the full cost minus your deductible. The average hurricane deductible in Florida is **2% of insured value**.

## When to Repair Drywall — Warning Signs

**Water stains appear on ceilings.** Brown rings or yellowing paint indicate a leak. If you touch it and it feels soft, the drywall is compromised. Do not paint over it; it will show through again.

**Visible mold growth.** Black or green spots mean moisture is present. In Florida, mold spreads fast. You need to remove the affected drywall immediately. Standard cleaning products will not kill mold in porous material.

**Cracks around windows and doors.** Structural settling can cause cracks. If they are wider than **1/8 inch**, they may indicate foundation issues. A contractor should inspect the framing behind the wall.

**Musty odors.** A damp smell often means hidden moisture. Even if you don’t see damage, the wall could be wet inside. Use a moisture meter to check the surface before painting.

## How to Hire a Drywall Contractor in Florida

1.  **Verify their Florida license** at [MyFloridaLicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com). You need a Certified Contractor (CCC) or Registered Contractor (RC). Check for any disciplinary actions.

2.  **Confirm active insurance.** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing general liability (minimum $1 million) and workers comp. Call the insurer directly to verify.

3.  **Check for local business tax receipt.** This confirms they are registered in your county. Out-of-state storm chasers often lack this.

4.  **Get 3–4 written estimates** with line-item breakdowns — materials, labor, disposal, timeline, and warranty. Make sure texture matching is included.

5.  **Check reviews on Google and BBB.** Look for patterns in complaints, not just star ratings.

6.  **Never pay more than 10% upfront.** Florida Statute 489.126 restricts contractor deposits. Payment should tie to milestones: deposit, materials on site, final inspection.

7.  **Get everything in writing.** Contract must include: scope, material specs, dates, payment schedule, warranty terms, and cleanup plan.

### How long does drywall repair take in Florida?

Small patches take **4–8 hours**. Room repairs take **1–2 days**. Water damage remediation takes **3–7 days** for drying and curing. Rainy season can add 2–3 days to drying times.

### Does drywall repair increase home value in Florida?

Yes, if it is done properly. Mismatched textures or water stains lower value. Proper repairs restore curb appeal and prevent mold. A home with clean walls sells faster in Florida’s competitive market.

### Can I repair drywall myself in Florida?

You can patch small holes yourself. However, water damage and mold require professionals. DIY repairs on mold can spread spores. Insurance may deny claims if they find unpermitted or improper work.

### Does homeowners insurance cover drywall repair?

Sudden damage like water leaks or wind is typically covered. Wear and tear is not covered. The average hurricane deductible in Florida is **2% of insured value**. File a claim before starting repairs.
